More job cuts looming at GM

By Katie Merx
Bloomberg News / August 4, 2009

SOUTHFIELD, Mich. - General Motors Co. may have to cut more US hourly jobs after an offer of buyouts and early retirements fell about 7,500 workers short of the reorganized automaker’s target.

The possibility of layoffs was disclosed yesterday by Sherrie Childers Arb, a spokeswoman, in an interview after GM announced that more than 6,000 United Auto Workers members, or 11 percent of the hourly workforce, left the company Saturday.

GM’s latest voluntary exits pushed the total of US hourly workers leaving through buyouts and retirement offers to about 66,000 since 2006.

The biggest domestic automaker is shrinking its workforce to match reductions, including the shutdown of 14 US plants and three warehouses by the end of 2011.

“It’s not surprising they didn’t reach their goal,’’ said Dennis Virag, president of Automotive Consulting Group Inc. in Ann Arbor, Mich. With US unemployment at 9.5 percent in June, “workers are more reluctant to accept a buyout because the prospects for other employment are more challenging.’’

GM aims to eliminate 13,500 hourly positions in 2009, trimming that payroll to about 40,500 jobs, said Tom Wilkinson, a spokesman.

Detroit-based GM began the year with about 61,000 US hourly jobs and cut that total to about 54,000 at the end of April with buyouts and early retirements.

Any layoffs probably wouldn’t total 7,500, Childers Arb said.

Some employees will probably leave on their own or retire rather than relocate once GM shuffles work between its facilities, dropping jobs in some locations while keeping others, she said.

GM hasn’t said where the job cuts will take place.

Hourly workers who took the buyout and retirement offers are receiving cash payments of $20,000 to $115,000 as well as $25,000 vehicle vouchers.
