With federal grant, Salem State to expand its nursing lab

By Kathy McCabe
March 22, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

Salem State College will use $224,000 in new federal grant money to add a second simulated nursing laboratory, to improve education for nursing students, and provide training for registered nurses and other healthcare workers.

"This will give us much more resources to support training in the region's workforce," said Corey Cronin, a college spokesman.

The college still must develop a plan with local healthcare providers to determine how to address the training needs, Cronin said. The grant money, requested for the college by US Representative John F. Tierney, a Salem Democrat, is not part of the federal stimulus plan.

The so-called SimLab simulates patient conditions for student nurses. A robotics mannequin, which is programmed to give the symptoms of a heart attack or other ailment, is used to create clinical scenarios for nursing students. The first lab was installed at the college in 2007.

"It's real-life scenarios," Cronin said. "It really does mirror what happens in a hospital."

The grant money will not be enough to cover the total cost of the laboratory. The college will have to raise money for the balance, Cronin said.

In a statement, Salem State president Patricia Maguire Meservey said the new lab should aid workforce development in a critical industry. "Healthcare continues to be a growth area," she said. "This grant significantly expands our ability to provide meaningful educational experiences without straining the resources of regional hospitals."

Newburyport bank has record year
The Institution for Savings in Newburyport had record earnings in 2008, netting $7 million in profit and increasing assets 13 percent, the bank reported at its recent annual meeting at Ipswich Country Club.

Total assets grew to $983.1 million, the highest in history for the 189-year-old community bank. And despite a weak housing market, the majority of the bank's growth was in residential mortgages, which increased 23 percent, compared with 2007, the bank said.

Total deposits increased 18 percent, to $759.1 million. The growth, spread among the bank's five branches, is due mostly to better interest rates and new customers, the bank reported.

The Institution For Savings merged in 2007 with Ipswich Co-operative Bank, boosting its profile in Essex County.

As of June, 2008, the Institution had a 4.6 percent deposit share, according to the most recent data filed with the Federal Deposit Insurance Commission. The bank ranked seventh in deposits county-wide, behind Bank of America, TD Banknorth, Eastern Bank of Lynn, SalemFive Bank, Sovereign Bank, and Danversbank.

New head at North Shore Medical
Dr. Raymond Smith of Marblehead is the new president of the medical staff at North Shore Medical Center in Salem. Smith, an anesthesiologist, will serve as a liaison between the 650 doctors and administrators and trustees. He will also sit on the trustees during his two-year term. He succeeds Dr. Mark Girard. The center operates facilities in Salem, Lynn, and Peabody.

Smith, who lives in Marblehead, has worked at North Shore for more than 20 years. He previously chaired the center's Institutional Review Board and received the Physician Excellence Award in 2000.

He is a graduate of the University of Miami School of Medicine, and has a doctorate in pharmacology from Oxford University in England.
