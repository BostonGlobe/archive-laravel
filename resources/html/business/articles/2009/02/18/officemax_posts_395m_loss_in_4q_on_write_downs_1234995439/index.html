OfficeMax posts $395M loss in 4Q on write-downs

By Ashley M. Heher
AP Retail Writer / February 18, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

CHICAGO—Office supply chain OfficeMax Inc. said Wednesday it lost $395.2 million in the fourth quarter -- far more than Wall Street expected -- as sales fell and it accounted for a $429 million charge and other write-downs.

"We sell discretionary items in the office supply industry, and we're a victim of the economy because of that," said Chief Executive Sam Duncan. "As the economy goes, we go, meaning the office supply industry. As long as the economy continues to go on a down slide, then we're going to see the effect of that."

Shares initially tumbled, then recovered to close up 3 cents at $4.20.

For the three months ending, Dec. 27, the company lost $5.21 per share. That compares with a profit of $71.5 million, or 92 cents per share, during the period a year earlier.

Excluding one-time costs, the company earned $1.9 million, or 2 cents per share, during the quarter on revenue of $1.88 billion, down from $2.2 billion a year earlier.

The $429 million charge accounts for lower values of OfficeMax's goodwill, trade names and stores. Reviews of those items are required when a company's share price stays low for a long time, its market capitalization drops or it is operating in a weak economic environment.

Analysts surveyed by Thomson Reuters, who typically exclude one-time costs, had predicted a profit of 15 cents per share on revenue of $1.94 billion for the quarter.

Making matters worse for the Naperville-based retailer, Duncan said 2009 sales would decline compared with 2008 and are on track for a drop in the first quarter bigger than the 14 percent drop seen in the fourth quarter.

Duncan told investors during a conference call that the company is making "tough decisions": suspending pay increases and employee retirement contributions and cutting other spending where possible.

Sales in the company's contract segment slid more than 18 percent, to $953.9 million, while retail sales fell nearly 10 percent to $929.2 million. And same-store sales, an important industry metric that compares sales in stores open at least a year, plunged 13.6 percent during the period as consumers and small-businesses scaled back on spending.

Analysts said that despite the poor results, OfficeMax continues to have adequate liquidity, with net debt of $183.6 million.

"Business is very weak," Deutsche Bank analyst Mike Baker told investors in a research note. "The balance sheet was stable, which is the saving grace."

For the full year, the company lost $1.66 billion, or $21.90 per share, compared to a profit of $207.4 million, or $2.66 per share, in 2007. Revenue fell 9 percent to $8.3 million in 2008.

Excluding charges, the company's full-year adjusted profit was $100 million, or $1.30 per share.

------

AP Retail Writer Vinnee Tong in New York contributed to this report.
