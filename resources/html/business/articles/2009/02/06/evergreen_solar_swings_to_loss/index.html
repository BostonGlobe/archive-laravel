Evergreen Solar swings to loss

February 6, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

YESTERDAY
Close$2.22
Change+$0.14
52-WEEK
High$12.64
Low$1.89

Evergreen Solar Inc., a maker of thin polysilicon solar laminates, reported a bigger-than-expected fourth-quarter loss on a charge to write down the value of a closed factory and higher start-up costs at another.

The loss was $52.1 million, or 32 cents a share, compared with profit of $788,000, or 1 cent, a year earlier, the Marlborough company said.

Evergreen was expected to lose 24 cents, the average of nine analyst estimates compiled by Bloomberg. Sales doubled to $44.2 million from $22.2 million.

Evergreen shuttered its Marlborough plant and took a charge of $23.1 million to write down its value as the recession slowed new orders and reduced prices for solar power modules. The company is expanding capacity at a Devens plant that will produce up to 40 megawatts per quarter by yearend.

"Evergreen has been experiencing some cost overruns in their start-up associated with the Devens facilities," said Vijay Singh, an analyst at Janco Partners Inc. in Greenwood Village, Colo., who has a "market perform" rating on the shares and owns none. He estimates start-up costs this year of $10 million.

Charges hurt Millipore profit

Change-$0.34
52-WEEK
High$76.92
Low$43.36

Millipore Corp., a maker of membranes for sterilizing and filtering antibiotics and beverages, said its fourth-quarter profit fell 26 percent on lower revenue and a series of charges.

The Billerica company earned $33.3 million, or 60 cents per share, compared with net income of $45.1 million, or 81 cents per share, in the year-ago period. Sales fell 2 percent to $396.8 million from $405.2 million.

Excluding charges, earnings were 95 cents per share.

Analysts polled by Thomson Reuters expected profit of 90 cents per share on revenue of $401.4 million.

Millipore said its bioscience division helped offset a decline in revenue from its bioprocess division.

For the full year, earnings were $145.8 million, or $2.62 per share, up 6.8 percent from profit of $136.5 million, or $2.48 per share, in 2007. Sales rose 4.6 percent to $1.6 billion from $1.53 billion.

Profit rises 15% at Skyworks Solutions

Close$5.02
Change+$0.11
52-WEEK
High$11.24
Low$3.57

Chip maker Skyworks Solutions Inc. said its fiscal first-quarter profit climbed 15 percent, boosted by faster product launches and strong smart phone demand.

For the three months ended Jan. 2, the Woburn company earned $22 million, or 13 cents per share, up from $19.1 million, or 12 cents per share, in the same period a year earlier.

Excluding items, earnings were 17 cents per share in the latest quarter.

Revenue was nearly flat at $210.2 million compared with $210.5 million.

Analysts, on average, were expecting a profit of 15 cents per share on sales of $211.2 million, according to a poll by Thomson Reuters.

The latest quarter's results were boosted by a gain of about $2 million on the early retirement of convertible debt.
