Group 1 posts 4Q loss on sales drop

February 19, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

HOUSTON—Auto dealership chain Group 1 Automotive Inc. said Thursday it posted a $44.5 million fourth-quarter loss, as sales plunged as a result of the steep industrywide drop in demand for new vehicles.

Group 1's loss amounted to $1.96 per share and came after a profit of $5.5 million, or 24 cents per share, in the same quarter of 2007. The prior-year quarter's results included a charge of $859,000, or 4 cents per share, from discontinued operations.

The recent quarter's results included asset impairment charges of $67.2 million, or $2.96 per share, while the 2007 quarter included charges of $10.2 million, or 46 cents per share.

The 2008 quarter also included a gain of $20.9 million, or 92 cents per share, related to debt repurchases.

Excluding those items, the company posted a fourth-quarter 2008 profit from continuing operations of 8 cents per share.

Total revenue dropped 24 percent to $1.13 billion from $1.5 billion, as same-store sales dropped 28 percent.

Analysts surveyed by Thomson Reuters expected a profit of 5 cents per share, excluding one-time items, on $1.2 billion in revenue.

New vehicle retail sales dropped 30 percent to $655.2 million, while used vehicle retail sales fell 16 percent to $225.5 million and used vehicle wholesale sales plunged 44 percent to $39.9 million.

Parts and service revenue rose 2.5 percent to $178.7 million.

For the full year 2008, Group 1 posted a loss of $31.5 million, or $1.39 per share, compared with a profit of $68 million, or $2.90 per share, in 2007. Revenue fell to $5.65 billion from $6.16 billion the year before.

The company said it does not expect to acquire any new franchises this year and said it was not issuing a 2009 guidance because of the continued uncertainty surrounding the automotive industry.
