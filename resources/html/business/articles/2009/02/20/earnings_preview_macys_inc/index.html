Earnings Preview-Macy's Inc.

By Anne D'Innocenzio
AP Retail Writer / February 20, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

NEW YORK—Macy's Inc. reports earnings for the fourth quarter on Tuesday before the market opens. The following is a summary of key developments and analyst opinion related to the period.

OVERVIEW: Department stores such as Macy's Inc. are facing big challenges as shoppers -- worried about their shrinking retirement funds and job security amid massive layoffs -- are in full retreat and focusing on basics like food.

The Cincinnati-based chain announced this month that it will eliminate 7,000 jobs, almost 4 percent of its work force, and cut capital spending, reduce its contributions to its employees' retirement funds and slash its dividend to preserve cash. Macy's also announced the national rollout of a plan to localize merchandising to specific markets, which it began in some regions last year.

The moves follow Macy's announcement in January -- on the heels of the worst holiday season in decades -- that it would close 11 stores, affecting 960 employees.

BY THE NUMBERS: After reporting a smaller-than-expected decline in same-store sales in January, Macy's boosted its earnings forecast for the fourth quarter, which ended in January. Same-store sales are sales at stores opened at least a year and are considered a key indicator of a retailer's health.

Macy's now expects its fourth-quarter earnings between $1 and $1.02 per share, excluding one-time costs associated with consolidations of regional divisions announced in February 2008 and the store closings announced last month. Its previous forecast was for earnings of 90 cents to $1 per share. For the full year, Macy's predicts earnings of $1.20 to $1.22 per share, excluding one-time items.

Analysts surveyed by Thomson Reuters project earnings of $1.01 for the fourth quarter and $1.22 for the year that ended in January. The Wall Street estimates exclude one-time charges.

ANALYST TAKE:Robert Drbul, an analysts at Barclays Capital, wrote in a report that, while he is optimistic about the localization initiatives, aimed to boost same-store sales and eliminate costs, he believes it will take time for the company to "begin to realize material benefits from these consolidation and alignment efforts."

Drbul noted that Macy's large size positions it "well to navigate through the difficult retail environment."

WHAT'S AHEAD: Analysts will be looking for more details about which specific merchandise categories are doing well and which areas are not. They will also want to know more details about inventory levels and any updates on Macy's localization efforts.

Macy's anticipates it will earn between 40 cents and 55 cents per share, excluding one-time restructuring costs, for the year that ends next January. It predicts its same-stores sales will fall between 6 percent and 8 percent for the year that ends in January 2010.

For the fiscal year that ends next January, Thomson Reuters expects 53 cents per share.

STOCK PERFORMANCE: Shares fell 23 percent during the quarter and have dropped nearly 70 percent in the past 52 weeks and closed at $7.66 on Thursday. Over the past 52 weeks they have traded from $27.08 to $5.07 per share.
