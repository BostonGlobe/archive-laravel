Earnings roundup

January 28, 2009

  • Print|

Waters cuts costs in fourth quarter

Change+$0.98
52-WEEK
High$71.00
Low$32.21

Waters Corp. said its fourth-quarter profit remained nearly flat, as the analytical instrument company cut expenses to offset lower revenue.

Earnings for the quarter edged up less than 1 percent to $99.4 million, or $1.01 per share, from $98.9 million, or 98 cents per share, in the same quarter last year. The Milford company had 2.6 percent fewer shares outstanding in the latest quarter.

Excluding one-time costs such as adjustments for litigation and restructuring, the company earned $1.07 per share. Wall Street expected a more modest 96 cents per share, according to analysts polled by Thomson Reuters. Such estimates typically exclude one-time items.

Revenue slipped 4 percent to $418.3 million from $437 million, citing the effects of foreign currency exchange rates.

The company managed to scale back expenses during the quarter, and benefited from a lower tax rate, helping to push net income higher.

For the full year, earnings rose 20 percent to $322.5 million, or $3.25 per share, from $268.1 million, or 2.67 per share, in 2007. Revenue increased 7 percent to $1.58 billion. On an adjusted basis, the company said it earned $3.30 per share for the year. (AP)

Profit declines 45% at EMC

Data storage company EMC Corp. said its fourth-quarter profit fell 45 percent as one-time restructuring expenses weighed on its results. Excluding charges, the Hopkinton company's results beat Wall Street estimates, but EMC said it would not issue any financial guidance due to the uncertain economy.

EMC said its earnings for the quarter fell to $288 million, or 14 cents per share, from $525.7 million, or 24 cents per share, in the same quarter last year.

Revenue rose 5 percent to $4.02 billion from $3.83 billion a year ago as businesses continued to buy its typically recession-proof data storage products.

EMC, which is laying off 2,400 people, said it earned 32 cents per share excluding restructuring and other one-time charges.

Analysts polled by Thomson Reuters expected earnings of 23 cents per share on $4 billion in revenue. The earnings estimates typically exclude one-time items. (AP)

Display ad sales slow at Yahoo

52-WEEK
High$30.25
Low$8.94

Yahoo Inc., owner of the second most popular US search engine, reported a third-quarter loss after shrinking demand for online ads led to the company's first sales decline since 2001.

The third-quarter loss was $303.4 million, or 22 cents a share, compared with a profit of $205.7 million, or 15 cents, a year earlier. Excluding fees passed on to partner sites, sales were $1.38 billion. Analysts had estimated revenue of $1.37 billion on average, according to a Bloomberg survey.

The company has lost Internet-search customers to Google Inc., and sales are slowing for so-called display advertising, such as banner ads.

Yahoo depends more heavily on display ads than search advertising. Google handled 63.5 percent of US searches in December, while Yahoo had 20.5 percent, according to ComScore Inc.

The display market should grow about 5 percent this year, compared with 10 percent growth for search ads, according to Susquehanna International Group. Last year, search ads grew 25 percent, while the display market climbed 18 percent. (Bloomberg)

VistaPrint Ltd., an online printing services company, said its fiscal second-quarter profit jumped 66 percent as customers and order volume rose.

Earnings for the quarter ended Dec. 31 rose to $18.5 million, or 42 cents per share, from $11.2 million, or 24 cents per share, a year earlier.

Excluding stock options expenses, profit totaled $23.5 million, or 53 cents per share. Analysts surveyed by Thomson Reuters forecast earnings of 39 cents per share, excluding stock options expenses.

Revenue jumped 32 percent to $138.9 million from $105 million in the prior year.

The company said it added 1.5 million customers in the quarter, and averaged more than 43,000 orders per day - a 43 percent improvement from the prior year. However, the average value per order of custom-printed business cards and other items fell 5 percent, VistaPrint said. (AP)

Delta posts $340m loss

Change-$2.00
52-WEEK
High$18.99
Low$4.00

Delta Air Lines Inc., the world's largest carrier, fell the most in four months in New York trading after posting a fourth-quarter loss that exceeded analysts' estimates.

Excluding one-time costs for fuel hedges and stock grants related to buying Northwest Airlines, the loss was $340 million, or 50 cents a share, Delta said. Revenue was $6.71 billion, which also trailed some projections.

Delta's contracts to purchase jet fuel in advance became a drag on results after market prices plunged 63 percent in 2008's second half, leaving the airline locked in at higher rates. For 2009, Delta plans to cut available seats by as much as 8 percent to weather a drop in travel from the recession.

Delta's net loss was $1.44 billion, or $2.11 a share. That compared with a year-earlier loss of $70 million, or 18 cents, when Atlanta-based Delta was a standalone company.

Ray Neidl, a Calyon Securities analyst in New York, said the deficit was 38 cents a share after subtracting costs for purchase accounting related to the Northwest merger, wider than his 25-cent estimate. (Bloomberg)
