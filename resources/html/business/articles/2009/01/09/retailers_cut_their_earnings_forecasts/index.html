Retailers cut their earnings forecasts

Wal-Mart, Macy's actions may signal a painful 2009

Bloomberg News / January 9, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

NEW YORK -- Wal-Mart Stores Inc., Macy's Inc., and Gap Inc. slashed earnings forecasts after the worst holiday shopping season in 40 years, and the retail landscape may not improve heading into 2009.

Wal-Mart, the world's biggest retailer, yesterday said fourth-quarter earnings will be at most 94 cents a share, down from a November projection of as much as $1.07. Sales at stores open at least a year rose 1.7 percent last month, missing the 2.7 percent average of analysts' estimates compiled by Retail Metrics Inc.

To attract customers amid rising unemployment and tightening credit, some US retailers cut prices in December by up to 70 percent. That threatens to erode fourth-quarter profit margins.

US December same-store sales dropped 1.7 percent, the International Council of Shopping Centers reported. The New York trade group said sales declined 2.2 percent in the last two months of the year, the biggest such drop since it started tracking the data in 1969.

Natick's BJ's Wholesale Club Inc. said same-store sales grew 1.6 percent in December as lower gasoline sales weighed on results. Excluding gas sales, same-store sales grew 5.9 percent. Prepared foods, produce, televisions, and other electronics were among the strongest-selling categories.

TJX Cos. of Framingham said its same-store sales fell 5 percent in December as the stronger US dollar hurt international sales, but results were better than expected. Analysts polled by Thomson Reuters had expected the operator of T.J. Maxx and Marshalls stores to report a larger decline of 7.9 percent.

Retail Metrics said overall comparable store sales dropped 0.9 percent last month, less than it had estimated, as department stores used discounts to lure more shoppers.

The Swampscott, Mass., researcher said its index hasn’t posted three consecutive monthly declines since it started collecting the data in 2000.

The US government also said yesterday the number of Americans collecting unemployment benefits reached a 26-year high of 4.6 million. President-elect Barack Obama warned that without immediate government action, family incomes will drop and the unemployment rate could reach "double digits."

Wal-Mart said apparel sales were weak in the United States, Canada, and Japan. The stock, which rose 18 percent last year, declined $4.16 to $51.38 on the New York Stock Exchange.

Sales at San Francisco-based Gap fell 14 percent in December. The largest US clothing retailer cut its full-year profit forecast to a maximum of $1.30 a share from a previous high of $1.35 after December merchandise margins narrowed.

Macy's cut its fourth-quarter profit forecast to as little as 90 cents a share from a previous minimum of $1.10. It also said it would close 11 of its 859 stores. The shares slipped 10 cents to $11.21. The second-largest US department store plans a sale tomorrow with discounts as deep as 75 percent.

There were some bright spots. J.C. Penney Co. same-store sales fell 8.1 percent, less than it and analysts had estimated. Kohl’s Corp.'s dropped 1.4 percent, helped by last-minute shopping and "strong post-Christmas business," it said. Analysts had anticipated a 5.9 percent decline.
