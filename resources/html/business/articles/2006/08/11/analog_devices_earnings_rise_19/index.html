Analog Devices earnings rise 19%

August 11, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $31.90
Change -$0.35
52-WEEK
High $41.48
Low $29.89

Analog Devices Inc., whose semiconductors are used in products from DVD players to cars, said third-quarter profit rose 19 percent.

Net income rose to $144.7 million, or 39 cents a share, from $121.4 million, or 32 cents, a year earlier, the Norwood company said. Sales gained 14 percent to $663.7 million in the period ended July 29. Excluding one-time items, earnings were 43 cents a share.

Analysts expected earnings of 42 cents a share and sales of $680 million.

The company predicted fourth-quarter profit of 42 to 43 cents a share, excluding costs of 9 cents for stock-based compensation, restructuring, and acquisitions. Analysts had projected sales of $706.3 million and profit of 48 cents. (Bloomberg)

Close $46.42
Change +$1.02
52-WEEK
High $58.26

First Marblehead Corp. said fourth-quarter profit soared a better-than-expected 65 percent, as the education-loan processing firm topped Wall Street projections.

Quarterly profit rose to $70.8 million, or $1.12 per share, from $43 million, or 65 cents per share, in the year-ago period.

Boston-based First Marblehead said the strong results came from an increase in service revenue, boosted by stronger securitizations of private loans and better yields. Revenue rose 24 percent to $148.8 million.

Analysts forecast earnings of 84 cents per share on revenue of $141.2 million. (AP)

Immunogen posts increased losses

Close $3.06
Change +$0.11
52-WEEK
High $7.46
Low $2.82

Cancer drug developer Immunogen Inc. said its fiscal fourth-quarter loss widened as research and development costs doubled.

The Cambridge company lost $6.6 million, or 16 cents per share, compared with a loss of $2.7 million, or 7 cents per share, a year ago. Revenue jumped 21.5 percent to $5.7 million from $4.7 million.

The revenue increase was outweighed by a 48.7 percent jump in operating expenses to $15.9 million. That boost was mainly due to a near doubling of research and development costs to $12.4 million. The company is testing several products for a range of possible treatments, including colon and pancreatic cancers.

For the year ended June 30, the company lost $17.8 million, or 43 cents per share, compared with a loss of $11 million, or 27 cents per share, in the previous fiscal year. Revenue increased to $21.8 million from $18.4 million. (AP)

J.C. Penney Co. said it earned $179 million, or 76 cents per share, in the second quarter ended July 29. It said earnings from continuing operations were 75 cents per share.

Analysts had expected 73 cents per share. A year earlier, the Plano, Texas, company earned $131 million or 50 cents per share.

Revenue rose 6.5 percent to $4.24 billion, from $3.98 billion a year earlier. The sales results were slightly above the $4.19 billion forecast by analysts. (AP)

Change +$2.44
52-WEEK
High $59.29

Target Corp. said second-quarter earnings were $609 million, or 70 cents per share, in the period ended July 29, up from $540 million, or 61 cents per share, in the quarter that ended July 30 last year.

Revenue rose 11.3 percent to $13.3 billion. That included credit card revenue of $388 million, up 20 percent from the same period last year.

Analysts were expecting earnings of 69 cents per share on revenue of almost $13.4 billion. Last month Minneapolis-based Target said it would meet or exceed 69 cents per share. (AP)

SatCon Technology Corp., a Boston company that makes solar and other alternative energy sources, reported a loss of $3.5 million or 9 cents per share in its fiscal 2006 third quarter, which ended July 1. That's a 52.2 percent deepening of red ink compared with the same quarter a year ago when it lost $2.3 million, or 7 cents per share.

The losses came as revenue remained flat and operating expenses increased slightly. Total revenue in the quarter was $8.1 million, compared with $8.2 million in the 2005 quarter. Total operating expenses, meanwhile, grew 11.9 percent, to $11.6 million from about $10.4 million last year. (Keith Reed)

R&D costs propel CombinatoRx loss

Close $6.60
Change +$0.21
52-WEEK
High $14.50
Low $6.00

CombinatoRx Inc.'s second-quarter loss widened due to higher research and development expenses.

The Cambridge pharmaceutical company had a loss of $9.03 million, or 32 cents a share, compared with a loss of $8.83 million, or $11.44 a share, in the comparable period a year ago.

CombinatoRx said revenue for the recent quarter ended June 30 jumped to $3.4 million from $547,000 in the 2005 period.

Research and development costs widened 54 percent to $8.7 million from $5.65 million a year earlier.

In addition, CombinatoRx said it expects the loss for all of 2006 to be $31 million to $36 million. (Dow Jones)
