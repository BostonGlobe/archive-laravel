BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Comcast offers free game service to Net customers

May 2, 2006

THE REGION
Comcast Corp. is introducing a free online gaming service that is available only to subscribers to its fastest and most expensive Internet service. GameInvasion will be offered to customers who pay $52.95 per month for Comcast's top-tier 8-megabits-per-second broadband connections. Those customers will also get a free subscription to IGN Entertainment's IGN Founder's Club website, which features game reviews, demos, cheat codes, downloads, and other video-game-related content. (Keith Reed)

Bank of America appoints Gallery president for Mass.
Bank of America Corp. has appointed Robert E. Gallery its president for Massachusetts. Gallery will serve as a public face for the bank and represent its interests in civic and philanthropic affairs. He replaces Jane Magpiong. She continues to run Bank of America's private bank that serves the wealthy. In addition to his role as state president, Gallery, 54, is the private bank's market executive for the Eastern Massachusetts region. He is also a member of the board of directors of the Make-A-Wish Foundation of Massachusetts and Horizons for Homeless Children. (Sasha Talcott)

Erie Plastics to shut down Westborough facility
Erie Plastics, an injection molding company, said it will close its plant in Westborough by the end of June, eliminating 35 jobs. The company said it has had less need for the satellite plant after a 2001 relocation to much larger headquarters in Corry, Pa. When it opened in 1996, the Westborough plant supplied Gillette Co. and evolved into making plastic components such as bottle tops. ''Although we attempted to expand business in New England, most of our opportunities have occurred in the Midwest," said P.C. Hoop Roche, chief executive of Erie Plastics. (Chris Reidy)

US malls owner to buy 7 Federated properties
Simon Property Group Inc., the largest owner of US shopping malls, agreed to buy seven Federated Department Stores Inc. buildings located at Simon malls in Massachusetts, Indiana, and New Hampshire. The stores include the Filene's at Burlington Mall and the Macy's at South Shore Plaza in Braintree and Pheasant Lane Mall in Nashua. No purchase price was given. (Bloomberg)

Hanover Insurance profit drops 13% in 1st quarter
The Hanover Insurance Group Inc. said first-quarter profit dropped 13 percent from last year, due to a loss on the sale of one of its businesses. Income for the quarter was $40.5 million, or 75 cents per share, down from $46.5 million, or 86 cents per share, last year. Earnings were reduced by a charge of $20.1 million, or 38 cents per share, related to loss on the sale of its variable life insurance and annuity business to Goldman Sachs. (AP)

THE NATION
Google irked by settings on new Microsoft browser
Google Inc. is hoping to pressure Microsoft Corp. into changing a new Internet Explorer browser feature that could direct more people to Microsoft's online search engine instead of Google's far more popular offering. Google has informally complained to US and European antitrust regulators about what it says are biased settings. The next version of Internet Explorer, available now in test form, includes a box in the corner that lets people perform an Internet search without going to a separate Web page, much like what's available from Google's toolbar. (AP)

SEC, IRS chiefs mull more corporate tax information
Stock regulators and the IRS are exploring the idea of requiring companies to publicly release more tax information, the chairman of the Securities and Exchange Commission said. Chairman Christopher Cox said he discussed the subject recently with IRS commissioner Mark Everson. Cox stressed that the idea is not a formal proposal. He said he and Everson ''recently had lunch to discuss in part whether or not side-by-side presentation of tax reporting and financial reporting would be beneficial for investors. (AP)

AMC Theatres to set aside screens for independents
AMC Theatres is about to start showcasing independent films in theaters in areas where art house viewers are believed to reside. AMC plans to designate 72 of its 5,672 theaters as ''AMC Select" venues. There, screens will be set aside not just for studio blockbusters, but also for independent films and low-budget documentaries. (Los Angeles Times)
