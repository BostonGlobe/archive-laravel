BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Verisign to pay $125m for Needham company

May 18, 2006

THE REGION
GeoTrust Inc., a Needham supplier of services that enable customers to transmit documents securely over the Internet, has agreed to be acquired for about $125 million by security company VeriSign Inc. of Mountain View, Calif. VeriSign provides services that protect interactions across voice and data networks. The merger, which is subject to regulatory approval, is expected to be completed in the second half of 2006. GeoTrust, which has 50 employees, was backed by Prism Venture Partners of Westwood. (Robert Weisman)

FDA grants fast-track status to hepatitis drug
The Food and Drug Administration granted fast-track designation to an experimental treatment for hepatitis C being developed by Coley Pharmaceutical Group Inc. of Wellesley. The experimental drug, Actilon, is in a phase two clinical trial intended to determine its safety and effectiveness in treating patients that haven't responded to traditional drugs. Extensive additional testing is required before the drug can be approved, but the designation will enable company officials to work more closely with federal regulators, potentially speeding the review. (Jeffrey Krasner)

President of Financial Research leaving firm
Neil Bathon, founder and president of Financial Research Corp., is stepping down at the Boston financial data company owned by Bisys Group Inc. Bathon, who sold the company nine years ago, will continue to advise it under a consulting contract. Bathon, who plans to start a consulting business serving financial service firms, said he decided to leave after his attempts to buy back the company were unsuccessful. Financial Research will operate under the management of Mark Phillips, senior vice president at Bisys Fund Services, the company said. (Steven Syre)

Adidas promotes official to run newly acquired unit
Adidas-Salomon AG placed Uli Becker, who helped boost sales of the three-stripe Adidas brand, in charge of global marketing for the recently acquired Reebok unit to revive demand. Becker will replace Dennis Baldwin, who is leaving, and will start at Reebok's Canton headquarters on July 17, Adidas said. Becker's job as head of Adidas brands global marketing will be taken by Eric Liedtke, who has been in charge of Adidas marketing in the United States. (Bloomberg)

Retailer's net income falls 20.6% in first quarter
Hingham-based clothing retailer Talbots Inc. said first-quarter profit fell 20.6 percent as higher compensation expenses offset increased revenue and projected a current quarter loss due to costs from its recent acquisition of J. Jill Group. For the quarter ended April 29, net income was $27.4 million, or 51 cents per share, versus the prior year's $34.5 million, or 63 cents per share. Excluding stock-option expenses, earnings totaled 54 cents per share, while revenue rose to $453 million from $446.5 million in the year-earlier period. Sales in stores open at least one year rose 0.9 percent. (AP)

THE NATION
Google plans to offer instant video-sharing
Google Inc., the most-used Internet search engine, is introducing a feature that lets people instantly post videos to the Web in a bid to topple market leader YouTube Inc. The Google site will let users submit clips and then provide a Web link for the video that they can share with friends, Hunter Walk, a Google product manager, said. Watching user-generated videos on the Web is becoming more popular as more people buy high-speed connections and sites such as YouTube and Google make it easier to post video clips. (Bloomberg)

Chairman says profit drop doesn't require firings
Intel Corp.'s falling profit, loss of market share, and slumping share price don't amount to a ''firing offense," chairman Craig Barrett told shareholders. The decline in the value of Intel shares and performance-related pay was ''punishment" for executives, Barrett said in response to questions at the company's annual meeting. ''It's not clear to me that a firing offense has been committed," he said. (Bloomberg)
