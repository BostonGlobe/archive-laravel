Staples profit rises 26% in 1st quarter

May 17, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $24.82
Change -$1.60
52-WEEK
High $27.71
Low $19.78

Staples Inc. said yesterday first-quarter profit rose 26 percent on gains in its office products delivery business and overseas operations. But the world's largest office products retailer reported disappointing sales growth at its North American stores.

Staples said net income for the period ended April 29 rose to $186.1 million, or 25 cents per share, from a profit of $147.7 million, or 20 cents per share, in the same quarter a year ago. Sales rose 9 percent to $4.24 billion from $3.9 billion a year ago.

The most recent quarter's profit beat the consensus forecast of 23 cents a share by analysts surveyed by Thomson Financial, and sales results fell just shy of analysts' forecast of $4.26 billion.

Framingham-based Staples said first-quarter sales edged up 1 percent at North American stores open at least a year, the company's smallest such increase since 2002's third quarter. (AP)

Cost reductions aid TJX bottom line

Close $23.75
Change -$0.13
52-WEEK
High $26.28
Low $19.95

Discount retailer TJX Cos. said higher sales and cost reductions sent first-quarter profit up 21 percent, offsetting a one-time expense to pay severance and other costs incurred from cutting 250 headquarters jobs.

The operator of T.J. Maxx, Marshalls, and other chains reported net income of $163.8 million, or 34 cents per share for the quarter ended April 29, compared with a profit of $135.6 million, or 28 cents per share, in last year's first quarter. Revenue rose 7 percent to $3.9 billion from $3.65 billion.

Wall Street had forecast a profit of 33 cents per share, the average estimate of 18 analysts surveyed by Thomson Financial, on projected sales of $3.94 billion.

Framingham-based TJX said results included a one-time pretax charge of $7 million related to job cuts, which reduced profit by a penny per share. TJX cut roughly 6 percent of its headquarters staff during the quarter and noted that 12 top executives agreed to a 10 percent pay cut. (AP)

Wal-Mart Stores Inc., the world's largest retailer, said fiscal first-quarter profit rose 6.3 percent on the company's biggest sales gain in two years.

Net income climbed to $2.62 billion, or 63 cents a share. Sales rose 12 percent to $80.5 billion.

Chief executive H. Lee Scott said gasoline prices are beginning to affect customers and could ''pressure" the second quarter. (Bloomberg)

Home Depot Inc., the world's largest home improvement retailer, said first-quarter profit rose 19 percent on demand for building supplies and appliances. The shares had the biggest drop in almost three years after sales missed analyst estimates.

Net income climbed to $1.48 billion, or 70 cents a share, from $1.25 billion, or 57 cents, a year earlier, the Atlanta-based retailer said. Revenue in the period ended April 30 gained 13 percent to $21.5 billion.

Chief executive Robert Nardelli said he was ''disappointed" with retail sales in the quarter, particularly flooring and seasonal products such as lawn mowers. (Bloomberg)

Change -$0.52
52-WEEK
High $34.52

Hewlett-Packard Co., the world's number two personal computer seller, said second-quarter profit rose 51 percent as the company took orders from Dell Inc. Earnings this quarter may top analysts' estimates.

Net income rose to $1.46 billion, or 51 cents a share, from $966 million, or 33 cents, a year earlier, Palo Alto, Calif.-based HP said. Sales rose 4.6 percent to $22.6 billion in the period ended April 30.

Profit, including stock-based compensation but excluding some costs, was 54 cents a share. Goldman Sachs Group Inc.'s Laura Conigliaro expected 49 cents on sales of $22.6 billion. Her estimates matched the average projections from a Thomson Financial survey. (Bloomberg)

Teen clothing retailer Abercrombie & Fitch Co. said first-quarter profit jumped 39 percent, exceeding analyst estimates, on higher sales of T-shirts and shorts. The company raised its profit forecast for the first half, sending its shares up 2.6 percent after the markets closed.

Net income climbed to $56.2 million, or 62 cents a share, from $40.4 million, or 45 cents, a year earlier. Revenue in the period ended April 29 increased 20 percent to $657.3 million, the New Albany, Ohio, company said. (Bloomberg)

American Eagle beats forecasts
YESTERDAY
Close $32.95
Change -$0.22
52-WEEK
High $34.82
Low $19.45

Youth clothing retailer American Eagle Outfitters Inc. said first-quarter profit rose 16 percent, aided by double-digit sales growth.

For the quarter ended April 29, the company reported net income of $64.2 million, or 42 cents per share, versus a prior-year profit of $55.3 million, or 35 cents per share.

Revenue rose 14 percent to $522.4 million from $456.5 million.

Wall Street had forecast a profit of 41 cents per share, the average estimate of 26 analysts surveyed by Thomson Financial, on projected sales of $518.1 million.

American Eagle projected second-quarter earnings of 39 to 41 cents per share, versus the 37 cents per share it reported a year ago.

The outlook includes stock option expense of about a penny per share for the quarter.

Such expense is seen totaling 4 to 5 cents per share for the year. (AP)
