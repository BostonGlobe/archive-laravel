EARNINGS ROUNDUP

Affiliated keeps income streak alive

October 26, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $98.49
Change +$3.63
52-WEEK
High $108.58
Low $72.90

Affiliated Managers Group Inc. said third-quarter profit rose 16 percent as institutional investors more than doubled deposits.

Net income climbed to $33.1 million, or 87 cents a share, from $28.5 million, or 67 cents, a year earlier, the Beverly company said. It was the company's 13th consecutive quarterly increase. Revenue rose 20 percent to $280.4 million.

While Affiliated Managers' revenue rose, it fell short by 3.4 percent of the analysts' estimate of $290.2 million.

In an unrelated development, Affiliated Managers said it will acquire a 60 percent stake in Chicago Equity Partners LLC, a so-called quantitative manager of $11.4 billion for institutional investors. (Bloomberg)

Thermo Electron posts revenue gain

Close $43.20
Change -$1.05
52-WEEK
High $44.65
Low $29.30

Thermo Electron Corp., a Waltham maker of electronic measurement equipment, laboratory gear, and other scientific instruments, said that third-quarter earnings fell 18 percent from a year-ago period boosted by discontinued operations. But adjusted profit beat estimates, and the company raised guidance for the full year.

Net income dipped to $48.8 million, or 30 cents per share, from $57.7 million, or 35 cents per share, a year ago. The latest quarter includes 3 cents per share of stock option expense compared with last year's gain of 10 cents from discontinued operations.

Adjusted earnings per share grew 16 percent to 44 cents per share, including stock option costs.

Revenue rose 7 percent to $725 million from $679.4 million last year.

Analysts were looking for profit of 43 cents per share on sales of $711.8 million. (AP)

Cytyc profits up 16% as sales rise

Change -$0.12
52-WEEK
High $30.77

Cytyc Corp., Marlborough supplier of products used in screening for and treating women's health issues, said third-quarter profit rose 16 percent on stronger sales of surgical and diagnostic products.

Net income rose to $36.1 million, or 31 cents per share, from $31.1 million, or 26 cents per share, in the year-ago period.

Sales expanded 18 percent to $154.3 million from $130.8 million a year ago, driven by a 33 percent rise in sales of domestic surgical products to $53 million and a 7 percent rise in sales of domestic diagnostic products to $84 million. International sales jumped 39 percent to $17.3 million.

Analysts expected earnings of 28 cents per share on sales of $154.1 million. (AP)

Earnings off by 3% at TD Banknorth

Close $29.53
Change -$0.29
52-WEEK
High $31.40
Low $26.00

Northeastern regional bank TD Banknorth Inc. said third-quarter earnings fell 3 percent year-over-year, hurt by a drop in securities income and higher interest expenses.

TD Banknorth, a Portland, Maine-based unit of Canada's TD Bank Financial Group, said net income fell to $86.1 million, or 38 cents per share, from $88.7 million, or 51 cents per share, a year ago. Earnings adjusted to exclude one-time items rose to $117 million, or 51 cents per share, from $109.5 million, or 63 cents per share, last year.

The discrepancy in per-share earnings is caused by an increase of shares outstanding after the company issued 62 million shares to acquire Hudson United Bancorp in January.

Analysts expected profit of 52 cents per share.

Net interest income rose 21 percent to $301.1 million from $249 million last year, boosted by the acquisition of Hudson United. However, interest expense doubled to $203 million. (AP)

Factory closings slow GM losses

Close $34.71
Change -$1.48
52-WEEK
High $36.56
Low $18.33

General Motors Corp., the world's largest automaker, narrowed its third-quarter loss to $115 million on savings from closing factories, buying out union workers, and trimming health care expenses.

The loss shrank to 20 cents a share from a loss of $1.66 billion, or $2.94, a year earlier, GM said. Excluding costs for shutting plants and shedding jobs, the Detroit company earned $529 million, or 93 cents a share, beating analysts' estimates of 49 cents.

Revenue rose 3.5 percent to a record $48.8 billion. GM's net loss from North American auto operations narrowed to $374 million from a loss of $2.2 billion. Worldwide automotive losses fell to $62 million from $2.5 billion. (Bloomberg)

Monster sees loss as revenue rises

Close $40.75
Change -$1.17
52-WEEK
High $59.99
Low $29.88

Monster Worldwide Inc., owner of the online job-listing service, reported a loss of $83.8 million on costs to sell its TMP advertising agency and said it will restate nine years of results to correct stock-options costs.

The loss was 64 cents a share. Revenue rose 38 percent to $285.9 million, bolstered by demand in Europe, the New York-based company said. Monster didn't provide year-earlier results because of a continuing stock option probe.

Excluding the sale of TMP Worldwide, profit was $40.1 million, or 31 cents a share, matching the estimate of analysts surveyed. (Bloomberg)
