Tycoon Kerzner dies in copter crash

By Jonathan M. Katz, Associated Press Writer  |  October 12, 2006

SANTO DOMINGO, Dominican Republic --Resort tycoon Howard "Butch" Kerzner, who ran a casino empire spanning the globe, died in a helicopter crash as he was surveying potential development sites on the Dominican Republic's north coast, his company said. He was 42.

Kerzner, chief executive officer of Bahamas-based Kerzner International Ltd., was killed with three others aboard the helicopter Wednesday afternoon when it struck a building in the tourist resort of Sosua, said Yolanda Manan, a spokeswoman for the Dominican airport authority.

In the Bahamas, where the company's flagship Atlantis resort is the second-largest employer behind the government, Prime Minister Perry Christie said it was "a sad moment for our nation."

The late executive's father, Sol Kerzner, built a casino empire in South Africa that included the controversial gambling resort Sun City, which opened in 1979. Artists such as Frank Sinatra and Rod Stewart who played there were accused of breaking cultural boycotts imposed against the country to protest apartheid.

Resort and casino operator Kerzner International now has stakes in five resorts in Mauritius, one in Mexico and manages a resort in Dubai. It developed and owns nearly 70 percent of the Bahamas' Paradise Island, home to its 2,300-room Atlantis casino resort, which includes a man-made marine habitat and 60 acres of pools.

The company went private in September, when Howard Kerzner and his father led a group of investors in a US$3.8 billion (euro3 billion) buyout.

Sol Kerzner, who is now chairman of Kerzner International's board, was en route to the Dominican Republic on Wednesday night.

The helicopter, piloted by two officers from the Dominican armed forces who also died, crashed on the roof on a plant nursery. A commission was investigating what caused the Robinson 44 helicopter to go down.

The fourth victim was Delio Luis Gonzalez, Manan said. The online Dominican newspaper Clave Digital identified him as the son of a U.S.-based real estate investor who had worked with Kerzner International. His nationality was not known.

Howard Kerzner, a South African who held permanent residency in the Bahamas, was born in Durban and earned a master's in business administration from Stanford University.

Kerzner International is one of several companies bidding to build a casino resort in Singapore. The company said in a statement Thursday that it is "fully committed" to continuing its joint bid with Singapore's CapitaLand Ltd.

Paul O'Neil, former president and managing director of Kerzner International, has been named to replace Kerzner as chief executive, the company said.

Kerzner is survived by his wife, Vanessa, and two young children, the company said.

Associated Press writer Jessica Robertson contributed to this report from Nassau, Bahamas.
