BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Metabolix shares climb almost 11% on first day

November 11, 2006

THE REGION

Shares of Cambridge-based Metabolix Inc., which makes plastic out of crops, climbed $1.52, or 10.9 percent, to $15.52 in first-day trading. The company sold 6.8 million shares -- a million more than originally planned -- at an initial public offering price of $14 a shares. Agribusiness giant Archer Daniels Midland Co., which is a current Metabolix stockholder and collaborative partner on its biggest project, purchased $7.5 million of additional stock at the IPO price in a private placement.(Dow Jones/AP)

Real estate investment trust buys 3 Cambridge locations
Boston Properties Inc. has signed a contract to buy two office buildings and a parking garage in Cambridge for $186 million according to a third-quarter regulatory filing the Boston real estate investment trust made to the Securities and Exchange Commission. The office properties represent a total of 436,000 rentable square feet. The garage can accommodate 840 vehicles, the company said. (Dow Jones)

Drug maker to sell EU rights to sleep aid Lunesta
Sepracor Inc. plans to sell European rights to its Lunesta sleeping aid, a challenger to Sanofi- Aventis SA's Ambien, and will include data on the drug's benefits on depression in the application for approval next year. The Marlborough company plans to submit its application for European approval of the medicine in the second quarter of 2007, Jonae Barnes, vice president of investor relations has said. Tests have shown that it can help depressed patients, who have trouble sleeping. It was approved by the US Food and Drug Administration in 2004. (Bloomberg)

Linear Retail acquires 15 White Hen Pantry sites
Linear Retail Properties LLC, a Burlington real estate acquisition and operating company, said it paid $12.3 million to acquire 15 White Hen Pantry locations in Eastern Massachusetts. Linear described the deal as a sale-leaseback transaction. The seller was New England Pantry Inc., which operates 55 White Hen Pantry locations in New England, Linear said. Stores in Winthrop, Revere, Everett, Lynn, and West Roxbury were part of the deal. (Chris Reidy)

Bangor Daily News lays off 11 newsroom employees
The Bangor Daily News said it is laying off 11 employees, citing industry trends of declining circulation and ad lineage, and increasing expenses. Publisher Richard Warren said that even though the newspaper has cut costs, layoffs could not be avoided. The News offered extended severance packages to the affected people, who include several longtime employees. The Maine newspaper has a daily circulation of about 60,000; 70,000 for its Saturday-Sunday editions. (Dow Jones)

Arqule antitumor drug shows promise in study
Woburn biotechnology company Arqule Inc. said interim results of an early-stage antitumor drug trial showed positive results. Shares rose 47 cents, or 11.6 percent, to $4.51. The company said interim phase 1 clinical trial data show ARQ 197 was tolerable and showed signs of antitumor activity in cancer patients. In all, 37 patients were involved. (AP)

. . . Etc.
First Marblehead Corp.'s board declared a 3-for-2 stock split, payable Dec. 4 to stockholders of record Nov. 20. The Boston education-finance company said the board also increased its dividend payout 20 percent by declaring a quarterly cash dividend of 12 cents on the post-split shares, also payable Dec. 4 to shareholders of record Nov. 20. The dividend is equivalent to 18 cents on presplit shares, on which the company had been paying 15 cents quarterly . . . IBasis said it has delayed the filing of its third-quarter report with the Securities and Exchange Commission as it has not yet completed its review of historical accounting for stock-based compensation. The Burlington long-distance carrier said it expects to record charges of $10 million to $20 million from December 1999 to April 2006 . . . The Tisch family acquired a $63 million stake in The New York Times Co. during the third quarter. Loews Corp., the Manhattan-based holding company that served as an investment vehicle for the late Laurence Tisch, disclosed in a regulatory filing that it held 2.61 million New York Times class A shares on Sept. 30. It's the first time Loews has reported owning the stock since 2001, when it had 6,000 shares. (Globe wire services)
