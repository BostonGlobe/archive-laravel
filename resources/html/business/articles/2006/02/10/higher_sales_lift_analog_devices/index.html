Higher sales lift Analog Devices

February 10, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $40.91
Change +$0.01
52-WEEK
High $41.48
Low $31.71

Analog Devices Inc., the Norwood maker of digital signal processors, said profit grew 12 percent in the fiscal first quarter as demand surged in its consumer electronics and industrial segments.

For the quarter ended Jan. 28, earnings rose to $120.6 million, or 32 cents per share, from $107.4 million, or 28 cents, a year ago. Restructuring charges and stock option costs reduced latest-quarter income by 5 cents per share. Sales for the period totaled $621.3 million, a 7 percent gain from $580.5 million a year earlier.

Analysts expected quarterly earnings of 36 cents per share and $624 million in sales. (AP)

MetLife Inc. of New York said that higher premiums driven by its acquisition of Travelers Life & Annuity caused fourth-quarter profit to rise 33 percent.

Profit rose to $677 million, or 88 cents per share, from $511 million, or 68 cents per share, in the year-ago period. Operating earnings rose to $799 million, or $1.04 per share, from $660 million, or 88 cents per share. Analysts projected profit of $1.01 per share on revenue of $12.15 billion, according to Thomson Financial. (AP)

Timberland Co. posted a better-than-expected 4 percent rise in quarterly profit on higher sales, sending the footwear maker's shares up more than 6 percent.

The Stratham, N.H., company, which makes shoes, apparel, and accessories, said fourth-quarter profit rose to $46.9 million, or 71 cents per share, from $45 million, or 64 cents per share, a year earlier.

Analysts expected 62 cents per share.

Quarterly revenue increased 2.3 percent to $465.3 million.

The company had said in October that revenue would likely be flat with a year earlier. Analysts expected $455.5 million. (Reuters)

Annuity business sparks Manulife
YESTERDAY
Close $61.50
Change +$1.03
52-WEEK
High $61.83
Low $44.57

Manulife Financial Corp. reported a higher fourth-quarter profit, helped by strong annuity sales in Japan and the United States.

Manulife said net income for the quarter ended Dec. 31 rose to $790 million, or 98 cents a share, from $660 million, or 80 cents a share, in the year-ago period.

Analysts had expected, on average, a profit of 98 cents a share before one-time items.

Revenue climbed to $7.2 billion from $6.9 billion.

Chief executive DominicD'Alessandro has increased earnings outside Canada through the $13.9 billion purchase of Boston-based John Hancock Financial Services and expansion in Asia.

Manulife now receives more than 60 percent of its profit outside of Canada.

Manulife offers financial protection products and wealth management services. (Globe wires)

Beacon Roofing Supply Inc.'s fiscal first-quarter net income rose 48 percent, helped by strong sales.

The Peabody provider of roofing and building products reported net income for the quarter ended Dec. 31 of $12.9 million, or 46 cents a share.

That was up from $8.7 million, or 32 cents a share, in the year-ago quarter.

Analysts expected earnings of 38 cents a share.

Sales in the latest quarter saw a 71 percent surge to $339.9 million from $199.2 million a year earlier. (Dow Jones)

Merger costs hurt Nuance in period
YESTERDAY
Close $8.90
Change -$0.02
52-WEEK
High $9.42
Low $3.42

Nuance Communications Inc., a Burlington provider of digital imaging and speech technology, reported a loss for its fiscal first quarter as merger-related expenses pulled down results.

The company reported a loss of $4.9 million, or 3 cents per share, compared with earnings of $3.1 million, or 3 cents per share, during the same period a year ago.

Excluding items related to the combination of Nuance and ScanSoft Inc. that created the company last year, as well as costs related to the expensing of stock options, the company had earnings of $11.4 million, or 7 cents per share, compared with adjusted earnings of $9.1 million, or 8 cents per share, a year ago.

Sales rose 25 percent to $75.6 million from last year's $60.6 million.

Analysts were looking for earnings of 5 cents per share on sales of $74.9 million. (AP)

Palomar Medical Technologies Inc., a Burlington researcher and developer of light-based systems for cosmetic treatments, said its profit remained relatively flat in the latest quarter despite a 32 percent rise in revenue.

Palomar had earnings of $5.3 million or 27 cents a share in the fourth quarter, vs. $5.4 million or 29 cents a share, a year ago.

Analysts expected earnings of 27 cents for the latest quarter.

Revenue was $21.6 million, up from $16.4 million in the fourth quarter of 2004. (Dow Jones)
