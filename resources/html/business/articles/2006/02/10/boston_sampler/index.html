DOWNTOWN

Boston sampler

By Steve Bailey, Globe columnist  |  February 10, 2006

Staples Inc., one of the great made-in-Massachusetts companies, is kicking off its 20th anniversary celebration with a big party beginning this weekend at the Mandalay Bay casino in Las Vegas. Founder Tom Stemberg was supposed to make a champagne toast to 5,000 people on the big night of the global sales meeting. It's not going to happen.

Stemberg is still fuming about being pushed off the board in March. Despite the hard feelings, Stemberg had agreed to give the anniversary toast until he learned that the two directors who engineered his ouster -- former Toys ''R" Us chief executive Robert C. Nakasone and Rowland T. Moriarty, a former Harvard Business School associate professor -- would be at the dinner.

''I will never knowingly enter a room when either of them is present," Stemberg told me. ''I would smell them."

It was Nakasone and Moriarty, both members of the board's corporate governance committee, who told Stemberg they wanted him to resign as chairman and a board member when his term expired. Stemberg had turned over the chief executive job to Ron Sargent in 2002, but the two directors told him current corporate governance protocol called for him to cut all ties with the company he founded.

Stemberg saw it otherwise, and was so angered he threatened to launch a proxy campaign urging shareholders to withhold their votes for the two directors. ''Get over it" are not three words that you associate with the passionate Stemberg.

Now a director at Highland Capital Partners, Stemberg was supposed to have been in Vegas along with his girlfriend, Katherine Chapman, and their new son. ''I feel bad that I can't be there. It hurts," says Stemberg, 57.

Leo Kahn, who helped bankroll Staples for Stemberg, is staying home, too.

Staples declined to answer any questions about the event.

Business is booming at Logan's Terminal C, thanks to JetBlue, but at gorgeous new Terminal A the restaurants and shops are hurting because of bankrupt Delta Air Lines.

Things have gotten so bad that Massport is restructuring its arrangement with Westfield Group, the leasing agent, which will then be able to reduce rents to the retailers. The hardest hit are the dozen or so shops in the terminal's annex, such as Summer Shack, Harpoon Brewery, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art. The Massport board is expected to vote on the new deal next week.

Boston super-chef Jasper White, who heads the four-restaurant Summer Shack chain, says traffic needs to increase 30 percent before the place is viable. But he remains optimistic. ''There is not a chance I would pull out. We have too much invested" -- about $2 million. ''We can suck it up a while longer."

Neighborhood news

  • Largely out of sight, one of the better things happening in town is the planning and fund-raising for a new facility for the Greater Boston Food Bank. Hunger is unfortunately a growth business, and the food bank wants to more than double its space on 2.8 acres of state-owned land on a former incinerator site adjacent to its current location off the Southeast Expressway. The fund-raising campaign, headed by Ted Cutler of GWV Travel and Joanne Jaxtimer of Mellon Financial, has quietly raised $13.5 million of the $25 million goal. Look for a statement soon on the campaign's public phase.

  • Today is state Consumer Affairs director Beth Lindstrom's last day. A Republican, Lindstrom is weighing a challenge to Democratic Treasurer Tim Cahill. Don't buy the story she jumped; sources inside and outside of the Romney administration say she was pushed by Ranch Kimball, the state economic development chief, who has been consolidating his power. Lindstrom and Kimball are said to have been at odds for awhile, including her support for a historic regional compact to cut greenhouse gas emissions. Kimball and Lindstrom deny all. But she is just the latest to be Kimballed: Barbara Berke, director of business and technology, left soon after he arrived, and Boston developer Robert Beal was squeezed out as chairman of MassDevelopment.
