EARNINGS ROUNDUP

Sales growth lifts Dell profit by 52%

February 17, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $31.96
Change +$0.19
52-WEEK
High $41.99
Low $28.62

Dell Inc. said that fourth-quarter profit rose 52 percent as the world's largest personal computer maker finally turned in stronger sales growth than expected, led by sales to businesses and to international customers.

Dell said it earned $1.01 billion, or 43 cents per share in the quarter ended Feb. 3, compared to $667 million, or 26 cents per share a year earlier.

Analysts had expected the PC maker to earn 41 cents per share in the most recent quarter. Revenue rose 13 percent to a record $15.18 billion, exceeding the $14.82 billion forecast of analysts.

Dell said first-quarter earnings would be 39 cents to 41 cents per share excluding a cost of 3 cents per share for stock compensation.

Analysts forecast 42 cents per share. The company said revenue would be $14.2 billion to $14.6 billion, a bit below analysts' prediction of $14.73 billion.

Yesterday's report followed two previous disappointing quarters from Dell, which was hurt by slowing sales growth and profit-sapping restructuring charges. (AP)

XM Satellite Radio Inc., the biggest US pay-radio service, posted a wider loss than analysts predicted and said a director quit after warning of an impending financial crisis.

Director Pierce Roberts, who had criticized XM Satellite's surging spending, resigned this week, Washington-based XM said. The company's fourth-quarter net loss expanded to $268.3 million, or $1.22 a share, wider than the 97 cents estimated by Banc of America analyst Jonathan Jacoby.

XM Satellite accelerated spending on promotions as the company pushed to win customers and fight off Sirius Satellite Radio Inc., its closest competitor and the home of Howard Stern. XM last week signed a $55 million contract with Oprah Winfrey.

While revenue more than doubled to $177.1 million, marketing costs rose to $196.5 million from $107.9 million. The fourth-quarter loss a year earlier was $188.2 million, or 93 cents a share, XM said in the statement. (Bloomberg)

52-WEEK

Semiconductor-testing equipment maker LTX Corp. said that it narrowed its fiscal second-quarter loss, and it expects third-quarter results to come in ahead of current estimates.

The Westwood company's loss for the quarter ended Jan. 31 was $1.4 million, or 2 cents per share, compared with a loss of $19.2 million, or 31 cents per share last year. During the quarter, the company recorded a charge of 3 cents per share related to downsized operations in the United Kingdom. Revenue for the quarter was $47.8 million, up from $27 million last year.

Analysts expected the company to earn a penny per share on $47.1 million in revenue. (AP)

Tax gain helps Sapient earnings

Change +$0.40
52-WEEK

Sapient Corp. posted higher fourth-quarter earnings, helped by a tax benefit.

The Cambridge technology and business consulting firm earned $11.4 million, or 9 cents a share, in the latest quarter, up from $8 million, or 6 cents a share, a year earlier.

Without the tax gain, which was related to the reversal of a deferred tax valuation allowance in Germany, Sapient earned $9 million, or 7 cents a share, which is above average analysts' estimates of 5 cents.

Revenue rose almost 30 percent to $87.3 million from $69.6 million while operating cash flow topped $30 million in the latest period. (Dow Jones)

Avici Systems Inc. narrowed its fourth-quarter losses and said it plans to reduce its staff by 45 percent as it restructures its business and realigns its cost structure.

The Billerica telecommunications company said its fourth-quarter loss narrowed to $8.6 million, or 67 cents a share, from $9.71 million, or 76 cents a share, as gross revenue climbed to $6.12 million from $3.84 million a year earlier.

The fourth-quarter loss before items narrowed to 63 cents from 72 cents a share. Analysts expected Avici to report a loss of 46 cents a share. (Dow Jones)
