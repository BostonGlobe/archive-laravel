BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Progress Software to delay SEC filing due to options

June 20, 2006

THE REGION
Progress Software Corp. said that it plans to voluntarily review its stock options program, and that as a result it would not immediately report its second-quarter earnings. The Bedford company, which makes development tools for business software, said quarterly revenue totaled $109.6 million, up 9 percent from $100.2 million a year ago. Software license revenue rose 11 percent to $41.4 million from $37.2 million in the prior-year period. (AP)

Hub financial services firm opens seven mutual funds
John Hancock Funds LLC, a unit of Canadian life insurer Manulife Financial Corp., opened seven equity mutual funds managed by Grantham, Mayo Van Otterloo & Co. to expand sales after stanching years of investor redemptions. The funds include US Core, which will invest in large-company stocks; Intrinsic Value, for stocks considered inexpensive relative to earnings; and Growth, for companies whose earnings are rising faster than those of peers, the Boston-based company said. Boston-based Grantham Mayo manages $125 billion for mainly institutional investors. (Bloomberg)

Defense contractor cuts 100 jobs from El Paso unit
Raytheon Co., the fifth-largest US defense company, eliminated more than 100 jobs supporting the Army at Fort Bliss, Texas, after government funding cuts. The jobs, shed from Raytheon's technical services unit in El Paso on Friday, provided logistics support to troops at Fort Bliss including maintenance and transportation services, company spokeswoman Kristen Giddens Pinto-Coelho said. Raytheon's technical services business accounted for $1.98 billion, or 9 percent, of the company's total revenue of $21.9 billion last year. (Bloomberg)

Vertex seeks partner for EU sales of hepatitis C pill
Vertex Pharmaceuticals Inc., which is betting on an experimental hepatitis C pill to help it turn profitable, expects to find a partner this year to market the drug in Europe. The Cambridge company plans to license the experimental drug, VX-950, for sale in Europe when it is available as early as 2008, chief executive Joshua Boger said. The company plans to seek US approval for the drug at that time. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION
Drug maker recalls patch version of Triaminic
Novartis AG, the maker of Triaminic children's cough and cold medicine, is recalling a patch version of the treatment after some youngsters swallowed the product and suffered seizures. There have been ``multiple reported complaints," Novartis said. The product contains camphor, eucalyptus oil, and menthol. Symptoms of ingestion of camphor and eucalyptus include headache, nausea, and seizures, the company said. The recall only involves the Vapor Patch version, which is placed on a child's throat or chest to allow vapors to reach the nose and mouth. (Bloomberg)

Hollywood studios in talks for deal with iTunes site
Apple Computer Inc. is in talks with most of Hollywood's studios to offer movie downloads via its iTunes Music Store website, show business newspaper Daily Variety reported. Citing unnamed sources, Variety said iTunes might begin offering film downloads by year-end, but currently a price on iTunes is a sticking point in negotiations. Apple's chief executive Steve Jobs is said to want one flat price of $9.99 per movie, whereas studio executives want a range of prices that mirrors videos and DVDs at retail stores which, generally speaking, run from discount titles at a few dollars each up to $19.99 or more for new releases. (Reuters)

NYSE-Euronext merger could lead to new market
The merger of Euronext and the New York Stock Exchange could lead to a stock market's being launched in London, the head of the New York exchange said. John Thain, chief executive of the NYSE Group Inc., told the Financial Times that a new exchange in London was an option if the NYSE-Euronext combination did not deliver enough international listings. Thain said NYSE might also consider a move to take over the London Stock Exchange, though the US-based Nasdaq already holds a 25 percent stake and Thain said his preference would be to set up a rival exchange. (AP)
