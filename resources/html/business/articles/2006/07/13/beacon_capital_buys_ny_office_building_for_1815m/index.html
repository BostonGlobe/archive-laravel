BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Beacon Capital buys N.Y. office building for $181.5m

July 13, 2006

THE REGION
Beacon Capital Partners LLC, the largest US private real estate investment fund, bought a 21-story office building in White Plains, N.Y., for $181.5 million, its fourth acquisition in the metropolitan area. Boston-based Beacon bought the building, known as Westchester One, on June 30 from closely held Bianco & Pepe, said Alex McCallum, a Beacon Capital spokesman. (Bloomberg)

Mass. exports on record pace after five months
Massachusetts companies exported goods at a record pace through May. The World Institute of Strategic Economic Research, a nonprofit research group at Holyoke Community College, reported state exports totaled $9.5 billion in the first five months of 2006, four percent higher than the same period in 2005. Exports hit a record $22 billion for all of 2005. In five months of 2006, the fastest-growing exports included semiconductor testing equipment, which more than doubled from a year ago; parts for transmitting radar, radio, and television, which grew 65 percent; and medical devices and instruments, up 31 percent. Golf ball exports jumped 50 percent percent from year ago, topping $80 million. Massachusetts' fastest growing market among major trading partners is China, to which exports rose 45 percent over last year. China is now the state's sixth-largest trading partner, buying more than $470 million through May. (Robert Gavin)

Chicago trust pays $23.5m for Burlington building
Equity Office Properties Trust of Chicago, the nation's largest publicly traded office building owner and manager, said it paid a total of $548.6 million to acquire buildings in New York and Massachusetts. The company said it paid $525.1 million for the office portion of 1540 Broadway, formerly known as the ``Bertelsmann Building," in New York's Times Square, and paid $23.5 million for 77 South Bedford in Burlington. (AP)

AmberWave Systems sued in continuing legal action
Intel Corp., the world's largest computer-chip maker, filed a lawsuit seeking a ruling that it isn't infringing a patent for a method of chip fabrication held by semiconductor developer AmberWave Systems Corp. In the suit, filed in federal court in Delaware, Intel says its products don't use the same technology as Salem, N.H.-based AmberWave's protected process for coating chips to make them work faster. AmberWave was awarded the patent yesterday. The suit is one of several filed in Delaware and Texas that pit Intel -- with 99,000 employees and $38.8 billion in sales last year -- against closely held AmberWave, a company with 20 employees, according to its website. An AmberWave representative wouldn't comment on the latest lawsuit. (Bloomberg)

Conn. Wal-Marts will sell Plan-B contraceptive pill
Wal-Mart Stores Inc., the world's largest retailer, has agreed to sell a morning-after contraceptive pill in Connecticut, bringing to at least three the number of states where it will carry the drug. The agreement was reached four months after Connecticut Attorney General Richard Blumenthal ruled Wal-Mart could be excluded from that state's health insurance network if it refused to dispense the pill. Wal-Mart said it will continue to allow pharmacists to refuse to fill the prescription and to refer customers to other pharmacies. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION
Fed official concerned about nonfinancial company banks
A Federal Reserve official raised concerns about nonfinancial companies such as Wal-Mart and Home Depot establishing banks. Legislation to block such arrangements has been moving forward in the House. The two retailers are among 14 companies awaiting approval from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. to establish what is called an industrial loan corporation. The central bank is concerned because the owners of federally insured ILCs are able to avoid the regulatory requirements that apply to owners of other types of insured banks overseen by the Fed, Fed general counsel Scott Alvarez said. (AP)

Chain spicing up fast food battle with McDonald's
McDonald's Corp. is pulling the Hot `n' Spicy McChicken sandwich from its menu after just six months, according to the Chicago Tribune. The paper said the spicy sandwich would be replaced by a snack-sized chicken wrap. Meanwhile, Wendy's International Inc. said it would test a spicier chicken sandwich at the end of July. Wendy's said its Spicy Chicken sandwich, on the menu since 1996, has been a best-selling item. (Reuters)
