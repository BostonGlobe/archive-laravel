Earnings roundup

July 26, 2006

UPS fails to deliver on Wall St. forecast
YESTERDAY
Close $71.80
Change -$8.20
52-WEEK
High $83.99
Low $66.75

United Parcel Service Inc., the largest package shipping company, said second-quarter profit rose less than analysts' estimates and reduced its forecast as higher fuel costs squeezed international margins. The shares had their biggest one-day drop ever.

Net income increased 7.6 percent to $1.06 billion, or 97 cents a share, from $986 million, or 88 cents, a year earlier, UPS said. That compares with $1 a share in a Thomson Financial survey of 15 analysts. UPS's third-quarter forecast of 87 to 91 cents a share also trailed Thomson's 97-cent average. (Bloomberg)

Increased costs cut Amazon.com profit
YESTERDAY
Close $33.59
Change -$0.72
52-WEEK
High $50.00
Low $31.52

Amazon.com Inc., the world's largest online retailer, said second-quarter profit fell 58 percent after it increased spending on technology and content and lost a lawsuit filed by former partner Toys ``R" Us Inc.

Net income declined to $22 million, or 5 cents a share, from $52 million, or 12 cents, a year earlier, missing most analysts' estimates. Sales rose 22 percent to $2.14 billion, Seattle-based Amazon.com said. (Bloomberg)

Fisher Scientific posts strong gains
YESTERDAY
Close $73.16
Change +$3.10
52-WEEK
High $82.05
Low $53.50

Fisher Scientific International Inc., a maker of scientific equipment and instruments, said its second-quarter profit rose 20 percent on strong sales and a lower tax rate.

Net income grew to $122 million, or 93 cents per share, from $101.4 million, or 80 cents per share, a year ago.

The Hampton, N.H., company reported net income from continuing operations of 92 cents per share, which included one-time items of 12 cents per share and stock options expenses of 6 cents per share. Analysts surveyed by Thomson Financial, who included stock option charges in estimates but normally do not count one-time items, expected earnings per share of 96 cents.

Revenue rose 10 percent to $1.47 billion from $1.34 billion last year, with about 6 points of the increase from internal growth in the core scientific research and healthcare products. Analysts estimated revenue of $1.47 billion. (AP)

World Cup gives lift to McDonald's
YESTERDAY
Close $34.76
Change -$0.08
52-WEEK
High $36.75
Low $29.97

McDonald's Corp., the world's largest restaurant company, posted the biggest profit increase in more than a year on sales gains in Europe. The company set plans to dispose of its shares of Chipotle Mexican Grill Inc. in October.

McDonald's stockholders will be able to swap shares for Chipotle stock in an exchange offering, the company said. Second-quarter net income rose 57 percent to $834.1 million, or 67 cents a share, Oak Brook, Ill.-based McDonald's said. Per-share profit matched preliminary earnings released last week.

Revenue climbed 9.4 percent to $5.57 billion as European comparable sales gained the most in at least 10 years because of soccer's World Cup. (Bloomberg)

Sun Microsystems swings to a loss
YESTERDAY
Close $4.09
Change +$0.07
52-WEEK
High $5.38
Low $3.57

Sun Microsystems Inc., the world's number four maker of server computers, reported a fourth-quarter loss on costs related to job cuts and said demand for new products boosted sales.

The net loss was $301 million, or 9 cents a share, compared with net income of $50 million, or 1 cent, a year earlier, Santa Clara, Calif.-based Sun said. Sales rose 29 percent to $3.83 billion. (Bloomberg)

Acquisition, unit sales buoy Hologic
YESTERDAY
Close $48.17
Change +$3.30
52-WEEK
High $56.71
Low $18.34

Diagnostic imaging systems maker Hologic Inc. of Bedford said third-quarter profit rose 46 percent on surging mammography segment sales.

Net income for the quarter ended June 24 increased to $12 million, or 25 cents per share, from $8.2 million, or 18 cents per share, in the prior-year period.

On average, analysts polled by Thomson were looking for earnings of 26 cents per share.

The company incurred a $1 million charge for stock-based compensation expense and a charge of $629,000 to operating expenses to amortize intangible assets acquired from Fischer Imaging. Its May acquisition of AEG Elektrofotografie added a net loss of $1.7 million.

Revenue leaped 62 percent to $119.7 million compared to $74.1 million during the same period last year. (AP)

Art Technology makes turnaround
YESTERDAY
Close $2.40
Change -$0.31
52-WEEK
High $3.81
Low $0.84

Art Technology Group Inc., a Cambridge e-commerce, marketing, and customer care software company, said it swung to a profit in the second quarter due to improved sales in both its product license and services divisions.

Earnings were $2.3 million, or 2 cents per share, compared with a loss of $327,000, or break-even per share, in the year-ago period.

Results included about $906,000 for stock options expenses. Excluding this, profit was $4 million, or 3 cents per share, meeting the expectations of analysts polled by Thomson.

Revenue grew 25 percent to $25.2 million from $20.3 million in the year-ago period. (AP)

3M net income just meets forecast low
YESTERDAY
Close $68.11
Change -$3.58
52-WEEK
High $88.35
Low $67.05

3M Co., the maker of products from Post-it Notes to electronic road signs, posted second-quarter earnings at the low end of its already reduced forecast. Shares fell to their lowest point in three years.

Net income rose 17 percent to $882 million, or $1.15 a share, from $754 million, or 96 cents, a year earlier, St. Paul-based 3M said. Sales increased 7.4 percent to $5.69 billion. (Bloomberg)

AT&T exceeds analysts' estimates
YESTERDAY
Close $28.95
Change +$1.17
52-WEEK
High $29.77
Low $21.75

AT&T Inc., the largest US phone service provider, said second-quarter profit beat analysts' estimates and boosted its forecast for savings from job cuts and network fees.

Net income rose 81 percent to $1.81 billion, or 46 cents a share, from $1 billion, or 30 cents, a year earlier, before the merger of SBC Communications Inc. and AT&T Corp. Sales increased 53 percent to $15.8 billion. (Bloomberg)
