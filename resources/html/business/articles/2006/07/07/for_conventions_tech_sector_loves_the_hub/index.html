For conventions, tech sector loves the Hub

EBay event is latest to choose Boston; over 15,000 expected


In this file photo eBay president and CEO Meg Whitman delivers a speech at eBay Live conference in Las Vegas on Tuesday, June 13, 2006. EBay Inc. announced Thursday, July 6, 2006, the departure of Jeff Jordan, who most recently was president of eBay's PayPal payment business and had been presumed by some analysts as the likely successor to Whitman. (AP Photo/Jae C. Hong)

By Peter J. Howe, Globe Staff  |  July 7, 2006

More than 15,000 eBay buyers and sellers are set to come to Boston next June for the online auction site's main convention, bolstering the city's growing reputation as a hot destination for Internet- and technology-themed confabs.

The five-year-old ``eBay Live" show, which has been held in Las Vegas the past two years, offers a venue for thousands of eBay's 1.3 million regular sellers to swap tips and strategies and hear presentations from -- and deliver gripes to -- eBay executives. This year the San Jose, Calif., company dispatched 720 of its 12,000 employees to staff the three-day show. The Boston convention will mark the first time eBay Live has come to the Northeast.

For the new Boston Convention & Exhibition Center on the South Boston Waterfront, eBay Live will be another in a string of tech- and Net-oriented events. Microsoft Corp. and business software provider SAP AG had events earlier this year that drew thousands of attendees to the convention center. Next April, 30,000 people -- the biggest show booked yet for the center -- are expected for the Questex AIIM/On Demand Expos , an information-technology trade show and conference.

Convention officials said it appears that the Boston region, home to the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and a thriving high-tech sector along Route 128 and Interstate 495, is popular for tech-industry shows because events can draw thousands of local techies.

In the last year, tech-themed shows generated 40 percent of revenues for the BCEC and the John B. Hynes Veterans Memorial Center , said Bridget M. Perry , a spokeswoman for the Massachusetts Convention Center Authority, which runs the two halls. A total of 33 more tech-related conventions and trade shows are booked over the next five years, representing about 13 percent of all MCCA Boston events.

James E. Rooney , executive director, said technology events are particularly good business for convention halls because their sponsors are big-spending companies, rather than nonprofit institutions or associations that are focusing mainly on having economical meetings. Microsoft and SAP, for example, both had sit-down meals for more than 5,000 people during their events.

Additionally, tech spends three times as much as medical and life sciences conventions -- and far more than lower-tech sectors -- on Internet-related services while staging shows. The convention authority gets to reap higher revenues for electricity and Internet access plus a cut of spending on show-floor rigging, Rooney said.

The eBay convention stands out in that it has broad consumer appeal. More than 100 million people around the world have registered accounts to shop on eBay, which typically lists millions of items available for sale on any given day.

Angela Einstein , an official ``trading assistant" on eBay who helps buyers and sellers with antiques and heirlooms, said: ``It's huge that eBay Live is going to be coming to Boston. It's going to be very, very exciting. It's going to make New England much more aware of what eBay has to offer."

Einstein, who runs Cambridge's UpperLevelAntiques.com, said she has been too busy to attend other eBay Live events and expects it will draw thousands of people from the Northeast. But she recently attended another eBay conference on selling strategies and said: ``I left there with a gold mine of information on how to grow my business. With eBay Live in Boston, I'm looking for sparks flying from my head."

The three-day event is set for June 14-16. The most recent eBay Live drew attendees from every state and 35 countries, including nations as far-flung as Estonia and Thailand, according to the company. Boston convention officials expect the eBay event to generate demand for over 17,000 nights' worth of hotel rooms.
