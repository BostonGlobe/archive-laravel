Biogen Idec swings to 2d-quarter loss

July 27, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $40.97
Change -$0.58
52-WEEK
High $50.72
Low $35.66

Pharmaceutical company Biogen Idec Inc. of Cambridge said yesterday it swung to a second-quarter loss from a profit due to acquisition charges and stock compensation expenses.

The net loss was $170.6 million, or 50 cents per share, compared with earnings of $34.5 million, or 10 cents per share, during the same period a year ago. The latest figures include $331 million in acquisition costs related to its purchases of Conforma Therapeutics Corp. and Fumapharm AG.

Excluding acquisition costs, and stock compensation expenses, the profit was $197 million, or 57 cents per share.

Analysts polled by Thomson Financial, who excluded stock option expensing from their estimates, were looking for earnings of 49 cents per share.

Revenue increased 9 percent to $660 million from $605.6 million last year. Analysts expected revenue to reach $636.4 million. (AP)

Akamai Technologies Inc. of Cambridge, the leader in Internet content delivery services, said second-quarter net income fell 28 percent.

Net profit fell to $11.3 million, or 7 cents per share, compared with net profit of $15.9 million, or 11 cents per share, a year earlier.

The latest results include about $13.2 million, or 7 cents per share, in stock compensation charges. Excluding one-time items, profit was 20 cents per share in the latest quarter compared with 12 cents a year ago.

Revenue rose 56 percent to $100.6 million. Wall Street was looking for revenue, on average, of $94.8 million, according to Reuters Estimates. (Reuters)

Parametric profit drops as sales rise
YESTERDAY
Close $14.83
Change +$1.35
52-WEEK
High $17.85
Low $11.66

Parametric Technology Corp., a Needham maker of computer-aided design, manufacturing, and engineering software, said fiscal third-quarter profit fell despite higher sales, due mainly to stock options expensing.

But adjusted results beat Wall Street's estimates.

For the quarter ended July 1, the company earned $16.9 million, or 15 cents per share, down 37 percent from $26.7 million, or 24 cents per share, during the same period a year ago. Excluding stock option costs of 9 cents per share and amortization, restructuring, and other charges of 2 cents per share, earnings were $29.5 million, or 26 cents a share.

Sales jumped 20 percent to $216.7 million from last year's $180.3 million.

Analysts, on average, were expecting earnings of 23 cents per share on sales of $207.1 million, according to a poll by Thomson Financial. The estimates exclude options costs. (AP)

Change +$1.34
52-WEEK
High $37.57
Low $18.33

General Motors Corp. posted a larger-than-expected operating profit and raised its cost-cutting target by $1 billion as its restructuring took hold, sending its shares to a 10-month high.

The world's number one automaker by sales volume reported a wider net loss for the second quarter after writing down $4.3 billion in costs mainly associated with buyouts for almost a third of its factory workforce.

But excluding those restructuring charges, GM outperformed the most optimistic Wall Street projections.

GM raised its target for cutting recurring costs in North America by $1 billion to $6 billion by year-end after 34,400 workers accepted its buyout and early retirement offers. (Reuters)

Monster Worldwide Inc., parent of the leading US recruiting website, reported slightly higher-than-expected second-quarter profit as strong international operations and ad sales lifted revenue.

Close $29.67
Change +$0.72

High $31.40

Financial services company TD Banknorth said second-quarter net income fell because of charges related to an acquisition.

Net income was $93.4 million, or 41 cents per share, for the quarter ended June 30, versus profits of $95.6 million, or 55 cents per share, in the year-ago quarter. The company had 31 percent more shares in the recent quarter than in the second quarter of 2005.

Adjusted for one-time charges mostly related to the acquisition of Hudson United Bancorp, earnings increased to $128.3 million, or 56 cents per share, from $109.1 million in the year-ago quarter.

Lucent experiences severe drop-off
YESTERDAY
Close $2.09
Change +$0.10
52-WEEK
High $3.49
Low $1.99

Lucent Technologies Inc., the biggest US maker of telephone equipment, said third-quarter net income plunged 79 percent as sales to wireless companies waned.

Profit in the period ended June 30 fell to $79 million, or 2 cents a share, from $372 million, or 7 cents, a year earlier. Sales fell 12 percent to $2.05 billion. The figures are in line with the preliminary estimates Lucent released July 10.

Lucent, whose sales have slid for three straight quarters, disclosed plans for a product in North America that will help companies such as Verizon Wireless improve Web access and video downloads. Fourth-quarter sales should be the highest in 2006 ``by a significant margin," chief financial officer John Kritzmacher said. He declined to give specifics.

Revenue was $2.14 billion, down 8.5 percent from the year-ago quarter's $2.34 billion. (Bloomberg)

Thermo Electron earnings drop 20%
YESTERDAY
Close $36.35
Change -$0.28
52-WEEK
High $41.85
Low $27.32

Thermo Electron Corp., the world's biggest maker of medical laboratory instruments, said second-quarter earnings fell 20 percent from a year earlier, when it had gains from the sale of investments.

Net income fell to $47.9 million, or 29 cents a share, from $60.2 million, or 37 cents, a year earlier, also hurt by expenses for stock options. Sales rose 9.2 percent to $713.5 million, the Waltham company said.

Chief executive Marijn Dekkers in May agreed to buy Fisher Scientific International Inc. for $10.6 billion in stock to become the largest supplier of medical laboratory equipment.

In the quarter, life and laboratory science unit sales rose 11 percent, benefiting from the more than $1 billion in acquisitions Dekkers made last year.

Excluding some items, earnings were 42 cents a share. On that basis, profit beat the 40-cent average estimate of analysts surveyed by Thomson. Thomson doesn't provide a breakdown of what's included. (Bloomberg)

Boeing sustains 1st loss since 2003
YESTERDAY
Close $79.90
Change -$3.85
52-WEEK
High $89.58
Low $62.01

Steep costs to settle a government investigation into its contracting and hiring practices and payments for delays to an airborne surveillance system sent Boeing Co. to its first quarterly loss in three years.

Boeing disclosed a $160-million loss, which amounted to 21 cents a share. A year ago earnings were $566 million, or 70 cents a share.

Revenue rose 2 percent to $15.0 billion from $14.7 billion a year ago.

While the $1.07 billion in charges were expected, Wall Street showed disappointment that the aerospace company chose not to write off any of its Justice Department settlement and increased its 2007 outlook less than many anticipated. (AP)

Honda sets another quarterly record
YESTERDAY
Close $32.76
Change +$0.51
52-WEEK
High $37.95
Low $25.00

First-quarter profit at Honda soared nearly 30 percent as a solid reputation for gas mileage lifted US sales that make up half of the Japanese automaker's vehicle sales.

Americans snatched up small car models like the Civic and Fit, boosting Honda's North American sales nearly 9 percent to 456,000 vehicles for the quarter versus the year-ago period.

Net profit grew 29.6 percent to $1.2 billion in the April to June quarter, up from $95.1 million in the year-ago period.

Sales climbed 14.8 percent to $22.3 billion from $19.4 billion a year earlier, as the company chalked up its sixth straight record first-quarter earnings and sales. (AP)
