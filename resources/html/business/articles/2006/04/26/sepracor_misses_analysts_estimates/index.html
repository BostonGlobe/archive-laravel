EARNINGS ROUNDUP

Sepracor misses analysts' estimates

April 26, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $44.43
Change -$3.54
52-WEEK
High $63.75
Low $43.86

Shares of Sepracor Inc., maker of the Lunesta sleeping pill, dropped the most in 2 1/2 years after the company's first-quarter earnings missed analysts' estimates.

Net income was $10.3 million, or 9 cents a share, compared with a loss of $22.6 million, or 22 cents, a year earlier, Marlborough-based Sepracor said. Analysts expected the company to earn 29 cents.

Lunesta sales of $138.1 million were lower than the $145 million estimated by David Webb, an analyst with Stanford Group in Boca Raton, Fla.

Revenue more than doubled to $285.7 million. (Bloomberg)

Acquisition boosts AT&T in 1st quarter
YESTERDAY
Close $25.60
Change +$0.70
52-WEEK
High $28.82
Low $21.75

AT&T Inc., formerly SBC Communications Inc., said its earnings rose 63.3 percent in the first quarter mostly because of its acquisition of AT&T Corp.

It was the first full quarterly report since SBC bought AT&T Corp. in November and adopted its brand to become AT&T Inc.

Net income was $1.445 billion, or 37 cents a share, up from SBC's earnings of $885 million, or 27 cents a share, a year-ago.

Revenue came to $15.8 billion, up 55 percent from SBC's $10.2 billion the previous year. Counting AT&T Corp.'s results last year, revenue declined 5 percent. Revenue figures exclude sales from Cingular Wireless, AT&T Inc.'s joint venture with BellSouth Corp.

Excluding the costs of SBC's acquisition of AT&T and Cingular's 2004 acquisition of AT&T Wireless, earnings were 52 cents a share, beating the average estimate of analysts polled by Thomson Financial by 3 cents. (AP)

Amazon.com's earnings drop 35%

Change -$0.24
52-WEEK
High $50.00
Low $30.60

Amazon.com Inc. said that earnings fell nearly 35 percent in the first quarter, compared to the period last year in which the online retailer had a big one-time gain.

For the three months ended March 31, the Seattle-based company earned $51 million, or 12 cents per share, compared with $78 million, or 18 cents per share, in the same period a year ago.

A year ago, Amazon.com said it recorded a one-time gain of $26 million, or 6 cents per share, because of an accounting change.

Sales increased nearly 20 percent, to $2.28 billion, from $1.9 billion in the first quarter of 2005. The company said sales would have grown 25 percent if not for a $94 million impact from foreign exchange rates. (AP)

Lucent profit hurt by lower gear sales

Close $2.93
Change -$0.04
52-WEEK
High $3.49
Low $2.39

Lucent Technologies Inc. said second-quarter profit fell 32 percent, dragged down by a decline in sales of wireless gear to North American customers.

Net income at Lucent, which is being bought by France's Alcatel SA, fell to $181 million, or 4 cents a share, from $267 million, or 6 cents, a year earlier. Revenue dropped 8.4 percent to $2.14 billion, Murray Hill, N.J.-based Lucent said.

Net income for the January to March period beat the 3-cent estimate of UBS AG's Nikos Theodosopoulos, ranked second among wireline equipment analysts by Institutional Investor magazine.

Lucent said it spent $20 million on stock options during the latest quarter. The company did not provide a per-share cost. (Bloomberg)

Change -$0.87
52-WEEK

Vertex Pharmaceuticals Inc. said its first-quarter loss widened because of higher spending on drug development.

The Cambridge biotechnology company reported a net loss of $50.1 million, or 47 cents a share, compared with a loss of $44.7 million, or 56 cents a share, a year earlier.

Vertex said it still expects to post a 2006 loss of $205 million to $225 million, including stock options expenses of about $34 million and restructuring expense of about $6 million.

Revenue rose 36 percent to $39.1 million, helped by an increase in income from collaborative research and development agreements, including about $8.8 million in milestone payments from Merck & Co. for an experimental cancer drug. (Reuters)

JetBlue blames fuel costs for losses
YESTERDAY
Close $10.64
Change +$1.24
52-WEEK
High $16.85
Low $8.93

JetBlue Airways Corp. posted its second consecutive quarterly loss and outlined a plan that includes deferring delivery of some aircraft, selling others, and trimming costs.

JetBlue said high fuel costs drove it to a first-quarter loss of $32 million, or 18 cents per share, versus a year-ago profit of $6 million, or 4 cents per share. Revenue rose 31 percent to $490 million from $373 million a year ago.

The results topped Wall Street forecast for a wider loss of 20 cents per share on stronger revenue of $497.9 million.

The recovery plan includes focusing on medium- and short-haul flights, revamping fare structures and slowing capacity growth. The company said its initiatives should boost revenue by about $35 million and yield cost savings of another $35 million this year. (AP)

Close $50.06
Change -$1.58
52-WEEK
High $56.71
Low $15.38

Hologic Inc., a provider of diagnostic and digital imaging systems directed towards women's health, saw an 85 percent rise in net income during the second quarter, reflecting the increase in product sales of Selenia digital mammography systems.

The Bedford company said it earned $11.2 million or 24 cents a share in the latest quarter versus $6.0 million or 13 cents in the year-ago period.

Share earnings information for 2005 has been restated to reflect Hologic's 2-for-1 stock split effected Nov. 30, 2005.

Second-quarter revenue totaled $101 million, a 46 percent increase when compared to revenue of $69.2 million in the second quarter of fiscal 2005. (Dow Jones)
