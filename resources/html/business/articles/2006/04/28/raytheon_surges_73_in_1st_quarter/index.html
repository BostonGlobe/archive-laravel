EARNINGS ROUNDUP

Raytheon surges 73% in 1st quarter

April 28, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $46.02
Change +$0.84
52-WEEK
High $46.85
Low $35.96

Raytheon Co. said first-quarter earnings surged 73 percent as weapons sales and improved results at its business jet unit fueled the biggest profit gain in more than five years.

Net income rose to $287 million, or 64 cents a share, from $166 million, or 36 cents, a year earlier. Sales gained 4.2 percent to $5.15 billion, the Waltham company said. Analysts expected earnings of 53 cents a share.

Also yesterday, Moody's Investors Service upgraded the firm's long-term senior debt ratings to Baa2 from Baa3 and its short-term rating to Prime-2 from Prime-3, citing the company's improved financial profile. (Bloomberg)

Program orders lift Microsoft profit
YESTERDAY
Close $27.25
Change +$0.15
52-WEEK
High $28.38
Low $24.25

Microsoft Corp. said third-quarter profit rose 16 percent on orders for database programs. Earnings this year may miss some analysts' estimates.

Net income increased to $3 billion, or 29 cents a share, from $2.56 billion, or 23 cents, a year earlier, the Redmond, Wash., company said. Sales in the quarter ended March 31 rose 13 percent to $10.9 billion.

Excluding some legal costs, profit would have been 31 cents a share in the third quarter. Goldman, Sachs & Co.'s Rick Sherlund, the top-rated software analyst by Institutional Investor magazine, predicted third-quarter profit of 33 cents a share and sales of $11 billion.

The report raised concern that Microsoft may be spending too much as chief executive Steve Ballmer adds new products to spur growth and compete with Google Inc.

For the current fourth quarter ending June 30, Microsoft said it expects to earn 30 cents per share, below the 34 cents per share that analysts had been predicting. (Globe wires)

Close $62.42
Change -$0.68
52-WEEK
High $65.96
Low $52.78

Exxon Mobil Corp., the world's biggest oil company, said first-quarter profit climbed 6.9 percent because of record prices and the first production increase in a year and a half.

Net income rose to $8.4 billion, or $1.37 a share, from $7.86 billion, or $1.22, a year earlier, Irving, Texas-based Exxon Mobil said. Per-share profit was 10 cents lower than analysts' estimates. Sales climbed 8.4 percent to $89 billion.

Profit fell short of expectations because Exxon Mobil's effective income tax rate jumped to 46 percent from 39 percent, shaving $1.03 billion in net income, said Kenneth Carroll, an analyst at Johnson Rice & Co. in New Orleans. (Bloomberg)

Change +$1.46
52-WEEK
High $54.28

First Marblehead Corp. reported first-quarter profit rose 25 percent as the Boston-based private college student loan servies provider realized higher fees and residuals.

Quarterly profit rose to $59.2 million, or 93 cents per share, from $47.4 million, or 71 cents per share, in the year-ago period. Total service revenue rose to $149.2 million from $19.5 million in the year-ago period, driven by higher advisory fees and residuals from new securitizations.

Results surpassed Wall Street estimates for earnings of 89 cents per share. (AP)

Loss narrows at Millennium

Close $9.49
Change -$0.03
52-WEEK
High $11.25
Low $7.63

Millennium Pharmaceuticals Inc., a developer of treatments for cancer and heart conditions, said that its first-quarter loss narrowed 43 percent as total costs fell.

The company reported a net loss of $20.8 million, or 7 cents per share, from $36.4 million, or 12 cents per share, a year ago. Excluding one-time charges and stock option expenses, the company reported a loss of 3 cents per share for the latest quarter.

Revenue fell 1 percent to $122.5 million from $123.7 million last year, despite a 19 percent rise in US sales of cancer treatment Velcade to $53.4 million, because of lower revenue from collaborations.

Analysts expected a loss per share of 3 cents on revenue of $116.5 million. Estimates exclude stock option expenses. (AP)

PerkinElmer Inc.'s first-quarter net income rose to $23.6 million, or 18 cents a share, from $19.8 million, or 15 cents a share, a year ago.

Revenue for the Boston health sciences firm was $355.5 million, down slightly from last year's $358.2 million.

Excluding one-time charges, PerkinElmer would have reported earnings of 22 cents a share, matching projections from analysts. (Dow Jones)
