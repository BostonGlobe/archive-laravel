BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Acquisition to give Hologic breast cancer technology

April 25, 2006

THE REGION
Hologic Inc., which makes medical imaging equipment, agreed to buy R2 Technology Inc. for $220 million in stock to gain technology to detect breast cancer. Closely held R2 Technology, of Sunnyvale, Calif., had $45 million in 2005 revenue, Bedford-based Hologic said. The acquisition will add to profit next year, it added. The company last week said it agreed to buy Suros Surgical Systems Inc. for $240 million to gain products used in biopsies. Hologic shares gained 43 cents to $51.64; the stock has gained 36 percent this year. The deal was disclosed after markets closed. (Bloomberg)

Credit card will serve as a free pass to museums
Anyone with an MBNA credit card will be admitted free to museums in the Northeast in May, Bank of America Corp. said. Its Museums on Us program already lets holders of its own debit or credit cards to visit 56 cultural institutions at no charge in May; they can bring a guest for free, too. The bank expanded the program after acquiring MBNA Corp. this year. Participating museums include the Museum of Fine Arts, Museum of Science, and Peabody Essex Museum. (Sasha Talcott)

Boston law firm makes first foray into West Coast
Goodwin Procter LLP has opened offices in Los Angeles and San Francisco -- the first time the Boston law firm has had a West Coast presence. It's designed to bolster the 650-lawyer firm's practice areas in real estate capital markets and complex litigation, and to expand its work in technology, life sciences, private equity, and intellectual property, managing partner Regina M. Pisa said. Initially, the Los Angeles office will be staffed with four partners, the San Francisco office with seven. Most are new hires. Both offices will roughly double in size within a month. Goodwin, with 120 lawyers in New York and 85 in Washington, hopes to open a Silicon Valley office, too. (Sacha Pfeiffer)

EMC is launching unit for security consulting
EMC Corp., the world's biggest maker of data-storage computers and software, is starting a security consulting unit. The Hopkinton company will hire 65 consultants this year, lifting its security unit to about 90 workers, security chief Dennis Hoffman said. EMC will spend as much as 10 percent of its $1.2 billion research and development budget on creating new programs, he said. Last year, 157 breaches of US computer systems may have affected 58 million people, according to ChoicePoint Inc. EMC on Feb. 27 bought Authentica, of Lexington, whose software controls employees' access to documents and e-mail. (Bloomberg)

Seniors advocacy group offers three mutual funds
AARP, the seniors advocacy group, is selling three mutual funds -- conservative, moderate, and aggressive -- geared toward retirement that are designed to simplify investment choices. Tewksbury-based AARP Financial, established last year, sells investment products for people 50 and older, including the new mutual funds; life, home, and auto insurance; credit cards; and motorcycle insurance. The funds have no sales fees. Annual operating expenses will have a 0.50 percent ratio. Expense ratios for index funds generally range from 0.10 percent to 1.8 percent. (AP)

Bedford company acquires PassMark for $48.2m
RSA Security Inc., a Bedford provider of Internet security software, acquired privately held PassMark Security Inc., of Menlo Park, Calif., in a cash-and-stock deal worth $48.2 million. PassMark, which develops Internet antifraud and authentication systems, was cofounded by Bill Harris, former chief executive of Intuit and PayPal. RSA Security expects the buy to add $4 million to $5 million in revenue in 2006 and $10 million to $15 million in 2007. (AP)

Providence firm to test a mobile TV service
A Providence company is joining with satellite service provider SES Americom to launch a trial mobile TV and entertainment service, supported by advertising and subscriptions, for hand-helds and cellphones. Aloha Partners LP said it is forming a subsidiary, Hwire, to test the network. It will probably have 30 to 40 television, music, interactive, and e-commerce channels. ''We will be primarily including content from the major broadcast cable and satellite television networks," said Scott Wills, Hwire president. The channels will include local content. Aloha is the largest owner of 700 MHz spectrum in the United States. SES Americom, of Princeton, N.J., is the largest supplier of satellite services. (AP)
