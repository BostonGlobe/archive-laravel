Demand helps raise Novartis net 31%

April 25, 2006
YESTERDAY

Change +$0.15
52-WEEK
High $57.60
Low $46.67

Novartis AG, Europe's third-largest drug maker, said first-quarter profit rose 31 percent, helped by demand for the hypertension treatment Diovan and a gain from the sale of its nutrition unit.

Net income rose to $1.95 billion, or 83 cents a share, from $1.48 billion, or 63 cents, a year earlier, Basel, Switzerland-based Novartis said. Sales increased 13 percent to $8.3 billion, missing analysts' forecasts.

Switzerland's biggest drug maker said sales climbed to $8.3 billion from $7.3 billion.

Analysts surveyed by Bloomberg had forecast revenue of $8.54 billion and net income of $1.7 billion. (Bloomberg)

Cognex Corp. said that first-quarter profit rose to $8.8 million, or 18 cents a share, from $5.3 million, or 11 cents a share, a year ago.

Sales for the three months ended April 2 advanced to $59 million from $43.2 million in last year's first quarter.

Analysts' average estimate was for 19 cents a share on sales of $61.4 million, according to Thomson First Call.

For the second quarter, the Natick maker of vision systems forecast earnings of 20 cents to 22 cents a share, including stock option expenses. The analysts' estimate stands at 23 cents.

Cognex also expects second-quarter revenue of $62 million to $64 million. (Dow Jones)

Skyworks Solutions Inc., a maker of chips and integrated circuits for mobile phones, said its fiscal second-quarter profit dropped by nearly 26 percent, due to a variety of expenses, but results beat Wall Street expectations.

For the quarter ended March 31, net income fell to $926,000, from $1.2 million a year earlier. On a per-share basis, earnings were flat at a penny per share.

Excluding one-time expenses for stock options, amortization, and other costs, net income was $4.6 million, or 3 cents a share in the latest period.

Revenue for the quarter was $185.2 million, down 3 percent from $190.5 million.

Results beat analysts' consensus estimates, which pegged earnings at 2 cents a share on revenue of $180.98 million. (AP)

Hasbro Inc., the nation's second biggest toymaker, reported a wider loss for the first quarter, and said the second quarter would be challenging as the company braces for a falloff in ''Star Wars" merchandise.

The first-quarter loss of $4.9 million, or 3 cents a share, for the three months ended April 2, was attributed largely to foreign currency fluctuation and new accounting rules for stock options.

In the same quarter last year, Hasbro lost $3.7 million, or 2 cents a share.

Expenses related to stock-based compensation reduced results by 2 cents a share in the latest quarter.

If similar expenses had been included a year ago, the loss for that period would have been 4 cents a share.

Revenue rose to $468.2 million from $454.9 million with a strong performance from Hasbro's North American operations.

Currency fluctuations reduced revenue by about $9 million in the first quarter.

Analysts expected a loss of 1 cent a share, including stock options expenses, on revenue of $430 million. (AP)

Vicor Corp.'s first-quarter net income rose to $3.08 million from $39,000 a year earlier, helped by an 11 percent increase in revenue.

The Andover maker of modular power components said earnings increased to 7 cents a share from less than a penny in the year-ago period.

During the latest quarter, the company said it accrued $207,000 for incentives to be paid after the end of 2006 and recorded $178,000 in stock option expenses.

Analysts expected first-quarter earnings of 5 cents a share.

Revenue rose to $47.9 million from $43.2 million a year ago. (Dow Jones)

American Express Co., which specializes in travel services and credit cards, said its profit fell in the first quarter, reflecting the spinoff last year of its Ameriprise Financial Inc. advisory unit.

Excluding the Ameriprise divestiture, however, earnings were up sharply on strength in both domestic and foreign card operations.

Net income in the January-March period was $873, or 69 cents a share, down from $946 million, or 75 cents a share, a year earlier.

Revenue for New York-based American Express for the quarter was $6.33 billion, up 12 percent from $5.64 billion a year earlier.

Analysts had projected first-quarter earnings at 69 cents a share on slightly higher income of $6.35 billion. (AP)
