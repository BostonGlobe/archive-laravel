EARNINGS ROUNDUP

Boston Scientific profit drops 7%

April 19, 2006
YESTERDAY
Close $21.74
Change +$0.26
52-WEEK
High $31.32
Low $20.57

Boston Scientific Corp. said first-quarter profit fell 7 percent on declining sales of its top-selling heart stent heading into the medical device maker's acquisition of Guidant Corp., which could be completed as soon as this week.

Boston Scientific reported earnings for the January-March period of $332 million, or 40 cents per share, compared with a profit of $358 million, or 42 cents per share, in the same quarter a year earlier. Sales edged upward to $1.62 billion from $1.6 billion, narrowly beating the $1.59 billion figure forecast by analysts.

The profit figures include the impact of new accounting rules that require companies to record costs for stock-based compensation. Boston Scientific reported a $22 million stock-compensation charge, amounting to 3 cents per share.

Analysts forecast earnings of 44 cents per share, on average, including stock-based compensation. That consensus estimate did not include the effects of a $27 million charge, or 3 cents per share, that Boston Scientific reported for prematurely ending a gene therapy clinical trial. (AP)

State Street Corp. posted a 29 percent jump in first-quarter profit, as higher servicing and investment management fees helped drive revenue.

Net income came to $292 million, or 87 cents a share, for the latest quarter, compared with $226 million, or 67 cents, a year earlier, the Boston firm reported.

Analysts had expected quarterly earnings of 77 cents a share.

Revenue for the first quarter totaled $1.5 billion, up 16 percent from the $1.3 billion of a year earlier. (Dow Jones/AP)

IBM gains 22% following job cuts

Close $83.31
Change +$1.67
52-WEEK
High $89.94
Low $71.85

International Business Machines Corp. said first-quarter profit gained 22 percent after job cuts.

Net income increased to $1.71 billion, or $1.08 a share, from $1.4 billion, or 84 cents, a year earlier, Armonk, N.Y.-based IBM said. Revenue fell 9.8 percent to $20.7 billion, reflecting the sale of IBM's personal computer unit.

Chief executive Samuel Palmisano failed to revive sales growth after selling the money-losing personal computer business, even as he increased production of video-game chips and expanded units in countries with fast-growing economies, such as India and China.

He lowered spending partly by trimming 14,500 jobs.

Analysts expected profit of $1.05 and sales of $20.7 billion. (Bloomberg)

52-WEEK
High $80.53

Financial services firm Merrill Lynch & Co. posted sharply lower first-quarter earnings after taking a hefty charge for stock-based compensation, but record revenue from investment activity helped the results beat Wall Street estimates.

For the quarter ended March 31, Merrill's earnings after preferred dividends fell 64 percent to $432 million, or 44 cents per share, which included a $1.2 billion expense from changing how it accounts for stock options paid as compensation. It earned $1.21 billion, or $1.21 per share, a year earlier.

Analysts were expecting earnings of 32 cents per share, including the stock-option expense.

Revenue rose to $7.96 billion for the quarter, up 28 percent from $6.23 billion last year amid an industrywide pickup in investment banking activity. Analysts were expecting Merrill to report $7.33 billion in revenue. (AP)

Sovereign Bancorp Inc. said its first-quarter earnings fell 3 percent on charges and a decline in net interest income.

The Philadelphia company's first-quarter profit dropped to $141.4 million from $146.2 million. Earnings per share remained flat at 38 cents as a lower number of shares outstanding affected results in the latest quarter.

Excluding proxy and related professional fee charges, and merger-related and integration cost reversals, the company's earnings were 43 cents per share, matching analysts' consensus estimate.

Net interest income declined 1 percent to $404 million, from $407.3 million last year, reflecting reclassified prepayment and late fees due to a regulatory change.

Noninterest income increased to $134.3 million from 132.2 million, as consumer and commercial banking fees climbed 10 percent from a year ago, the company said. (AP)

Stock option costs hurt Yahoo net

Close $31.30
Change +$0.33
52-WEEK
High $43.66

Yahoo Inc. said quarterly profit declined amid higher stock option expenses, as strength in online display advertising was partly offset by market share losses in paid search ads.

The Sunnyvale, Calif.-based company said net income for the first quarter fell to $160 million, or 11 cents per diluted share, from $204.6 million, or 14 cents per diluted share, a year earlier. Gross revenue rose 34 percent to $1.57 billion.

Excluding one-time items tied to the impact of stock-based compensation costs, Yahoo reported adjusted net income of $231 million, or 15 cents per diluted share, in the latest quarter.

The Wall Street consensus estimate, excluding one-time items, was 16 cents per share. Including items, analysts predicted a consensus net profit of 11 cents a share. (Reuters)

Teradyne Inc. reported a fiscal first-quarter profit as sales climbed 73 percent.

Analysts expected earnings of 19 cents per share. (Bloomberg)
