MUSICAL PROGRESSION: Talk to Molly Jewell on the phone and the 24-year-old Duxbury native has the voice of a young girl, high in tone and a giggly at times. Listen to Jewell sing, and it’s acoustic night and day, as she has a deeper, more soulful, silky voice that has already drawn comparisons to Joni Mitchell, one of her inspirations.

“I do get that a lot, that I don’t sound the same,” she said on the phone from Nashville, where she is pursuing a musical career that includes this month’s release of her first album, “Everybody Just Wants Something.” “I guess my singing voice is different.”

Jewell, who attended Vanderbilt University’s Blair School of Music, said she knew from an early age she’d be in the musical field, “always dancing around the house, singing to my Disney movies,” she said with a laugh.

She had a huge genetic boost: Her mother, Debby, was a singer with the opera company in Tulsa, Okla., and directs the children’s choir at a church in Duxbury, where she also gives voice lessons.

Molly’s brother Billy, 18, is a student at Belmont University in Nashville, and lives close to his sister. Her dad, Robert, who admits to being tone deaf, and mother would take Molly to gigs on the South Shore, including a regular one at the Vine in Plymouth on Wednesday nights.

“The fact that our son and daughter are so close to each other down there, with Billy having his big sister around, makes Mom and Dad feel a whole lot better,” Robert Jewell said.

Molly Jewell counts as her inspirations Billie Holiday “for her voice and emotion,” as well as Carole King and Joni Mitchell, and especially Aimee Mann, because “she’s taken control of her entire career, she’s a great female role model, I really admire her.”

Jewell plays piano and writes her own music, which her producer, David Malloy, described as “all little stories, little movies. Molly is not someone who writes for radio; she writes the story she’s feeling.”

Malloy, who has produced stars such as Dolly Parton, Reba McEntire, Kenny Rogers, and Mindy McCready, first saw Jewell at Nashville Hootenanny, which he produced, and knew there was something there.

“Over a few more months, I heard more songs and really thought she was special, unique,” he said. “Her voice has a real positive, charismatic feel.”

Her music is gritty, as in the album’s title song, “Everyone Just Wants Something,” with lyrics that include “when you’re down and out, they got you figured out, and you can count on it doing you in, ’cause everyone just wants something from you . . . then they’re through.” And on “Poor Happy People,” she sings, “You look like someone I could love, You look like someone who could hurt me, too.”

Jewell will start a tour soon, in the South and then over to Germany, which she said “is pretty much my first out-of-town stuff. I’m excited about it.”

Her parents have always been supportive of her efforts, Jewell said, adding, “Dad’s always there to help me, to bounce ideas and questions off. He’s not a musician, but I really value his advice.”

“You think that music is a tough field, it can be a tough life, very competitive, but our kids are so happy doing it,” Robert Jewell said. “It’s like they’re born to it, so you can’t discourage them from going out there and trying.”

And she’s got the goods, Malloy said, to be the next big thing.

“She’s the kind of artist that gets Grammy nominations,” he said. “She’s a great songwriter, singer, and musician. She’s the real deal.”

For more information, visit www.mollyjewell.com. She can be seen performing “Everyone Just Wants Something” on YouTube.

BUSINESS BRIEFS:Susana Griffin (inset) of Marshfield was appointed regional lending manager for Bank of Canton, working from its Marshfield office while handling real estate mortgages throughout the South Shore. Also at Bank of Canton, Sarah Tesorero of Dedham was promoted to marketing manager. She had previously worked as operations and marketing manager for the Make-A-Wish Foundation of Rhode Island, and as assistant in major gifts at Wheaton College in Norton.

Randolph-based ETS International, a ground transportation company, named Jonathan O. Ryan of Weymouth as a dispatch supervisor. He was previously lead dispatcher for Carey Limousine in Boston for 18 years.

Meg Parker of Hingham has joined the senior team at Hollywood Public Relations of Hingham. She came to the firm from Puma, where she spent six years as senior manager for international public relations. At her new post, Parker provides support for the Summer Infant and Samsonite brands, as well as generating new business.

ASTRONAUT LANDING IN CANTON: Randolph native and NASA astronaut Scott A. Tingle (inset) was among the speakers at the Blue Hills Regional Technical School’s commencement for its class of 2012 last week in Canton.

Tingle, a 1983 graduate of the school, was one of a group of 14 aviators to graduate last year from NASA’s astronaut training program.

As a Navy officer, Tingle flew dozens of missions in Iraq and Afghanistan before entering training to become an astronaut.
