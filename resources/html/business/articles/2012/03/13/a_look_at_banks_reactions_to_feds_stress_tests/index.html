A look at banks' reactions to Fed's stress tests

By The Associated Press
March 13, 2012

The Federal Reserve said Tuesday that 15 of the 19 major banks it surveyed passed a stress test.

Some of the 15 announced they will buy back some of their stock, and several said they will pay shareholders higher dividends. Citigroup Inc. and Ally Financial Inc. -- two of the four that failed to show they have the capital to survive another major downturn -- said they're still talking with federal officials.

Results of the tests were released after markets closed.

Here are details on reactions from banks that failed the tests, followed by after-hours share price movement.

-- Ally Financial said it continues talks with regulators and will submit a revised capital plan soon. It said the Federal Reserve has allowed it to proceed with paying preferred dividends. Ally's shares fell 16 cents to $22.30.

-- Citigroup said it plans to engage further with the Federal Reserve to understand the new stress loss models. Its shares fell $1.20 to $35.25.

-- SunTrust Banks Inc. also failed the Fed's tests. Its shares dropped $1.08 to $21.50.

-- MetLife Inc., the fourth bank that failed the tests, said the Fed objects to its plan to buy back $2 billion in stock and increase its dividend to $1.10 per share from 74 cents. The company says it's on track to cease being a bank holding company by the end of the second quarter. Its shares fell $1.50 to $37.96.

Here are details on reactions from banks that passed the tests, followed by after-hours share price movement.

-- Fifth Third Bancorp declared a quarterly cash dividend on its "Series G" stock of $531.25 per share, which equates to about $2.13 for each depositary share. Its shares fell 26 cents to $13.90.

-- Wells Fargo amp; Co. said it will nearly double its quarterly dividend to 22 cents. Wells shares rose 2 cents to $33.35. -- BBT Corp. said it will raise its quarterly dividend by 25 percent, to 20 cents from 16 cents. Its shares rose 10 cents to $30.50. -- JPMorgan Chase said it is increasing its quarterly dividend to 30 cents per share from 25 cents. Its shares fell 34 cents to $43.05. -- U.S. Bancorp said it will boost its annual dividend by 56 percent to 78 cents per share and repurchase up to 100 million of its shares. The stock lost a penny, failing to $31. -- KeyCorp said it might boost its dividend and will buy back up to $344 million in its stock. Its shares fell 8 cents to $8.18. -- American Express Co. plans to buy back up to $5 billion of its stock and boost its dividend to 20 cents per share from 18 cents. Its shares rose a nickel to $54.30. -- Discover Financial Services said it plans to buy back up to $2 billion in stock over the next two years. The shares rose 25 cents to $32.27. -- Regions Financial Corp. said it will sell $900 million in stock to repay some of the money it received as part of the 2008 bank bailout. Its shares rose 23 cents to $6.01 after hours. -- Goldman Sachs Group Inc. said it will buy back stock and may increase its quarterly dividend. Its shares slipped 4 cents to $124.50. -- Bank of New York Mellon Corp., known as BNY Mellon, will buy back up to $1.16 billion of its stock and will continue its quarterly dividend payment of 13 cents per share. Its shares fell 11 cents after hours to $23.17. -- PNC Financial Services Group Inc. said it will consider raising the dividend for its second quarter and buying back stock under its existing stock repurchase program. The shares fell 4 cents to $61.90. -- Morgan Stanley it will potentially buy an additional 14 percent of Morgan Stanley Smith Barney and continue paying dividends. Its shares rose a penny to $18.94. -- Bank of America Corp. shares were unchanged at $8.49. -- State Street Corp. shares fell 16 cents to $43.71.
