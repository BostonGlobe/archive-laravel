GE plant's neighbors sue over pollution

The owners of two businesses near the former General Electric Co. plant in Pittsfield sued GE and the government, seeking to compel them to remove all PCBs from their properties and pay them damages. In the federal lawsuit, filed in Springfield, Moldmaster Engineering and Vincent and Cheryl Stracuzzi claim the Environmental Protection Agency forced them to accept an incomplete cleanup of their land. GE used PCBs, probable carcinogens, in the manufacture of transformers from the 1930s until 1977. Four years ago, GE agreed to clean the plant site, two miles of the Housatonic River, and more than 100 residential properties, parks, and schools. Lawyer Cristobal Bonifaz said the EPA and GE concealed the extent of the contamination until three years after a judge approved the cleanup plan. Calls to the defendants were not immediately returned. (AP)

Halliburton takes $200m charge in Brazil

Halliburton Co. will take an unexpected $200 million charge in the second quarter on a project in Brazil, pushing after-tax charges on the $2.5 billion project to about $500 million since it began in 2000. The loss on the Barracuda-Caratinga project, 46 cents per share, emerged upon a detailed review that revealed higher cost estimates and schedule delays, the company said. The project involves conversion of two supertankers into production, storage, and offloading vessels for deepwater oil fields. Halliburton said earlier this year it didn't expect any charges beyond a $62 million, or 14 cents per share, charge taken in the first quarter. (AP)

Lexus first in dependability survey

Toyota Motor Corp. remains the automaker with the most dependable vehicles, led by its Lexus luxury brand, though Detroit's Big Three manufacturers all showed improvement in the past year, according to a J.D. Power and Associates study. For the 10th year, Lexus was the highest-ranking brand, followed by General Motors Corp.'s Buick, Nissan Motor Co.'s Infiniti, Ford Motor Co.'s Lincoln, and GM's revamped Cadillac. GM was the only domestic automaker to score better than the industry average of 269 problems per 100 vehicles. Last year's average was 273 per 100 vehicles. This year, GM was tops among the Big Three with 262 problems per 100 vehicles, two fewer than last year. Ford (275) improved by 12 problems; DaimlerChrysler (302) was better by nine. The bottom five were Volkswagen (386 problems per 100 cars), Isuzu (393), Daewoo (411), Kia (432), and Land Rover (472). (AP)

No verdict yet in Adelphia trial

Jurors in the fraud trial of Adelphia Communications Corp. founder John Rigas and three other former executives ended a second day of deliberations yesterday without a verdict. The jury has asked for more than 40 exhibits and testimony readbacks that relate to several charges and all defendants. Rigas, sons Timothy and Michael, and former assistant treasurer Michael Mulcahey are accused of conspiring to hide over $2 billion in Adelphia debt and raiding the firm for personal gain. (AP)
