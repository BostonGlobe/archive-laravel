BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Momenta Pharmaceuticals holds IPO

June 22, 2004

Momenta Pharmaceuticals Inc., which is developing treatments for blood clots, raised $34.8 million in an initial public offering to fund the development of its medicine. Cambridge-based Momenta sold 5.35 million shares at $6.50 each, compared with a price range of $13 to $15 a share indicated in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing. SG Cowen Securities Corp. and Banc of America Securities LLC managed the IPO, and will share fees of as much as $2.4 million with CIBC World Markets and ThinkEquity Partners LLC. Momenta is developing generic drugs to treat deep vein thrombosis and coronary syndromes. The company had a loss of $2.6 million in the three months ended March 31, compared with a loss of $1.5 million in year-earlier period. After the IPO, Polaris Venture Partners III LP will have a 20 percent stake in Momenta and Atlas Venture will have a 15 percent shareholding. Chief executive Alan Crane will have a 4.4 percent stake. Momenta will be listed on the Nasdaq Stock Market with the symbol MNTA. (Bloomberg)

S&P cites UMass Memorial debt, losses

Standard & Poor's investment rating service issued a negative outlook on UMass Memorial Health Care Inc.'s outstanding debt because of "continued operating difficulties and erratic profitability." While UMass Memorial Medical Center in Worcester and its parent organization earned an $8 million profit on operations last year, the system as a whole, including affiliates, lost $8.3 million. Standard & Poor's analysts said that losses in the organization's physicians' group are of particular concern. The doctors lost $13 million last year, $18 million in 2002, and $17 million in 2001. Standard & Poor's reaffirmed its BBB rating on UMass's bonds, but said failure to show a profit by 2005 is likely to result in a lower rating. (Liz Kowalczyk)

MIT magazine names editor in chief

MIT's Technology Review magazine has tapped Jason Pontin, 37, as its new editor in chief. He has been editor in chief of Acumen Journal and is the former editorial director of Red Herring magazine. He succeeds Robert Buderi, who returned to an editor-at-large position this spring and is writing his third book. At Technology Review, Pontin will oversee all editorial aspects of the company's strategy, including the magazine's international distribution and the Emerging Technologies Conference at MIT. (Robert Weisman)

IMlogic links with AOL for messaging

Software maker IMlogic Inc., of Waltham, said it has struck a deal to collaborate with America Online Inc. to provide instant messaging support services to AOL's business and other enterprise customers. The parties did not disclose financial terms. Under the partnership, IMlogic will provide privacy and network infrastructure protection to customers that use AOL's AIM Enterprise Gateway software. IMlogic will help those companies switch to its IM Manager software program and offer them free licenses, the companies said. (Robert Weisman)

Bank of America to settle for $63.8m

Bank of America Corp., the second-biggest US bank, agreed to pay Advanta Corp. $63.8 million to resolve litigation with Bank of America's FleetBoston Financial Corp. unit over the acquisition of a credit card subsidiary. The payment reimburses Advanta, which was ordered by a Delaware judge to pay FleetBoston $63.7 million earlier this year to compensate the Bostonbank for overpaying in a 1998 acquisition of Advanta's credit card business. The settlement also resolves tax disputes between the two companies, Advanta officials said. The settlement clears away litigation left over from Bank of America's $47 billion acquisition of Fleet in April. The buyout created the number two US bank by assets, behind Citigroup Inc. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION
Jackson Hewitt IPO raises $637.5m

Jackson Hewitt Tax Service Inc., the second-largest US tax preparation service, sold $637.5 million of shares in an initial public offering. Jackson Hewitt sold 37.5 million shares at $17 each, compared with a price range of $18 to $20 a share indicated in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing. The company won't receive any of the proceeds from the IPO as its parent, Cendant Corp., the real estate and travel company, sold the Jackson Hewitt shares bought in the IPO. Cendant no longer has a stake in Jackson Hewitt. In the three months to March 31 Jackson Hewitt had net income of $63.9 million on net revenue of $389.6 million, compared with net income of $25.8 million on net revenue of $178.9 million in the year-earlier period. Jackson Hewitt will trade on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol JTX. Goldman Sachs Group Inc. and J.P. Morgan Chase & Co. managed the IPO. Fees were not disclosed. (Bloomberg)

Lay's lawyer says no indictment coming

Enron Corp.'s founder and former chairman, Kenneth Lay, will never be indicted on charges stemming from the company's collapse, despite recent speculation to the contrary, his lawyer said. "Ken Lay didn't do a crime," Michael Ramsey said. "I don't believe Ken Lay will be indicted at all, ever." Still, Ramsey said published reports over the weekend that his client would be indicted within weeks prompted him to request an update from federal prosecutors. He hopes to meet with them this week or next week. Justice Department spokesman Mark Corallo declined to comment. Speculation about possible charges against Lay has intensified since former Enron chief financial officer Andrew Fastow pleaded guilty in January to two counts of conspiracy and became the most highly placed former Enron executive to cooperate with the government. (AP)

Gasoline prices drop to seven-week low

The average retail price of gasoline fell for the fourth week in a row to $1.937 per gallon, its lowest level since May 3, the government reported. The nationwide pump price for regular unleaded gasoline dropped by 4.8 cents, but prices still remain 44.1 cents above year-ago levels, according to the Energy Department's statistical arm, the Energy Information Administration. The major factors underpinning prices are strong demand, tight refining capacity, and expensive crude oil. Crude futures on the New York Mercantile Exchange fell $1.12 to settle at $37.63 a barrel. Oil prices have trended lower since OPEC said this month it would increase its production quota. However, fears about possible supply disruptions in the Middle East have added a premium of at least $5 per barrel, analysts say. (AP)

THE WORLD
Ex-Vivendi chief arrested in Paris

Jean-Marie Messier, former chief executive of Vivendi Universal, presented himself voluntarily at fraud police headquarters in Paris and was taken into custody on charges of insider trading and stock manipulation, almost two years after being ousted from the top job at the media and telecom group. Authorities are probing a massive share buyback in which Vivendi allegedly spent over $1.2 billion to prop up its own share price in the weeks following the Sept. 11 terror attacks. Messier and his top team are suspected of buying back Vivendi shares well above the authorized volumes even while the company was presenting its financial results -- a practice strictly forbidden by stock market rules. Messier could remain in custody for up to 48 hours before a decision is made on whether to place him under formal investigation. (AP)
