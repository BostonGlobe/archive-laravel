EPA enhances River's Edge development

By Kathy McCabe  |  June 20, 2004

A $400,000 federal grant will help turn dirty industrial land along the Malden River into a waterfront park at River's Edge, a 200-acre commercial-residential development proposed for Everett, Malden, and Medford.

The Environmental Protection Agency awarded the so-called brownfields grant last week to the Mystic Valley Development Commission, which is overseeing development of River's Edge. In addition to commercial space and housing, the development also includes construction of the 50-acre Malden River Park.

The grant will be used to clean up 6 acres of industrial land in Medford. The money will be combined with private funding from Preotle, Lane & Associates, the developer of River's Edge. The total cost to clean up the site, formerly used by a construction materials recycler and a trailer and box-storage company, is $1.4 million.

Peter Hollands, project manager for River's Edge, said the site contains low levels of chemical and petroleum residue. ''The parcels aren't significantly contaminated," Hollands said. ''We were happy about the relatively low levels of contamination found there. They're not enough to [preclude] development of the park."

The cleanup is expected to begin next spring. The land will be capped with 3 feet of clean fill and topped with grass and landscaping, Hollands said.

Danvers workingto protect its vistas Danvers will take a historic step toward better land use planning Wednesday, when town and state officials meet with staff from the Essex National Heritage Commission in Salem to discuss ''heritage landscapes."

''We're going to brainstorm about what [landscapes] make Danvers special, " said Susan Fletcher, the town's assistant director of planning and development. ''We're doing this with a larger goal of providing protection to these areas, or vistas, that can lead to better land management."

Heritages landscapes are geographic areas that make a community unique. Mill buildings, scenic roads, and farmland are among the special spots.

A good example in Danvers is a stretch of Sylvan Street near Mill Pond, which often reflects the bandstand and the town library, Fletcher said.

People are asked to attend and discuss the landscapes they feel are important to the town's character. Natural, historic, and cultural sites will be considered. Once identified, a consulting team will evaluate the sites and make recommendations for their protection, Fletcher said.

The meeting will begin at 7 p.m. in the Carriage House at Endicott Park.

Home Depot opensnew Danvers storeThe Home Depot celebrated the grand opening of its second store in Danvers last week. The home improvement chain first opened on Route 1 in Danvers 12 years ago. The town is the second Massachusetts community to have two Home Depots, a spokeswoman said.

The new store behind Liberty Tree Mall includes an outdoor garden center, tool rental center, and Design Place, an expanded home decor department. At the official board-cutting ceremony last week, the company made a $3,000 donation to the town, which will be split evenly among the Danvers High School band, Danvers Little League, and DARE, a drug education program run by the Police Department.

Conference to focuson small businesses The Enterprise Center at Salem State College on Tuesday will host the third annual Small Business Expo, a day of networking that is expected to draw more than 100 small-business owners from across the North Shore.

The free event will run from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. in the O'Keefe Center, the college athletic complex on Loring Avenue. The expo is sponsored by the Enterprise Center and chambers of commerce in Beverly, Lynn, Marblehead, Peabody, and Salem.

Networking is key to the day's programming, said Christine Sullivan, executive director of the Enterprise Center.

''The expo showcases the wide range and diversity of our growing small-business community and provides everyone with an opportunity to generate leads that turn into sales," she said.

Banks, hospitals, hotels, technology, and business consulting firms are among the 100 exhibitors. Officials of the Small Business Administration and the Department of Labor also will be on hand to answer questions about government loan programs and workplace policies. A networking reception from 4 p.m. to 6 p.m. also is open to the public. More information is available at www.enterprisectr.org.
