JetBlue Airways said it plans to expand its service from Logan International Airport to a fourth city in Florida in October. The low-fare airline wants to begin service to Fort Myers, with three daily round trips. It will offer an introductory fare of $69 each way, with purchase required by July 8. Regular fares will range from $99 each way to $296 each way when booked through JetBlue's website. JetBlue also said it plans to increase flight frequencies on its other Boston-Florida routes to 20 daily, from 13. From Logan, the airline flies to Orlando, Fort Lauderdale, and Tampa in Florida; Oakland and Long Beach in California; and Denver. (Keith Reed)

Ex-Fleet employee settles SEC charges

A former FleetBoston Financial Corp. employee agreed to pay $525,000 for allegedly trading on insider information about an acquisition of his firm by Bank of America Corp. in October. According to the Securities and Exchange Commission, Guillermo Garcia Simon, a former employee in Fleet's Buenos Aires office, purchased 1,100 FleetBoston call options during the last hours of trading for about $11,000. His purchase represented more than 50 percent of trading volume in that series of options that day, the SEC said. Before the market opened Oct. 27, the acquisition was made public and Fleet's stock and call option values rose dramatically. The value of Simon's options jumped to $473,000, the SEC said. Simon agreed to the settlement without admitting or denying any wrongdoing. Under the settlement, on June 9 a court ordered Simon to give up the $473,000 plus interest of $1,600 and a civil penalty of $51,800. (Dow Jones/AP)

Reebok VP sentenced to home detention

Reebok International Ltd. vice president Thomas Shine was sentenced to six months of home detention and three years of probation for helping Just For Feet Inc. fake earnings. Shine, 58, is one of six former Just For Feet executives or vendors who pleaded guilty to the fraud, which may amount to $5 million, according to prosecutors. US District Judge Robert Propst sentenced Shine in Birmingham, Ala., and fined him $10,000. Shine was president of LogoAthletic Inc. when he helped Just For Feet inflate earnings by $700,000, US Attorney Alice Martin said. Shine agreed in February to plead guilty to conspiring to submit false statements to Just for Feet's auditors that said LogoAthletic owed $700,000 in receivables as of Jan. 30, 1999, records show. (Bloomberg)

New Internet virus attacks websites

Government and industry specialists warned of a mysterious, large-scale Internet attack against thousands of popular websites. The virus-like infection tries to implant hacker software onto the computers of all website visitors. Industry specialists and the Homeland Security Department were studying the infection to determine how it spreads across websites and find adequate defenses against it. ''Users should be aware that any website, even those that may be trusted by the user, may be affected by this activity and thus contain potentially malicious code," the government warned in one Internet alert. The mysterious infection appeared to target at least one recent version of software by Microsoft Corp. to operate websites, called its Internet Information Server, popular among businesses and organizations. A spokesman for Microsoft declined to comment. Specialists said the attack's effects were unusually broad but weren't substantially interfering with Internet traffic. (AP)

US asks judge to deny Stewart new trial

The government filed court papers opposing Martha Stewart's latest bid for a new trial based on perjury allegations against a Secret Service ink expert. Federal prosecutors in Manhattan urged US District Judge Miriam Goldman Cedarbaum to reject Stewart's request. Stewart and her former stockbroker, Peter Bacanovic, asked for new trials after prosecutors charged ink expert Larry Stewart with lying repeatedly on the witness stand in February. Legal experts say the requests are long shots, mostly because Bacanovic was cleared of a charge of doctoring the worksheet. Martha Stewart and Bacanovic are scheduled to be sentenced July 8 after being convicted in March of lying about why Stewart sold 3,928 shares of ImClone Systems Inc. stock in 2001. Each is expected to get 10 to 16 months in prison. Larry Stewart has pleaded not guilty to perjury charges and is scheduled for trial in September. (AP)
