Emotional response

June 6, 2004

Page 2 of 2 -- Coming soon to a Scion dealer near you: special wheels up to 19 inches in diameter, springs that will lower the ride, and a Toyota Racing Development supercharger to plop onto your tC engine.

ADVERTISEMENT

The tC has a base four-cylinder engine that produces 160 horsepower. The supercharger will bump that to 200 horsepower. A five-speed manual or four-speed automatic transmission are available. Disc brakes are standard, as is ABS.

I drove the tC in the suburbs of Manhattan over hill and dale and found, even in the 160-horsepower version, that it was a solid, snappy ride. And I didn't find I had to wind the engine out for performance as is often the case with Asian-built power plants.

It didn't draw the looks that the xB did when I had the box for a week, but I've always been partial to the look of a coupe and found this one's lines to be muscular and sleek at the same time.

I recall Jim Farley, Scion vice president, saying at the early introduction of the Scion line that once upon a time, "quality and reliability were enough" to sell a vehicle.

Then, he added, because buyers came to assume Toyota had good quality, "Now you need emotion, individuality."

And that is precisely what Toyota is adding to its lineup with the Scion stable.

There is, for instance, definite emotion that surrounds the xB.

In fact, in a quest for individuality, buyers are plopping down an average $1,000 for accessories for every xB they buy.

Toyota hopes that eventually 900 of the 1,200 Toyota dealers nationwide will open Scion hubs at their dealerships. The goal is 100,000 annual sales of the three Scion models by 2005. And the guess here is that Toyota will not be stopping at three Scions.

Remember when it tentatively entered the truck market? The SUV market? Today it is in both big-time -- literally and figuratively.

I'd expect more Scions to roll out of the Toyota stables before the company is done creating this shadow.

And it's one that could loom large over a landscape patrolled by young buyers looking for excitement, the chance to individualize, to tune for performance -- all on a subcompact budget.
