EMC widens lead in storage market

ADVERTISEMENT

EMC Corp. extended its lead as the top seller of computer-data storage machines, buoyed by first-quarter sales that expanded at nearly three times the industry's 8.5 percent growth rate, researcher Gartner Inc. said. EMC's sales grew 22 percent in the quarter to $736 million from $603 million the year before, Gartner analyst Roger Cox said. That earned EMC a 24 percent share of the market. Total first-quarter sales of so-called external storage systems rose to $3.12 billion from $2.88 billion a year earlier. Number-two ranked Hewlett-Packard, which saw its market share slip to 17 percent from 19 percent a year ago, and number-three ranked IBM, whose share fell to 10 percent from 13 percent, posted the biggest sequential declines. (Bloomberg)

SkillSoft posts profit in quarter

Nashua-based SkillSoft PLC swung to a first-quarter profit on higher revenue and fewer unusual charges from the year before. Also, the maker of Internet-based training courses and software for corporations, government agencies, and educational institutions revised its guidance for the year's remaining quarters to reflect a shift of bookings into the second half. However, full-year guidance was unchanged. For the first quarter, SkillSoft earned $3.2 million, or 3 cents a share compared to a loss of $12.6 million, or 13 cents a share, in the year-ago period. Revenue rose 21 percent to $52.8 million from $43.6 million in the year-ago period. (Dow Jones)

Calif. to vote on stem-cell funding

California, home to biotechnology firms including Amgen Inc., would sell $3 billion in bonds over a decade to fund stem-cell research under an initiative that qualified for the statewide ballot in November. The California secretary of state will certify the measure for the Nov. 2 ballot. Supporters of the ballot initiative submitted more than 1 million signatures on petitions to local election boards, 400,000 more than are needed. The measure would make California the largest source of state funding for studies using stem cells, which are seen as a potential source of cures for debilitating disease. (Bloomberg)

Ford told to pay $368.6m in suit

A jury has ordered Ford Motor Co. to pay nearly $369 million to a woman paralyzed in a rollover accident involving a Ford Explorer, the nation's best-selling sport-utility vehicle. The San Diego County jury ordered the automaker to pay $246 million in punitive damages, after awarding more than $122.6 million in compensatory damages on Tuesday. The award is one of the biggest ever against the automaker and marked the first loss after 11 victories in rollover lawsuits involving the Ford Explorer. Ford has said it will appeal. (AP)

V.I. Technologies Inc., a biotech company aiming to improve the safety of the world's blood supply, said it had merged with privately held Panacos Pharmaceuticals, a developer of a new drug for treating HIV infection, in a stock deal initially valued at $27 million. Watertown-based Vitex will issue 25 million common shares in exchange for all outstanding Panacos shares upon the close of the transaction, company officials said. Vitex will also issue up to 20 million additional shares to Panacos shareholders upon the successful completion of certain clinical milestones. . . . XTL Biopharmaceuticals Ltd. said Cubist Pharmaceuticals Inc. of Lexington will help fund development of XTL's HepeX-B treatment and get worldwide marketing rights. The payments may reach $30 million to $40 million, XTL chief executive Martin Becker said. XTL had been seeking partners to license HepeX-B, which prevents a recurrence of hepatitis B in patients after liver transplants. XTL estimates the global market for the drug, now in phase 2 trials, is worth about $100 million. . . . Ben & Jerry's is unveiling lines of ice creams and yogurts that have fewer carbohydrates or less fat as it taps into increased consumer demand for healthier food. The low-carbohydrate ice creams have only 2 to 5 grams of net carbs per serving, the company said. Ben & Jerry's, a subsidiary of Unilever, is also introducing sugar free and light ice cream flavors. (Globe wire services)
