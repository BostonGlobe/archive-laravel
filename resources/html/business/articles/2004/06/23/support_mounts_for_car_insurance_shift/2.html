SBC to build $6b fiber-optic network

ADVERTISEMENT

SBC Communications Inc., the number two US local telephone company, will spend up to $6 billion building a fiber-optic network that offers digital television service as it steps up competition with cable TV providers. Microsoft Corp. will supply Internet Protocol TV software for the network, which will be rolled out over five years, SBC said. SBC will use the connections to sell fast Internet service and Web calling to consumers and small businesses. "It will set the stage for full competition against cable," SBC chief executive Edward Whitacre said. Offering digital television is part of his strategy to retain clients as cable TV providers start offering telephone services. (Bloomberg)

Bill allows rollover of child-care accounts

Families who set aside money for child care in tax-free accounts wouldn't have to worry about forfeiting unspent dollars at the end of the year under a bill passed by the House. Lawmakers passed a bill by voice vote allowing families to roll over to the next year up to $500 in unused child-care savings held in a flexible spending account. Flexible spending accounts let employees set aside a portion of their salaries, before paying taxes, to pay for child care and healthcare expenses. Current laws require those employees to forfeit unused money that's left in the accounts at the end of the year. (AP)

Service enables dictation of e-mails

Nextel Communications Inc. has launched a service that lets subscribers dictate e-mails from their cellphones that arrive as links to "sound files" in recipients' computer mailboxes. The NextMail service costs $7.50 a month on any Nextel calling plan, plus the cost of airtime used in dictating messages, which can be up to five minutes in length. Subscribers need to program in e-mail addresses for recipients in their cellphone contact list and can send a spoken message to 50 recipients simultaneously. Recipients can respond by writing a short e-mail that arrives on the Nextel subscriber's phone as a text message. Pacific DataVision Inc. of San Diego operates the service for Nextel. (Peter J. Howe)

Guidant to spend $100m worldwide

Guidant Corp., the world's third-biggest maker of mesh tubes that hold clogged arteries open, will create 1,000 jobs in Ireland as part of a planned $100 million expansion of factories worldwide. Guidant is expanding factories, hiring workers in the United States and elsewhere, and trying to win regulatory approval for a new artery-opening stent to catch industry leader Boston Scientific Corp. and number two Johnson & Johnson in sales of the tubes. "As we move toward US and international approval of a drug-eluting stent, there is no doubt that we will require additional manufacturing capacity to meet demand," said Steven Tragash, a company spokesman. (Bloomberg)

Etc.

CBL & Associates Properties Inc., the largest owner of shopping malls in the Southeast, bought a shopping mall in Little Rock, Ark., from First Union Real Estate Equity and Mortgage Investments for $77.5 million. First Union, based in Boston, revealed the planned sale of Park Plaza Mall last month without identifying the buyer . . . Two newly minted Massachusetts public companies had mixed results in their first day of trading. Momenta Pharmaceuticals, a Cambridge developer of treatments for blood clots, jumped $1.31 to $7.81 after raising $34.8 million in its initial offering. Color Kinetics, a Boston company that designs, manufactures, and markets lighting systems fell 5 cents to $9.95 after being priced at $10 a share . . . Sepracor Inc., which makes the Estorra insomnia drug, isn't in talks to be acquired, chief financial officer David Southwell said. "We're managing the company to be an independent, big pharmaceutical company," he said. "We're not in discussions to be bought, and we certainly don't intend to start them." Business Week magazine reported in May that Marlborough's Sepracor may be a takeover target . . . Raytheon Co. of Waltham and Lockheed Martin Corp. said they won a contract to supply the Czech Republic with Javelin antitank missiles. The value of the order wasn't disclosed . . . Pepsi Bottling Group Inc., the world's largest distributor of PepsiCo Inc. beverages, agreed to buy closely held Seltzer and Rydholm Inc. to expand sales in southern and central Maine. The sale price wasn't disclosed. Seltzer and Rydholm has a plant in Auburn, Maine, and warehouses in Portland and Augusta, Pepsi Bottling said. (Globe wire services)
