Earnings Roundup

January 22, 2004

Parametric Technology Corp.

Yesterday

Close: $4.57

Change: -$0.18

52-Week High: $4.75

52 Week -Low: $1.80

Parametric Technology Corp., whose software is used by engineers at companies including Boeing Co. and Toyota Motor Corp., said its fiscal first-quarter net loss widened to 10 cents a share as sales dropped. The Needham company's loss was $26.5 million, compared with $11.4 million, or 4 cents, a year earlier. Revenue in the quarter ended Jan. 3 fell 8.8 percent to $156.8 million from $172 million, the company said.

Parametric blamed the wider loss on $21.6 million in costs for restructuring and a drop in sales from some of the products it licenses.

The company expects a loss of 5 cents to 9 cents and sales to drop to $150 million to $160 million in the second quarter. It had a net loss of 6 cents and $171 million in sales a year earlier. (Bloomberg)

EBay Inc.

Yesterday

Close: $64.38

Change: -$1.32

52-Week High: $67.42

52 Week -Low: $35.81

EBay Inc., the world's largest Internet auctioneer, said fourth-quarter earnings jumped 64 percent as more people bought and listed items for sale on its websites during the holidays. EBay raised 2004 profit and sales forecasts. Net income rose to $142.5 million, or 21 cents a share, from $87 million, or 14 cents, a year earlier. Revenue climbed a better-than-expected 57 percent to $648.4 million from $413.9 million, San Jose, Calif.-based eBay said.

EBay raised its 2004 net income forecast by 8 cents to as much as 99 cents a share and said it now projects revenue will be as much as $3 billion, or $100 million more that its prior projection. The revenue forecast, if met, means eBay will reach a sales goal set by chief executive Meg Whitman in September 2000 a year earlier than planned.

Excluding stock-based pay costs, amortization and certain other costs, fourth-quarter profit would have been $157 million, or 24 cents a share, eBay said.

On that basis, which doesn't comply with generally accepted accounting principles, analysts expected the firm to earn 22 cents. (Bloomberg)

Lucent Technologies Inc.

Yesterday

Close: $4.42

Change: -$0.33

52-Week High: $5.00

52 Week -Low: $1.35

Lucent Technologies Inc. had first-quarter net income of $338 million as sales rose for the first time in more than three years on demand for wireless network gear. Net income was 7 cents a share, compared with a loss of $264 million, or 11 cents, in the year-earlier quarter ended Dec. 31, Murray Hill, N.J.-based Lucent said.

Sales rose 8.9 percent to $2.26 billion, the first year-over-year increase since the quarter ended in September 2000.

Excluding items, first-quarter profit was 3 cents a share, which beat analyst forecasts for a loss of 1 cent, based on a Thomson Financial survey. Lucent said it had a net favorable impact of 4 cents a share from items including a tax benefit, an investment gain and costs related to restructuring and warrants expected to be issued as part of a lawsuit settlement.

Lucent, whose shares have jumped 56 percent this month, is benefiting from stronger demand for high-speed wireless equipment from customers such as Verizon Wireless and gear for sending calls using Internet technology. The company had $28.7 billion of losses in the past three years. Chief executive Patricia Russo expects a profit this fiscal year, excluding certain costs. (Bloomberg)

Merrill Lynch & Co.

Yesterday

Close: $59.60

Change: -$0.58

52-Week High: $60.88

52 Week -Low: $30.75

Merrill Lynch & Co., the biggest securities firm by capital, said fourth-quarter profit more than doubled to a record as a stock market rally boosted revenue in all of the firm's businesses. Merrill's fourth-quarter net income was $1.2 billion, or $1.23 a share, compared with $539 million, or 56 cents, the company said. Net revenue rose 17 percent to $4.9 billion and expenses fell 6 percent to $3.3 billion.

Quarterly earnings, excluding a 5-cent a share gain from Sept. 11, 2001-related recoveries, exceeded the $1.02 a share predicted by the 17 analysts.

New York-based Merrill benefited from 12 percent gains in the Dow Jones industrial average and Standard & Poor's 500 index in the quarter as more companies and individual investors used its investment banking, trading, brokerage and asset management services.

For the year, the firm earned a record $4 billion, or $4.05 a share, up 59 percent from $2.5 billion, or $2.63, in 2002. Revenue rose $1.5 billion, or 8 percent, to $20.2 billion in 2003 and was $7 billion lower than in 2000, when the firm earned $2.6 billion. More than 80 percent of the increased revenue directly added to profit because of the firm's lower costs. (Bloomberg)

J.P. Morgan Chase & Co.

Yesterday

Close: $40.10

Change: +$1.01

52-Week High: $40.10

52 Week -Low: $20.13

J.P. Morgan Chase & Co., which last week agreed to buy Bank One Corp., reversed a year-ago loss with a strong profit that easily beat estimates as investment banking and fewer bad loans boosted earnings. The number two US bank reported fourth-quarter net income of $1.86 billion, or 89 cents a share, sailing past the average Wall Street analyst estimate of 77 cents a share.

In the year-ago quarter, the bank took a $1.3 billion charge related mostly to its past dealings with bankrupt energy company Enron Corp. The bank reported a net loss of $387 million, or 20 cents per share, in the fourth quarter of 2002.

For the full year, J.P. Morgan's net income more than quadrupled to $6.72 billion, or $3.24 a share, from $1.66 billion, or 80 cents, a year earlier. (Bloomberg)

AMR Corp.

Yesterday

Close: $14.55

Change: +$0.83

52-Week High: $15.46

52 Week -Low: $1.25

American Airlines parent AMR Corp.'s fourth-quarter loss narrowed to $111 million from $529 million a year earlier on cost reductions led by worker concessions. The net loss per share shrank to 70 cents from $3.39, the Fort Worth company said. Sales rose 3.9 percent to $4.39 billion from $4.23 billion as American offered fewer discounts.

Excluding costs for grounding aircraft, closing facilities and cutting jobs, and gains of $84 million from a tax settlement and $150 million from the sale of its investments in travel websites Orbitz Inc. and Hotwire, AMR would have lost $95 million, or 59 cents a share. On that basis, the average forecast was a per-share loss of $1.01 in a Thomson analyst survey. (Bloomberg)
