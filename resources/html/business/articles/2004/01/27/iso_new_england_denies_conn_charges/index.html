BUSINESS IN BRIEF

ISO-New England denies Conn. charges

January 27, 2004

Blumenthal The operator of New England's power grid rejected as "baseless" charges by Connecticut Attorney General Richard Blumenthal that it acted "deceptively and misleadingly" when some natural gas-fired power plants shut during the Jan. 14-16 cold snap. The grid operator, ISO-New England, warned on Jan. 15 of possible rolling blackouts, in which power is interrupted to preserve the system's integrity, and said some gas-fired plants didn't have fuel. Last week, Blumenthal said that gas was available and that generators shut plants to sell gas at a profit. Gas in Boston jumped to a record $51 per million British thermal units during the cold spell. "Given our efforts, and the success in keeping the lights on during extreme weather and highly volatile operating conditions, we strongly object to the baseless allegations that we acted `deceptively or misleadingly,' " the grid operator said on its website. (Bloomberg)

Class-action status granted against SBLI

A Suffolk Superior Court judge has granted the plaintiffs in a long-running lawsuit against Savings Bank Life Insurance Company of Massachusetts class-action status. The suit, filed by Boston attorney Jason Adkins in 1998, contends that SBLI has not paid enough dividends to its policyholders and that it has kept too much money in its safety fund reserves. SBLI is disputing Adkins's allegations, saying that he ignores deductions the company is legally allowed to take. (Sasha Talcott)

Calif. company to purchase N.E. Pottery

Central Garden & Pet Co., the largest US distributor of lawn, garden, and pet products, agreed to buy New England Pottery Inc. for about $69 million so that it can add products that are less seasonal. New England Pottery, a Foxborough-based unit of Heritage Partners Inc., has annual sales of about $75 million, California-based Central Garden said. New England Pottery is the largest supplier of decorative pottery in the Northeast and mid-Atlantic, Central Garden added. Heritage Partners is a closely held equity firm that manages $1.4 billion. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION

Minorities targeted in auto financing

Auto financing charges cost consumers $1 billion each year, and blacks and Hispanics are particular targets, a consumer advocacy group said. When a car buyer arranges financing through an auto dealer, lenders quote a finance rate that's based on the buyer's credit history. In some cases, dealers are increasing that rate by several percentage points and sharing the profit with lenders, the Consumer Federation of America said. The National Automobile Dealers Association responded that the charges are fair compensation for dealers who set up financing. "The rate provided by finance companies to dealers is a wholesale rate," the association said. "It's the same as the difference between what McDonald's pays for a hamburger and what we pay for it. The McDonald's markup is undisclosed." Dealers typically increase the rate by about three percentage points, which can add $1,000 to the cost of the loan, the Consumer Federation said. (AP)

Ex-Global Crossing executives sued

Former Global Crossing Ltd. officials, including ex-chairman Gary Winnick, should be responsible for any damages that Goldman Sachs Group Inc., Citigroup Inc., and Merrill Lynch & Co. may be ordered to pay the telecommunications company's shareholders, the banks said in a lawsuit. The shareholders are suing seven investment banks, claiming they underwrote an April 2000 stock sale and issued favorable opinions about Global Crossing's finances while the company filed misleading disclosures with securities regulators. The banks said Winnick and five other former Global Crossing executives misled them and should be liable for any damages, according to the suit filed in federal court in New York. (Bloomberg)

Bear Wagner confirms SEC civil notice

Bear Wagner Specialists LLC confirmed it has received notice from the Securities and Exchange Commission that it could face civil charges for improper trading practices in stocks the firm manages on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange. A spokeswoman for Bear Stearns & Co., which has a majority stake in Bear Wagner, said that the specialist firm also could be penalized by the NYSE. On Friday, specialist firms LaBranche & Co., Fleet Specialist, and Van Der Moolen all said they had received "Wells notices" from the SEC, warning them of impending civil action, as well as notice from the NYSE about potential penalties. A fifth firm, the Goldman Sachs subsidiary Spears, Leeds & Kellogg, received notice of SEC and NYSE actions as well, sources close to the matter said. The five firms, which manage buy-sell auctions on the floor of the NYSE, are accused of illegally managing prices during transactions in order to skim profits. (AP)

THE WORLD

Luxottica to acquire Cole National

Eyeglass company Cole National Corp. has agreed to be acquired by Italy's Luxottica SpA in a $401 million deal that would boost Luxottica's share of the US eyeglass market to 24 percent, almost 10 times what the next largest player, Wal-Mart, holds. Luxottica, which operates the LensCrafters and Sunglass Hut chains, is offering $21.50 for each Cole share, or a premium of more than 60 percent from the company's share price Oct. 8. After the news, Cole shares climbed $1.75, or 8.6 percent, to close at $22.10 on the New York Stock Exchange. (Dow Jones/AP)

EU, Microsoft continue settlement talks

Microsoft Corp. continued to seek a settlement with the European Commission, even as the European Union took the next step toward a final judgment in its antitrust case against the software giant. The commission has started circulating a rough draft of its final judgment against Microsoft for abusing its dominant position and restricting competition by tying its Media Player program for video and music to its ubiquitous Windows operating system. "There is a draft on the table and this should come as no surprise; after all, the investigation has been going on for long enough. The final stage was the hearing in November and now we have to conclude," said Amelia Torres, a spokeswoman for EU Competition Commissioner Mario Monti. She did not indicate a time frame, but others have said a decision is expected by spring. (Reuters) . . .

Etc.

Avid Technology Inc. of Tewksbury acquired Munich-based NXN Software AG for $43.9 million in cash. Avid, which makes editing equipment for digital film, says integrating its products with NXN alienbrain software will help its customers to track, store, and control different versions of files. . . . Fidelity Investments said Ciaran O'Neill will take over management of its Structured Large Cap Value fund and Structured Mid Cap Value fund next month. O'Neill, who has been an analyst at Fidelity since 1995, replaces Robert Macdonald, who has managed the $24 million Large Cap and $89 million Mid Cap funds since they opened in November 2001. Macdonald will take another position at Fidelity. . . . Hearst-Argyle Television Inc. agreed to buy WMTW-TV, an ABC affiliate serving Portland, Maine, for $37.5 million in cash to gain its fourth station in New England. Hearst-Argyle already owns TV stations in Boston, Manchester, N.H., and Burlington, Vt., the company said. . . . PerkinElmer Inc. signed a $250 million, five-year deal to supply amorphous silicon digital flat panel X-ray detector technology to General Electric Co.'s GE Medical Systems and GE Aircraft Engines units. PerkinElmer Optoelectronics will be the sole provider of this technology to the units, Wellesley-based PerkinElmer said. (Globe wire services)
