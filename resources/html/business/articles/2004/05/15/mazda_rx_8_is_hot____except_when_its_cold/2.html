Mazda RX-8 is hot -- except when it's cold

May 15, 2004

Page 2 of 2 -- That weight distribution didn't help with traveling through the snow, though. Fitted with wide, blocky performance tires, the RX-8 spent every evening at the very bottom of my driveway, unable to make it up the slight incline. Once on dry pavement, however, the car handles curves with incredible ease.

Inside, the RX-8 is filled with top-quality materials. The design of the twin rotors churning under the hood is carried through in the interior, with a rotor-shaped shift knob and other details throughout. The instrument panel and radio controls are easy to read and intuitive to operate.

The test RX-8 came through with the $4,000 Grand Touring package that includes Dynamic Stability Control, xenon headlamps, a Bose audio system, a moonroof, and leather, among other niceties. Our advice? Skip the leather and the fancy radio; the DSC and xenon lamps are part of a much cheaper Sport package.

The DSC system includes traction control, keeping the car in line when either the rear or the front end of the car starts to slide to the outside of a corner. It's a godsend in wet conditions, but it borders on useless in the snow. The fat tires just slip too easily on truly slippery surfaces. That's not a fault of the RX-8 specifically. It's nothing that a good set of Swedish snow tires mounted on narrow steel rims wouldn't cure.

The RX-8 should be the harbinger of better days for Mazda. The new Mazda3 has proven to be a very compelling small car, and the Mazda6 is the basis for some exciting new product from Ford. The company is even considering the rotary engine in future products outside the sports-car realm. If it can prove itself as reliable as a four-cylinder, it might just have a shot.

THE BASICS

Base price: $26,680 (six-speed manual transmission)

Fuel economy: 18 m.p.g. city, 24 m.p.g. highway (Mazda est.)

THE EARLY LINE

With all-new technology smoothing the wrinkles out of its rotary powerplant, the RX-8 goes a long way toward rethinking internal combustion engines. Such horsepower from an engine the size of a motorcycle's is astounding. Just 18 m.p.g. in the city isn't.

THE SPECIFICS

Drivetrain: Rear-wheel drive

Seating: 4

Horsepower: 238

Torque: 159 lb.-ft.

Overall length: 174.3 inches

Wheelbase: 106.4 inches

Height: 52.8 inches

Width: 69.7 inches

Curb weight: 3,029 pounds

THE SKINNY

Nice touches: Envisioning yourself as an F1 driver as the tach needle sweeps to stratospheric levels. Great exhaust note.

Annoyance: Charging customers $135 for chromed rotary details on the front and rear airdams. Come on, guys. Give us a break.

Watch for: The unique rotary powerplant to take a bow in other Mazda products. The cost of engineering such an engine would be way too expensive for other manufacturers to undertake now, so Mazda's got the lock. The rear-opening doors are a great feature on a coupe, and have been in use for several years on the Saturn SC series.
