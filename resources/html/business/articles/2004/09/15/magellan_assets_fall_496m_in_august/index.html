BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Magellan assets fall $496m in August

September 15, 2004

Fidelity Investments' flagship equity fund, Magellan, saw assets slip about $496 million in August, to $61.9 billion, primarily a result of investor withdrawals. The fund had a year-to-date loss of 1.56 percent through Aug. 31, compared with the 0.43 percent gain of its benchmark, the Standard & Poor's 500 Index. Year-to-date through July 31, the fund lost 1.79 percent. According to Alpha Research Inc., Magellan had $647 million in investor outflows in August, which was offset by investment gains. The financial industry stock sector remained Magellan's largest allocation, at 23.5 percent of assets. (Dow Jones)

AMG to buy stake in TimesSquare unit

Affiliated Managers Group Inc. of Boston, which owns stakes in 18 money-management firms, agreed to buy the growth-stock business of TimesSquare Capital Management Inc., its fourth 2004 transaction. AMG will buy 60 percent of the unit, to be called TimesSquare Capital Management LLC, from Cigna Corp., AMG said. TimesSquare managers will own the rest. Terms were not disclosed. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION
Brokerage to pay $350k in trading probe

Charles Schwab Corp. agreed to pay $350,000 to end a Securities and Exchange Commission probe of allegations brokers improperly allowed some customers to change hundreds of mutual fund orders after the markets closed, violating rules that orders placed after 4 p.m. Eastern time be filled at the next day's prices. Schwab, of San Francisco, did not profit from the arrangement, and there was no evidence Schwab's customers engaged in systematic "late trading" to profit from news announced after the markets close, the SEC said. Instead, when Schwab's computers rejected an order, such as because the fund was closed to new investors, brokers would call the client after hours and offer a chance to make a new trade. (Washington Post)

New flaw found in Windows XP

Microsoft Corp. has found a new security flaw in its Windows XP operating system and warns that an attacker could infiltrate other computers by persuading their owners to open a specialized graphics file. The company released a patch to fix the flaw as well as a tool to scan for the problem. Those with the Service Pack 2 update are not affected. (AP)

2 contractors to build Army network

General Dynamics Corp. and Lockheed Martin Corp. will work together on a $12 billion program to design a communications network for the Army under a plan to speed development. The Warfighter Information Network-Tactical, or WIN-T, will be a high-speed communications network linking commanders at the Pentagon to the field. New systems like the $92 billion Future Combat Systems family of armored vehicles being developed by Boeing Co. and the $5.8 billion Joint Tactical Radio System being developed by Lockheed, Boeing, General Dynamics, and others will plug into the system. "This is an enormously critical program because it's the backbone for the rest of the Army's modernization," said John Pike, an analyst at Globalsecurity.org. Lockheed and General Dynamics had been competing; now, General Dynamics will lead the team and Lockheed will be the major subcontractor. (Bloomberg)

Sale heats up rivalry in music downloads

Yahoo Inc. will buy Musicmatch Inc. for $160 million to sell music downloads and compete with Apple Computer Inc. and Microsoft Corp. Musicmatch's service allows consumers to download songs for 99 cents each, store music from compact discs on their computers, and transfer music to CDs or digital players. Yahoo's music site, Launch, doesn't let customers store music and doesn't sell downloads itself. Apple's iTunes service sells the most music online. Microsoft opened an online music store Sept. 2. Adding Musicmatch's subscribers will expand Launch's audience by 78 percent to 23 million, Yahoo said. (Bloomberg)

Software firm's quarterly profit up 16%

California-based Oracle Corp., the third-largest software maker, reported a 16 percent gain in first-quarter net income, to $509 million, or 10 cents a share, from $440 million, or 8 cents, a year earlier. Sales rose 6.9 percent to $2.22 billion. Oracle is pursuing a $7.7 billion hostile takeover bid for PeopleSoft Inc. to help reduce the reliance on its database business, where sales surged 19 percent. New license revenue from applications software tumbled 36 percent, underscoring Oracle's need to buy number two-ranked PeopleSoft. Per-share earnings beat the 9-cent average estimate of 34 analysts surveyed by Thomson Financial. Earnings this quarter will be 13 cents; revenue will rise 3 to 7 percent, to $2.58 billion to $2.66 billion. (Bloomberg)

Hotel chain to use Expedia, Hotels.com

Cendant Corp. will offer reservations at its 6,000-plus hotels on IAC/InterActiveCorp.'s Expedia and Hotels.com Internet sites. New York-based Cendant franchises hotel chains, including Super 8, Days Inn, and Ramada. Expedia said the site previously offered rooms at about 500 Cendant hotels. Cendant said its hotels will be featured more prominently under the new agreement. Cendant is offering more rooms through Expedia as other hotel companies offer fewer. IAC, of New York, said its sales growth is slowing as hotel operators sell more rooms through their own sites. (Bloomberg)

Airline pensions protected, agency says

Workers at US Airways Group Inc. and UAL Corp.'s United Airlines will be protected if the companies' terminate their pension plans, the government's Pension Benefit Guarantee Corp. said. It will also ask Congress to change bankruptcy laws so pension claims would trump claims from unsecured creditors. US Airways will miss a pension payment tomorrow; it's in its second reorganization in two years. United plans to skip about $575 million in contributions. (Bloomberg)

THE WORLD
Russian gas producer to run oil operation

Russia's state-controlled Gazprom, the world's largest natural gas company, is taking over the state oil firm, Rosneft -- possibly a precursor to an even more dramatic step by the state to take over the core production unit of oil giant Yukos and merge it into the new Gazprom to create an energy conglomerate under Kremlin control. Prime Minister Mikhail Fradkov said the state plans "ambitious" expansion of Gazprom: "We will soon receive a large company that in the future would be turned into a transnational company of world significance." Gazprom produces about 20 percent of the world's natural gas. (Washington Post)
