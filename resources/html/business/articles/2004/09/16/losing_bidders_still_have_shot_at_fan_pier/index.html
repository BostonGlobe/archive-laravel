Losing bidders still have shot at Fan Pier

While talks continue with winning team, Pritzker tests waters with others

By Thomas C. Palmer Jr., Globe Staff  |  September 16, 2004

The owners of Fan Pier reached out to one of the losing bidders for the property over the last week to see whether the firm was still interested in the land, in case the winning team led by Lennar Corp. doesn't close the deal.

Nicholas J. Pritzker, chairman and chief executive of Hyatt Development Corp., which owns the undeveloped 21-acre site, confirmed yesterday that Hyatt has had conversations with at least one other prospective buyer in the last week. But he said "negotiations" on a sale were not going on with anyone except Lennar.

Lennar Corp. and its two partners, LNR Property Corp. and Turnberry Associates, all based in Florida, missed a Tuesday deadline for making a down payment on Fan Pier. Last month, Pritzker accepted their total bid, estimated at $125 million, and the Lennar group has been engaged in "due diligence," studying the deal, since then.

A standard down payment is 10 percent of the bid.

Pritzker characterized the deal as "very complicated" and said the Lennar team was "working very hard. . . . I see nothing but determination on their part" to close the sale, as planned.

"However," Pritzker said, "the period of exclusivity has lapsed. We may end up talking to other people."

Asked whether Hyatt officials or their representatives had spoken with anyone from Pembroke Real Estate, one of the unsuccessful bidders, Pritzker said, "Not definitively. . . . We've had continuing conversations with people. No negotiations, just taking their temperature."

"We're aware there's certainly interest out there," Pritzker added.

Vincent Loporchio, a spokesman for Fidelity Investments, which owns Pembroke Real Estate, would not confirm that there had been discussions recently but noted the company's holdings in the area and said: "We will continue to pursue opportunities if they are a good fit for our real estate development strategy."

The other local bidder was mall developer Stephen R. Karp, who teamed with Prudential Center owner Boston Properties.

Pritzker, whose Chicago-based family controls Hyatt, was in Boston for yesterday's groundbreaking of the Institute of Contemporary Art building on Fan Pier. The land, with an estimated value of $3 million, is being leased at almost no cost to the ICA. The Pritzkers are paying about $1 million to bring utilities to the project, which is being developed before anything else on Fan Pier.

Mayor Thomas M. Menino said yesterday he hoped the Fan Pier matter could be resolved as quickly as possible. "We're losing valuable time," he said. "If that developer that was originally chosen cannot meet its financial requirements, there are other developers waiting in the wings that see the potential of the property."

Stuart Miller, chief executive of Lennar Corp. and chairman of LNR Properties, reiterated yesterday that his group plans to follow through on the Fan Pier purchase.

"I think this is going just like any other large-scale development program," he said. "We're going through our investigation and due diligence. We will continue on and are hopeful we're going to complete a transaction."

Lennar issued a statement on Tuesday saying that the delay was "not material" and that the team hoped to complete the process in another two weeks.

In a telephone interview yesterday, Miller said: "You have two very sophisticated companies that will protect themselves and will do what's appropriate for their best interests. Nick very appropriately doesn't want to get strung along."

Lennar had said it hoped to close on the land in October, but yesterday Miller did not want to make any new predictions.

"You should see the number of people working around the clock to make this happen," he said. "Let's just see how things unfold."

Although Lennar and LNR Properties have been partners in previous development ventures, Turnberry -- unlike Lennar and LNR, a privately held company -- has not worked with the two as a team before.

"We think the partnership is a very strong one," Miller said. "The form of partnership, who's involved, that's still being worked out. But these are partners that get along and are going to do very well together."

Lennar and its partners, none with any experience in Boston, surprised the real estate community by making the winning bid for the prominent and long vacant Fan Pier land.

Asked whether City Hall has been supportive, Miller said: "I think that we've gotten along very well with the people there. They're very interested in the success of this particular project."

"As expected, they've been a delight to work with," Miller said. Then he paused and added: "Maybe that's an overstatement."
