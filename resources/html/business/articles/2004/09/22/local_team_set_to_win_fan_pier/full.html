Local team set to win Fan Pier

Hub developer expected to edge out Fla. builder

By Thomas C. Palmer and Steve Bailey, Globe Staff  |  September 22, 2004

A team led by local developer Stephen R. Karp is expected to sign an agreement today to purchase Fan Pier, elbowing out Lennar Corp., a Miami-based national home builder that last week missed making a down payment for the property.

In a twist, Hyatt Development Corp. is expected to accept Karp's bid of $125 million -- equal to what Lennar offered -- for the prime 21-acre waterfront site on the edge of downtown, according to a real estate executive familiar with the negotiations.

The executive, who asked not to be identified, said mall developer Karp, chairman of New England Development, would put down a nonrefundable payment today for his group, which made an unsuccessful bid early this month, when Hyatt instead chose Lennar.

Karp was teamed with Related Cos. LP of New York and Boston Properties, owner of the Prudential Center. None of the principals could be reached for comment yesterday.

But a spokesman for Hyatt, which is controlled by the Pritzker family of Chicago, issued a short statement, saying, ''As we said last week, Hyatt is talking to other interested parties."

Efforts to redevelop Fan Pier have been ongoing for the better part of two decades, with plans that were proposed and rejected. Numerous economic cycles have come and gone without any building. The prime land is a key piece of the South Boston Waterfront's transformation from industrial port to the next housing and commercial frontier.

It was unclear last night why Lennar was unable to close the deal in time or why Hyatt did not initially choose Karp. Real estate agreements often turn not just on price but on other specific terms of the deal, such as how quickly payments are made. Also, Lennar had expected to close the sale quickly, though the company could not meet that part of the agreement.

However, Lennar is not yet conceding defeat. A person involved with the Lennar team said he believed the company would succeed in purchasing the property. ''We have an expectation of completing our due diligence and moving toward concluding the deal," said the person, who asked not to be identified.

Lennar and its partners, LNR Property Corp. and Turnberry Associates, all based in Florida, were supposed to have concluded their examination of the property by early last week and made a down payment.

But Lennar let that date go by, ending the period of exclusivity that they and Hyatt executives had agreed to. Lennar said it expected to complete studying the deal and move toward closing in two weeks, which would have meant the end of this week.

Karp and his team matched the offer that Lennar had made, the real estate executive said, and will buy the entire 16 acres of land and 5 acres of marina. Some involved in the bidding process had said that Lennar might buy some, but not all, of the land and leave the remaining pieces for others.

Lennar executives made clear after their bid was accepted that they were considering taking on a local partner in the deal, and Karp was an obvious possibility.

Karp negotiated a contract Monday and yesterday with Hyatt Development Corp., and the deal is expected to be formalized today, the executive said.

Karp will put down an unspecified amount of money that is nonrefundable, and then will make the full down payment, expected to be 10 percent of the $125 million purchase price, when a period of due diligence on his part in completed.

The Fan Pier land, which has been a vacant lot for decades, has permits for approximately 3 million square feet of development, including residences, hotel, office, some retail space, and a significant amount of cultural and civic space, including a new Institute of Contemporary Art museum that broke ground last week, as well as a marina, Harborwalk, and parks.

The Pritzker family, which pushed through a grueling four-year permitting process with the city and state, decided months ago to put the property on the market and let someone else follow through with the expensive construction and buildout.

The Pritzkers accepted bids in early August, and the Lennar team surprised the Boston real estate community with its win. Most in real estate circles had expected that a local team more familiar with the property would have won.

Karp has an option to develop Anthony's Pier 4 property adjacent to Fan Pier, and putting the two pieces together could increase their collective value. The other locally led team that bid on the property included John E. Drew, developer of Boston's World Trade Center, and Pembroke Real Estate, the real estate arm of Fidelity Investments, Drew's partner in the World Trade Center.

Drew is also a partner in the development of the so-called Core Block, a nearby piece of land owned by the Massachusetts Port Authority, where he and Urban Retail Properties Co. plan a large shopping center, residences, and a hotel.

Nicholas J. Pritzker, chairman and chief executive of Hyatt Development, was in Boston last week for the ICA groundbreaking and acknowledged that ''conversations" were going on with a suitor other than Lennar.

He said they were not ''negotiations" and said Lennar was working hard to get the deal done -- but he also noted that the period of exclusivity for Lennar had expired.

''We're aware there's certainly interest out there," Pritzker told the Globe.
