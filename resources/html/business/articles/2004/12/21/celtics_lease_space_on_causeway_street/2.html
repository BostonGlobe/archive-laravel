Page 2 of 2 -- Genzyme gets OK to wrap Ilex purchase

Genzyme Corp. won US antitrust approval to complete its $1 billion purchase of Ilex Oncology Inc. after agreeing to license Schering AG to develop an alternative use for Ilex's leukemia drug Campath. The Federal Trade Commission said it approved the takeover after Cambridge-based Genzyme agreed to the divestiture, designed to preserve the potential for competition for drugs to prevent organ rejection after transplants. Ilex and Schering have a joint venture to make and market Campath as a leukemia treatment. Campath was Ilex's top drug with sales of $72 million last year. Doctors have prescribed Campath for "off-label" use to prevent organ rejection in transplants. The FTC is requiring Genzyme, which has its own drug for organ transplants called Thymoglobulin, to grant Schering the exclusive right to obtain government approval to market Campath for that purpose. (Bloomberg)

Natick company promotes 3 executives

Boston Scientific Corp. named Paul LaViolette to the vacant post of chief operating officer, one of three promotions at the Natick medical devices company. The job adds to the responsibilities of the 10-year company veteran who will continue overseeing its large cardiac stent business. Analyst Mark Landy of Susquehanna International Group called LaViolette a likely successor someday to chief executive James R. Tobin and said the appointment resembled a recent move by rival Medtronic Inc. which shored up its executive succession when it named a new chief operating officer in May. LaViolette, 47, now will also oversee Boston Scientific's endosurgery business and quality and regulatory functions. Its previous head of regulatory affairs, Dennis Ocwieja, is retiring, Boston Scientific said. Other people being promoted include Mary E. Russell, now senior vice president for clinical and regulatory affairs, and chief financial officer Larry Best, now also executive vice president for finance and administration. (Ross Kerber)

TechTarget raises $15m in venture funds

Needham technology publisher TechTarget Inc., which is widely expected to file for an initial public offering next year, said it has raised another $15 million in venture capital. The new venture funding round, led by Technology Crossover Ventures of Palo Alto, Calif., and Polaris Venture Partners of Waltham, brings the company's total funding to $127 million. TechTarget raised $70 million in June, in one of the largest funding rounds of the year for venture-backed start-ups. The company, which publishes news and other content aimed at narrowly focused technology niches, said it would use the new funds to fuel growth, acquire companies, and expand globally. (Robert Weisman)

PanAmSat files for $1.1b offering

PanAmSat Holding Corp., the largest US commercial satellite operator, filed to sell as much as $1.12 billion in shares though an initial public offering eight months after being sold to Kohlberg, Kravis, Roberts & Co. Morgan Stanley, Citigroup Inc., and Merrill Lynch & Co. will manage the IPO, PanAmSat said in a Securities and Exchange Commission filing. The company didn't say how many shares it intends to sell or at what price and the company spokeswoman declined to comment. KKR bought PanAmSat in April from Rupert Murdoch's DirecTV Group Inc. for $3.5 billion. The Carlyle Group and Providence-based Providence Equity Partners in June agreed to pay $953 million each for 27 percent stakes in PanAmSat. (Bloomberg)

File-sharing website is shut down

One of the Web's most popular file-sharing sites has shut down less than a week after Hollywood revealed a flurry of lawsuits against operators of such Internet servers. A note posted on Suprnova.org, which facilitated sharing among users of the BitTorrent program, said the site was "closing down for good." The collection of links to downloadable files, including music, movies, and books, was taken down. Reached via Suprnova.org's chat room, the site's anonymous operator refused to comment on why it shut down. Last week, movie studios sued more than 100 operators of US and European sites that host BitTorrent links but did not name the defendants. (AP)
