BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Lennar, Leggat may bid for Fan Pier

December 7, 2004

Home builder Lennar Corp. of Florida, which made an unsuccessful attempt to buy the 21-acre Fan Pier development site on the South Boston Waterfront, is teaming with Leggat McCall Properties LLC of Boston to consider making another bid. The land, owned by a company controlled by the Chicago-based Pritzker family, is still on the market after a second prospective team, including mall developer Stephen R. Karp, also failed to complete a purchase this fall at a price of $125 million. David S. Hall, vice president of LNR Property Corp., a partner with Lennar, had no comment yesterday except to confirm that his group is working with Leggat, which previously had been interested in buying and developing the property. Fan Pier is fully permitted for about three million square feet of residential, office, hotel, retail, and public-use space. (Thomas C. Palmer Jr.)

Wind-power project sells entire output

A wind-powered generation project planned in the Berkshire County town of Hancock got a big boost when a group of Massachusetts municipal electric utilities agreed to buy all of the project's power output for the next 22 years. The Massachusetts Municipal Wholesale Electric Co., acting on behalf of 14 municipal departments, agreed to pay a permanent fixed rate of 3.65 cents per kilowatt-hour of electricity generated by the Brodie Mountain project, a deep discount from current conventional wholesale power rates. Project developer Berkshire Wind Power LLC, a unit of Distributed Generation Systems Inc., is building 10 wind turbines with total generating capacity of 15 megawatts, comparable to 11,250 average homes' electric demand. (Peter J. Howe)

TechTarget expected to acquire Bitpipe

TechTarget Inc., a Needham technology publisher that raised $70 million in venture capital in the second quarter, is scheduled to disclose today that it will acquire Boston's Bitpipe Inc., a publisher and distributor of white papers and other vendor-generated documents for information technology lead generation. The deal between the two private companies is expected to be valued at about $40 million. "It's two players in the market who are expanding their reach," said James Geshwiler, managing director of CommonAngels of Lexington, one of Bitpipe's financial backers. (Robert Weisman)

CMGI revenue triples in 1st quarter

CMGI Inc., an Internet venture company that has diversified into selling printing, packaging, and mail services, said fiscal first-quarter revenue almost tripled because of its acquisition of Modus Media Inc. Shares of Waltham-based CMGI rose 60 cents, or 32 percent, to $2.48, their highest close since April 12. Sales almost tripled to $257.1 million in the period ended Oct. 31, from $94.9 million a year earlier, CMGI said. The company had a net loss of $553,000, or break-even on per-share basis, compared with net income of $29.9 million, or 7 cents, a year earlier when it had a $40.5 million gain on the sale of Overture Services Inc. stock. (Bloomberg)

Biogen Idec releases more Zevalin data

Biogen Idec Inc. said new data on its Zevalin treatment for lymphoma shows the drug is effective against common types of the immune system cancer. Cambridge-based Biogen Idec reported positive results from a midstage trial of Zevalin, or ibritumomab tiuxetan, in older patients with certain types of B-cell lymphoma; presented new data on the use of the drug in follicular lymphoma; reaffirmed earlier positive results from an ongoing study of Zevalin in relapsed mantle cell lymphoma; and provided long-term data from four early trials of the cancer treatment showing that about 65 percent of these patients experienced an extended response. The company presented the results at a meeting of the American Society of Hematology in San Diego. (AP)

Millennium reports on cancer drug

Millennium Pharmaceuticals Inc.'s Velcade, approved in the United States as a last-resort treatment for the blood cancer multiple myeloma, may help patients in the disease's early stages, according to studies presented at a meeting of the American Society of Hematology in San Diego. A study of 32 newly diagnosed patients found that 88 percent of those who took the drug responded to it, researchers said. US regulators cleared Velcade last year only for patients who have not been helped by standard treatments such as chemotherapy and Celgene Corp.'s Thalomid drug. (Bloomberg)

2d quarter loss larger than expected

3Com Corp., which makes switches that run corporate data networks, said second-quarter revenue missed its forecast, two months after chief executive Bruce Claflin took charge of global sales. Shares fell 36 cents, or 7.9 percent, to $4.21. The loss for the period ended Nov. 26 was 12 to 14 cents a share on revenue of as much as $153 million, the Marlborough company said. The company in September forecast sales of $170 million to $180 million. The net loss narrowed from $139 million, or 37 cents, in the same period a year earlier, when the company had $181.9 million in sales. Claflin missed his revenue forecast by more than 10 percent for the second consecutive quarter. He fired the head of global sales and marketing in September and took over the post himself. The head of operations stepped down a month later. The statement didn't elaborate on why sales fell short. (Bloomberg)

GTech to buy stake in German company

GTech Holdings Corp., the world's biggest seller of computerized lottery systems, said it will buy a 50 percent controlling interest in Gauselmann Group's Atronic companies for about $100 million to $150 million in cash. The final value of the transaction will equal eight times Atronic's earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization for the year ending Dec. 31, 2006, West Greenwich, R.I.-based GTech said. GTech will assume debt as part of the transaction, the company said. Gauselmann's Atronic Group, based in Germany, makes video slot machines used in casinos in Europe, Russia, and Latin America. The deal is likely to have either no effect or boost GTech's earnings slightly in the fiscal year ending February 2008. (Bloomberg)

THE NATION
Business Week names editor

Stephen J. Adler, deputy managing editor of The Wall Street Journal, has been named editor of Business Week magazine, succeeding Stephen B. Shepard, who said last week that he would retire to become the first dean of a new journalism school at City University of New York. Adler, who is also editorial director of the online edition of the Journal, will join the magazine in January for a transition period before officially assuming the top editorial job April 1, the magazine said. (AP)

THE WORLD
China bans Nike kung fu commercial

China banned a Nike commercial showing NBA basketball star LeBron James in a battle with an animated cartoon kung fu master, saying the ad insults Chinese national dignity. The commercial, titled "Chamber of Fear," was broadcast on local Chinese stations and on state television's national sports channel before being pulled last month. It shows the Cleveland Cavaliers' James in a video game-style setting defeating the kung fu master. Two women in traditional Chinese attire and a pair of dragons, considered a sacred symbol in traditional Chinese culture, are also in the ad. The ad "violates regulations that mandate that all advertisements in China should uphold national dignity and interest and respect the motherland's culture," the State Administration for Radio, Film and Television said on its website. A Nike spokesman in Shanghai said the company "respected the government's decision." (AP)
