Defense giant to increase its dividend

December 2, 2004

Raytheon Co. said it plans to increase its dividend and repurchase $700 million in shares. The Waltham-based aerospace and defense giant increased its annual dividend 10 percent to 88 cents a share from 80 cents, starting with the first quarter of 2005. Raytheon said its board also authorized $700 million in share repurchases, which it expects to complete over 18 to 24 months, beginning in 2005. Based on Tuesday's closing share price of $40.34, the repurchase authorization will allow it to buy back about 17.4 million shares. Raytheon has about 452.1 million shares outstanding. Raytheon had $1.05 billion in cash and cash equivalents on its books, as of Sept. 26. Raytheon also backed its October outlook for 2004 earnings from continuing operations of 87 to 92 cents a share, as well as its April outlook for sales of more than $20 billion. (Dow Jones/AP)

Citizens Bank adding 525 jobs in R.I.

Citizens Bank will add 525 jobs in Rhode Island over the next 16 months, part of a $40 million investment to expand operations. The new jobs will make Citizens the fourth-largest private employer and the largest financial-services employer in the state, bank executives said. The salary range for the new positions is $30,000 to $70,000. Citizens will renovate and expand various facilities statewide. Its Providence-based parent, Citizens Financial Group Inc., is a $131 billion holding company with about 24,000 employees in 13 states that is owned by Royal Bank of Scotland Group PLC. The bank cited the recent acquisition of Charter One Financial Inc. as a reason for adding the jobs. (AP)

Dollar hits record low against euro

The dollar fell to a record low against the euro and a 12-year low against the British pound. Gold rose, hitting $454.00 on the New York Mercantile Exchange, up from $451.30 late Tuesday. The euro was quoted at $1.3319 in late New York trading, but rose to a fresh high of $1.3356 later in the evening, up from $1.3280 late Tuesday. The new euro high was primarily a result of thin markets and end-of-day positioning, traders said. The dollar slide may be losing momentum, said David Durrant, of Julius Baer Investment Management. He noted the euro highs of the past few days have been by mere tenths of a cent. The pound was quoted at $1.9327, up from $1.9107. The last time the pound traded above $1.9137 was September 1992. (AP)

Internet sales jump 36 percent

Sales at US Internet retailers jumped 36 percent last week as people used faster online connections to shop for holiday items. Sales of gifts and other nontravel items rose to $1.66 billion in the week ended Nov. 28, according to ComScore Networks Inc., a Virginia research company. Month-to-date online sales rose 24 percent. ComScore forecasts that nontravel Web spending will rise to as much as 26 percent in November and December, to more than $15 billion. "From the data we've seen so far it looks like it's shaping up to be a pretty good e-commerce season," said Aaron Kessler, a Piper Jaffray & Co. analyst. Kessler, who has an "outperform" rating on Overstock.com Inc., a seller of discounted merchandise, said Internet sales will rise as much as 28 percent during the holiday season. The number of people visiting Overstock.com's site over Thanksgiving weekend rose 31 percent. (Bloomberg)

Giuliani to open investment bank

Former New York Mayor Rudolph W. Giuliani, who led the city through the Sept. 11, 2001, terror attacks, is opening an investment bank, Giuliani Capital Advisors LLC. It will be a division of consulting firm Giuliani Partners LLC, according to Giuliani's website, www.giulianipartners.com. Many of its employees will come from Ernst & Young Corporate Finance LLC, a unit of accounting firm Ernst & Young LLP, which Giuliani Capital Partners is buying for an undisclosed amount. Steven D. Oesterle, a managing director at Giuliani Partners and former Ernst & Young vice chairman, will be chairman and chief executive of the investment bank. (Bloomberg)

Slowdown in health spending levels off

The slowdown in the growth of healthcare spending leveled off this year after two years of declines, a new study found, with the cost of treating a privately insured American rising 7.5 percent in the first half of 2004 -- virtually the same as the 7.6 increase in 2003. Paul B. Ginsburg, coauthor of the study, worries that the flat spot could be a pause before the rate of spending increases will once again starts to grow. Even if the rate of increase doesn't jump dramatically, he said, it is still at a dangerous level that outpaces inflation and could lead to more employers dropping health coverage. "We have leveled off at a place that is problematic," said Ginsburg, president of the Center for Studying Health System Change, which conducted the study with the Employee Benefit Research Institute. "There is just not much optimism that we know how to control costs." Healthcare spending growth slowed in 2002 and 2003 after reaching 10 percent in 2001. Ginsburg said the surge in 2001 was partially a reflection of the end of strident managed-care policies. Programs and policies that shift a greater burden of healthcare costs to employees helped to moderate spending growth in 2002 and 2003. But Ginsburg said cost-shifting can only accomplish so much because employees will drop coverage if they can't bear the expense. (AP)

Microsoft vows to make blogging easy

Hoping to keep more Internet users in its branded universe, Microsoft Corp. is offering blogging to the masses. MSN Spaces, which debuts in test form today, makes it easy to set up Web journals. It targets home users who want to share vacation pictures, text journals, or a list of favorite songs. It is free to anyone with a Hotmail e-mail or MSN Messenger account, both of which also are free. MSN Spaces will be supported by banner ads. America Online Inc. has provided its members with AOL Journals since mid-2003, and search engine leader Google Inc. offers a free service through its Blogger.com site. Microsoft already offers MSN Groups for people to create their own websites, but this is the first time it is focusing on the growing blogging phenomenon. The final version of MSN Spaces is expected by June. (AP)

SEC suspends trading in 26 companies

The Securities and Exchange Commission suspended trading in shares of 26 small companies, including a health website named for former Surgeon General C. Everett Koop, because they failed to file financial reports. The agency said the companies' failure to submit quarterly and annual reports means there is a lack of accurate information about them. The SEC said it has begun administrative proceedings against 12 of the companies to suspend or terminate their registration to issue securities. The companies can resume trading Dec. 15 if they file the required reports. (AP)
