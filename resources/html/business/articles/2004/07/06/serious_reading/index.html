Serious Reading

Some of Boston's busiest executives — who share a penchant for no-nonsense nonfiction — give us their recommendations on what books to pick up this summer.

By Charles Stein, Globe Staff  |  July 4, 2004

It's the season when vacationers hit the beach with novels by Danielle Steele and John Grisham. But not Boston's busiest executives. When we asked them for recommendations on books to read this summer, they responded with some pretty serious choices.

While it is true that no one mentioned "Swedish Land-Use Planning," a summertime favorite of former Gov. Michael Dukakis, the titles are dominated by nonfiction selections. "I read history, politics, and sports," said Ellen Zane, chief executive at Tufts-New England Medical Center.

Still the executives came up with an interesting list, and yes, a few novels did make the cut. So did a few books that might qualify as self-serving. Paul Grogan, president of the Boston foundation, recommended "The Good City," a forthcoming book of essays about Boston commissioned by the Boston Foundation. Lieutenant Governor Kerry Healey wants to read a soon-to-be-published book about the Olympics by her boss, Governor Mitt Romney. MFS Investment Management chairman Robert Pozen was too modest to recommend his own textbook, ''The Mutual Fund Business,'' but it is available from Amazon.com for a mere $71.96.

But seriously, the books look pretty good. So pick up a few, put up the beach umbrella, and enjoy yourself. There will be a quiz on all the books in September.

1 | LAWRENCE FISH
Chief executive, Citizens Financial Group
Recommended:
"King Leopold's Ghost," by Adam Hochschild
"It is the story of Belgium's colonization of the Congo. It's a frightful tale of greed and inhumanity."
On the list for this summer:
"Ben Hogan: An American Life," by James Dodson
"A biography of the golfer who was the Ted Williams of golf."

2 | ELLEN ZANE
Chief executive, Tufts-New England Medical Center
Recommended:
"Built to Last: Successful Habits of Visionary Companies," by James C. Collins & Jerry Porras
"The book undertakes a search for the elusive qualities that exist in some companies that enable them to stand the test of time."
"Theodore Rex," by Edmund Morris
"This book gives the reader an in-depth look at the outrageous personality as well as the exceptional accomplishments of President Theodore Roosevelt."
On the list for this summer:
"Profiles in Courage for Our Time," edited by Caroline Kennedy
"A collection of essays about people who risked their own careers for the sake and benefit of society."

3 | MILTON LITTLE JR.
Chief executive, United Way of Massachusetts Bay
Recommended:
"The Teeth of the Tiger," by Tom Clancy
"Tom Clancy delves into the post-9/11 world with a story thrilling enough to be our worst headline nightmare."
On the list for this summer:
"The Billion Dollar BET: Robert Johnson and the Inside Story of Black Entertainment Television," by Brett Pulley
"Written by my friend and former neighbor, this is the story of BET founder Robert Johnson and the price he willingly paid to achieve success in the media business."
"The Michael Eric Dyson Reader," by Michael Eric Dyson
"Reverend Dr. Dyson offers a thought-provoking and provocative journey through a wide range of contemporary social issues facing America."

4 | GLORIA LARSON
Chairwoman, Massachusetts Convention Center Authority
Recommended:
"The Little Friend," by Donna Tartt
"Tartt is an elegant novelist who also manages to explore human weaknesses and relationships in ways that stay with the reader for a very long time."
On the list for this summer:
"Benjamin Franklin: An American Life," by Walter Isaacson
"It was highly recommended to me by my best source of nonfiction, my husband, Allen."

5 | WILLIAM GALVIN
Secretary of the Commonwealth
Recommended:
"Smartest Guys in the Room: The Amazing Rise and Scandalous Fall of Enron," by Bethany McLean and Peter Elkind
"It shows how companies and professionals, like accountants, often have strong incentives to violate laws and professional standards to deceive investors."
"Extraordinary Popular Delusions and the Madness of Crowds," by Charles MacKay
"Though first published in 1841, it is written in an entertaining style and will teach the reader a lot about the behavior of investors and markets."
On the list for this summer:
"The Island at the Center of the World: The Epic Story of Dutch Manhattan and the Forgotten Colony that Shaped America," by Russell Shorto
"The story of the Dutch colony on Manhattan in the 17th century."

6 | ROBERT POZEN
Chairman, MFS Investment Management
Recommended:
"The Piano Tuner," by Daniel Mason
"A fascinating novel about the colonial British army in Asia with a wonderful musical twist."
On the list for this summer:
"What Went Wrong: The Clash Between Islam and Modernity in the Middle East," by Bernard Lewis

KERRY HEALEY
Lieutenant governor
Recommended:
"Democracy in America," by Alexis de Tocqueville
"Even in 1835 de Tocqueville could see that America's greatness stems from our willingness, particularly in tough times, to make really great sacri. ces for the common good."
"The Dante Club: A Novel," by Matthew Pearl
"I am naturally drawn to novels with characters named Healey from Beverly who have an interest in criminal justice."
On the list for this summer:
"Turnaround: Crisis, Leadership and the Olympic Games," by Mitt Romney
"The Olympics are filled with personal stories of heartbreak and heroism and Mitt Romney's rescue of the troubled 2002 Winter Games allowed those stories to be told."

THOMAS STEMBERG
Chairman, Staples Inc.
Recommended:
"Profit from the Core: Growth Strategy in an Era of Turbulence," by Chris Zook with James Allen
"This book speaks to the challenges facing many of the companies I am involved with."
"Dynasty's End: Bill Russell and the 1968-69 World Champion Boston Celtics," by Thomas J. Whalen
"I wanted to relive the final glory years of the Russell era. Those were my first years in Boston as a college student enjoying sub-$10 tickets."
On the list for this summer:
"The Teammates: A Portrait of Friendship," by David Halberstam

CATHY MINEHAN
President, Federal Reserve Bank of Boston
Recommended:
"Path Between the Seas: The Creation of the Panama Canal, 1870-1914," by David Mc-Cullough
"I loved this book. You find out about everything from financial panics in France to battling mosquitos in Panama."
"D-Day: June 6, 1944: The Climactic Battle of World War II," by Stephen E. Ambrose
"The book concentrates on just the one day and tells the story from the point of view of the people living through it."
On the list for this summer:
"Theodore Rex," by Edmund Morris
"This is the second part of the biography of Theodore Roosevelt. The first book was fabulous."

LAWRENCE BACOW
President, Tufts University
Recommended:
"Paris 1919: Six Months that Changed the World," by Margaret Olwen Macmillan
"It is about the Versailles Conference after World War I. We are still dealing with the issues they struggled with."
"Cruising At Last: Sailing the East Coast," by Elliott Merrick
"The author and his wife built a sloop and sailed from South Carolina to Maine and back. I'm a sailor, and I know some of that coast very well."
On the list for this summer:
"Freedom Just Around the Corner: A New American History: 1585-1828," by Walter A. McDougall
"Someone just gave it to me. It's about the early development of the United States."

PAUL GROGAN
President, The Boston Foundation
Recommended:
"The Good City: Writers Explore 21st-Century Boston," edited by Emily Hiestand and Ande Zellman
"It's a book of 15 essays by local writers whose task was to explore 21st-century Boston. I have to confess The Boston Foundation sponsored the project."
"A Preface to Morals," by Walter Lippmann
"He wrote the book in the 1920s, but it is a prescient foretelling of the rise of science and the decline of organized religion."
On the list for this summer:
"Alexander Hamilton," by Ron Chernow
"A new biography of one of the founding fathers."

VIVIEN LI
Executive director, Boston Harbor Association
Recommended:
"Johnny Got His Gun" by Dalton Trumbo "I recently reread this book to remind myself of the horrors of war. If only our elected leaders would read it."
"Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone," by J.K.Rowling
"A great book for both adults and children. One of our daughters stayed up all night to finish the book and recommended it to us. "
On the list for this summer:
"My Life" by Bill Clinton
"Must reading for anyone interested in politics, although I have to confess I probably won't read all 900-plus pages."
