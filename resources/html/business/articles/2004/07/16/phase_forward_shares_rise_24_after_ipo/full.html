BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Phase Forward shares rise 24% after IPO

July 16, 2004

Shares of software company Phase Forward Inc. rose sharply in trading after the company cut the price of its initial public offering to stimulate demand. The Waltham company saw its shares trade at $9.35 on the Nasdaq Stock Market, 24.7 percent above the $7.50 price set on its IPO of 5.25 million shares. The offering price was in line with Phase Forward's recent estimate, set Wednesday. However, that was below original price expectations of $8 to $10 a share. Phase Forward makes a package of products that automate many of the processes used in drug research, such as data capture of patient information, data management, and monitoring of drug safety. (Dow Jones/AP)

Digitas to acquire Internet ad company

Boston marketing firm Digitas Inc. said it will acquire Norwalk, Conn.-based Modem Media Inc. for about $200 million in stock and cash as it seeks to grow its business and add services for clients. The acquisition of Modem, an Internet advertising outfit, will boost Digitas's profits and add clients such as IBM, Kraft, and Sprint. Founded in 1980, as Bronner Slosberg Humphrey, Digitas helps companies such as American Express, Delta Air Lines, and General Motors promote their products on the Internet. Modem Media, which employs 270 people, will continue to operate under its own name. Digitas's shares fell $1.78, or 18.1 percent, to $8.05. (Naomi Aoki)

Fidelity adds independent stock research

Fidelity Investments, the world's largest mutual fund manager, added independent stock research by seven companies for its brokerage customers. The service, which covers more than 4,000 companies, will be offered free on the Internet to individual investors, Boston-based Fidelity said. Brokers who are clients of Fidelity's National Financial Services LLC unit will have access to research at discounted rates. Fidelity said the offerings now give holders of 9.6 million customer accounts broader access to independent analysis than any other major brokerage, including Charles Schwab Corp. and E.Trade Financial Corp. (Bloomberg)

The Patriot Ledger names publisher

Kirk Davis, former president and publisher of Community Newspaper Co., will become president and chief executive of Enterprise NewsMedia and the publisher of Enterprise's Quincy newspaper, The Patriot Ledger. He replaces the current Ledger publisher and Enterprise chief executive, James Plugh, who is being promoted to vice chairman ''to devote more time to potential acquisitions," according to the company. Davis, 43, resigned from Turley Publications, which owns community papers and shoppers, said a spokesman. Davis was publisher of Community Newspaper Co. when it was owned by Fidelity Investments, which sold the company in 2001 to Herald Media Inc. He remains an owner of Holden Landmark Corp. (Kimberly Blanton)

FDA accepts Sepracor inhaler for review

Sepracor Inc.'s metered-dose inhaler Xopenex was accepted for formal review by the Food and Drug Administration, which also accepted the resubmission for the company's Estorra insomnia drug. Sepracor said the formal review for its New Drug Application for Xopenex HFA is expected to be reviewed and acted on by March 12. The drug, which treats and prevents bronchospasm such as asthma and chronic pulmonary disease, was submitted May 12. Also, the New Drug Application for Estorra brand eszopiclone for the treatment of insomnia has begun its review of the resubmission. The company said the FDA has classified the resubmission as a class 2, under which the review is expected to close within six months. (Dow Jones)

THE NATION
Energy trader's bankruptcy plan OK'd

More than two years after its spectacular collapse, Enron won approval to emerge from bankruptcy under a plan in which creditors will receive less than 20 cents on the dollar and the Enron name will disappear. Once Enron sells off some of its major assets to pay creditors, all that will be left of the scandal-ridden company -- once the nation's seventh-largest -- will be a smattering of pipeline and power assets in 14 countries. The reorganization plan -- approved by a bankruptcy judge in New York -- aims to pay most of the creditors about $12 billion of the approximately $63 billion they are owed with cash raised from asset sales. That includes pipeline company CrossCountry Energy and the utility Portland General Electric. Shareholders will get nothing. What is left of Enron will be called Primsa Energy. (AP)

Stewart sentencing set for today

Martha Stewart faces a federal judge today to hear her sentence for lying to investigators about a stock sale. Experts on federal sentencing guidelines expect Stewart, 62, to get 10 to 16 months in prison, although she may be allowed to remain free while she appeals her conviction. Since the celebrity homemaker was convicted March 5 on four criminal counts, hundreds of fans have flooded the judge with letters begging for leniency. ''This woman is to homemakers what Einstein was to science and Freud was to psychiatry," wrote one woman. ''It would be a great waste to put this person in prison." In the event of a prison sentence, lawyers for Stewart are expected to ask the judge to delay her incarceration while they challenge her conviction in federal appeals court in New York. (AP)

US levels age-discrimination charge

A federal lawsuit alleges Maytag Corp. improperly demoted some managers older than 50 because the appliance maker feared they would not understand new computer-based sales procedures. The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission alleges Maytag violated age discrimination laws when it eliminated 13 of 22 regional sales manager positions in 1999. Of the 11 managers who were demoted, eight were older than 50, the lawsuit alleges. Only one employee over 50 kept his job. Maytag spokeswoman Lynne Dragomier said the company had not yet seen the lawsuit, but denied the allegations. (AP)

Visa USA CEO to retire next year

The chief executive of Visa International's US arm, Carl Pascarella, will retire next year after more than a decade at the helm of the credit card company. Visa does not have a replacement yet. Pascarella, 61, has agreed to remain flexible in the timing of his departure. His contract expires in September 2005. ''It's very important that this transition be seamless . . . this gives us enough lead time for a thoughtful, orderly transition," he said. He was named chief executive of Visa USA in August 1993 after an 11-year stint as president of Visa International's Asia-Pacific region. (Dow Jones/AP)

THE WORLD
PC sales continue strong growth

Double-digit growth in worldwide sales of personal computers for the fifth consecutive quarter was driven largely by strong demand in Europe, according to a research company that tracks sales. There was 15 percent growth over the year-ago quarter, with 39.7 million units shipped, led by the strong demand in Europe, as well as better than expected sales in Canada and Latin America, according to Framingham, Mass.-based IDC. Replacement purchases were the key for 13.3 percent growth, and worldwide shipments of 43 million units, according to preliminary results compiled by Stamford, Conn.-based Gartner Inc., which also tracks sales. The companies use slightly different measurement methods. PC shipments in the United States totaled 14 millions units, an 11.4 percent increase over the year-ago quarter, according to Gartner, which attributed the growth to strong sales in the business market. (AP)
