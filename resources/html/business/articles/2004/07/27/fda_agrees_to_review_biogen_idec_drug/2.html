Ex-chief financial officer to plead guilty

ADVERTISEMENT

A former Boeing Co. executive will plead guilty to a criminal charge related to the hiring of an Air Force official who oversaw a Boeing contract to supply refueling jets to the military, a source familiar with the plea agreement said. Former chief financial officer Michael Sears will plead guilty to one charge of aiding and abetting the hiring of Darleen Druyun, who worked on Boeing's negotiations to lease 100 767 tankers to the military, the source said. Sears is expected to enter his plea next week or soon after in US District Court in Alexandria, Va., the source said. Boeing, the number two Pentagon contractor and number two commercial jet maker, fired Druyun and Sears in November after discovering they violated company ethics rules by discussing a job while Druyun was still working on Boeing matters for the government. (Reuters)

Appliance maker sues Electrolux

Maytag Corp., the third-biggest US appliance maker, filed a lawsuit accusing Electrolux AB's Frigidaire unit of infringing on two patents related to clothes washing machines. Both patents are for the process of making plastic baskets inside washers that are stronger and don't damage garments. Maytag accuses Frigidaire of infringing on those patents, without saying the models at issue. Maytag sued Friday in federal court in Sioux City, Iowa. Maytag is guarding its market position against companies seeking to expand in the United States. In November, it sued LG Electronics Inc. after the South Korean company introduced a new line of washing machines. ''Patents are enforceable throughout their entire term. These are active patents," said Maytag spokeswoman Lynne Dragomier. Electrolux is the world's biggest maker of household appliances, with products such as Eureka vacuum cleaners, Electrolux dishwashers, and Weed Eater trimmers. ''We've not yet reviewed details of the charges and cannot comment on matters in litigation," said Electrolux spokesman Tony Evans. (Bloomberg)

$60m expansion planned for N.Y. area

In its first major expansion of branches in seven years, JP Morgan Chase & Co. will invest more than $60 million in the New York metropolitan area this year to open 16 new full-service branches and add new automated teller machines. The financial services company said it has already opened seven new full-service branches in New York and New Jersey this year, with nine more to open by year-end, expanding the metropolitan area's network of more than 400 branches and 1,500 ATMs. With the completed merger of J.P. Morgan Chase and Bank One Corp., customers of both banks can use 6,530 ATMs in 19 states. The banks' branches will be combined under a common computer system and Chase brand starting in 2005. (Dow Jones/AP)

American Express net income rises 15%

American Express Co., the fourth-largest US credit card issuer, said second-quarter profit rose 15 percent as an expanding economy prompted more travel than at any time since the terrorist attacks of September 2001. Net income advanced to $876 million, or 68 cents a share, from $762 million, or 59 cents, a year earlier, the company said. The quarterly gain was the 10th straight one for chief executive Kenneth Chenault, who increased advertising by one-third to draw more customers as US air travel and hotel occupancy and room rates climbed. The company was expected to earn 67 cents, based on the average estimate of 18 analysts in a Thomson Financial survey. Revenue rose 14 percent to $7.26 billion. (Bloomberg)
