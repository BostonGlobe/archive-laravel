The billionaires' Games

July 27, 2004

Page 2 of 2 -- Carrying on
US Olympic softball coach Mike Candrea, whose wife Sue died last week after suffering a brain aneurysm while touring with the team in Wisconsin, still is going to the Games, along with daughter Michelle and son Mikel. "Sue was so excited about the upcoming Olympics and would want me to continue on with our dream, " says Candrea, whose squad will be chasing a third straight gold medal . . . There'll be five new oarsmen on the US Olympic men's eight in Athens. Only Jason Read, Bryan Volpenhein, and Joey Hansen return from the boat that finished second at last year's world regatta. Mike Wherley and Harvard grad Wolf Moser will be in the four (along with Jamie Schroeder and Garrett Klugh) and Jeff Klepacki, Jon Watling, and Ryan Torgerson didn't make the squad. New faces include Beau Hoopman, Dan Beery, Matt Deakin, Sydney veteran Chris Ahrens and Wyatt Allen of Portland, Maine. Most of the fifth-place women's eight is back except for new mom Jen Dore-Terhaar, Michelle Guerette (who's in the quad) and Maite Urtasun, now a spare. The whole US flotilla leaves this week to train in Bulgaria en route to the Games. Meanwhile, the world championship for non-Olympic events begins this week in Spain. New Englanders on the squad are Gabe Winkler (St. Johnsbury, Vt.) in the men's lightweight eight and Maria Picone (Pownal, Maine) in the women's lightweight quad.

Game on -- and on
How goofy is the format for the Olympic women's soccer tournament? Since there are only 10 teams in the draw, they'll play a dozen games just to eliminate two, one of which is almost certainly going to be host Greece. Worse, it forces four of the countries (including the US) to play an extra game to advance. "When we play our second game, Germany and Sweden have a bye," says coach April Heinrichs. "When we play our third game, Japan and China get a bye." The US group (which includes the Greeks, Brazilians, and Australians) gets one break: The third-place team makes the quarterfinals. Not that the Americans, who won gold and silver at the last two Games, have much to worry about. Odds are they'll win the group and face either Mexico or Nigeria in the quarters . . . Back on the firing line is US Olympic trapshooter Bret Erickson after his heart stopped during a training jog last month. "It doesn't seem to be affecting my shooting," says the 43-year-old Army sergeant, who was revived by teammate Mark Weeks and now has a pacemaker. "And I even got a great physical out of the deal." . . . It'll be old-school colors for the US Olympic men's and women's basketball teams, who'll be garbed in hues they haven't worn at the Games in a number of years. The men will wear red for the first time since 1984, the women blue for the first time since 1992. Both also have a new stars-and-stripes uniform . . . Former marathon world record holders Khalid Khannouchi and Catherine Ndereba will head the field at Sunday's Peoples Beach to Beacon road race in Cape Elizabeth, Maine. For Ndereba, who won her third Boston Marathon this year, it'll be the final tuneup for Athens, where she'll be a top contender to win Kenya's first gold medal in the Olympic 26-miler.

In her Favor
Consolation prize for Suzy Favor Hamilton, who missed making her fourth Olympic team this month. She inherits last year's US title in the 1,500 meters that recently was taken away from doper Regina Jacobs as part of her four-year ban for taking the designer steroid THG. For Favor Hamilton, it's her fourth national crown in the event . . . Good omen for the US women's water polo team, which reaffirmed its Olympic favorite's role by beating Hungary in the World League Super Final after goalie Jackie Frank made two saves in the decisive penalty shootout. The Americans, who lost the gold medal in Sydney to Australia on a controversial goal in the final second, come in as world champs. Not so the US males, who finished sixth in their own Super Final, which is where they figure to end up in Athens . . . After a few late additions and deletions, the US canoe-kayak roster is finally final. It includes Andy Bussey (who earned a K2 spot when Jeff Smoke's partner Rami Zur opted for the K1), Bartosz Wolski (who was granted a late K2 berth with Angel Perez) and Benjie Lewis (who won a place in camp when Perez decided against competing in Athens) . . . Greece's Olympic baseball team is only "relatively" Greek. All but two of the 24 players are either Americans or Canadians with Hellenic roots, many no closer than great-grandparents. That bothers coach Dimitris Goussios, who threatened to quit coaching the team and vows to "fight this up until the last moment" to get at least four more homegrown players from the 17-team domestic league on the Olympic roster.
