News provider to launch trading platform

ADVERTISEMENT

Global news and information provider Reuters Group PLC unveils plans today to enable banks, brokers, and fund managers to buy and sell stocks and bonds over its desktop products in the first quarter of 2005. The electronic trading platform is the first major growth initiative announced by Reuters since chief executive Tom Glocer implemented a three-year restructuring plan in February 2003. The plan so far has focused primarily on cutting costs. The new service is a move to compete against rival Bloomberg LP in the fixed-income market, where Bloomberg has an established bond-trading platform and a strong presence. Another Reuters rival, Thomson Corp., launched an online corporate bond trading platform earlier this year. (Reuters)

Lawmakers agree to extend Net tax ban

US lawmakers agreed on a plan to ban taxes until 2007 on Internet access provided by companies such as Microsoft Corp.'s MSN and Time Warner Inc.'s America Online, according to two senators involved in talks with leaders in the House. As part of a compromise to win approval in the House, the Senate yesterday unanimously revised a bill it passed in April to extend the ban, said Senator George Allen, a Virginia Republican and chief sponsor of the moratorium. Since 1998, federal law has blocked state and local taxes on Internet connections for customers of such companies as AOL and EarthLink Inc. Since a series of temporary measures lapsed in November, state and local governments have stood pat, hesitant to impose new levies until Congress acted. (Bloomberg)

Stock fund inflows hit $15.4b last month

Investors added a net $15.4 billion to US stock funds in October, reflecting improved sentiment about the equity market, according to estimates by research firm Lipper Inc. of New York. More money was put in value equity funds than growth funds. Investors also deposited more in funds that hold domestic stocks rather than international shares, Lipper said. Among industry-specific funds, natural resources funds had the largest inflows during the month with $1.5 billion. Healthcare and biotechnology funds and science and technology funds each had net redemptions of $1.1 billion, the biggest of any industry. (Bloomberg)

Tobacco firms say US shouldn't get funds

The tobacco industry urged a federal appeals court to throw out a decision allowing the Justice Department to seek $280 billion from cigarette makers for allegedly misleading the public about the dangers of smoking. The government brought the case, now being heard in US District Court, under a 1970 civil racketeering statute originally designed to prosecute mobsters. Industry lawyer Michael Carvin told a three-judge panel of the US Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit that the civil RICO statute doesn't allow the government to recover money in the ongoing lower court case. (AP)

Etc.

EMC Corp. has started using Hon Hai Precision Industry Co. of Taiwan as its first supplier of finished equipment. Hon Hai is making data storage equipment selling for $5,000 that Hopkinton-based EMC is selling to small and medium-sized business clients . . . Lightbridge Inc. shares fell 11 percent to $4.48 after the Waltham maker of telecommunications software lowered its forecasts due to Cingular Wireless' acquisition of AT&T Wireless. In the nine months ended Sept. 30, revenue from AT&T Wireless totaled $16.6 million, or 17 percent of Lightbridge revenue. Cingular doesn't use Lightbridge software . . . Sonus Networks Inc., a Chelmsford supplier of voice-over-IP equipment, said it reached a deal with Chinese technology firm China Putian Corp. to sell its products and provide support services in China. Under the agreement, Putian Capitel Group, a unit of China Putian, will resell the full range of Sonus infrastructure hardware, and offer support and consultation services to China-based customers. Terms were not disclosed . . . Ron Bills, the president and chief executive of Bedford, N.H.-based Segway LLC, maker of the self-balancing, two-wheeled machines of the same name, is resigning after about a year on the job. No replacement has been named. He did not discuss his plans . . . Shares of ScanSoft Inc., the Peabody maker of voice recognition software, rose 9.5 percent to $3.81 after the company said Microsoft Corp. expanded an agreement to use its programs that convert text into speech. Terms were not disclosed. (Globe wire services)
