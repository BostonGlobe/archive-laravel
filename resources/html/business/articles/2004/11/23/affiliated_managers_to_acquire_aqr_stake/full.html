BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Affiliated Managers to acquire AQR stake

November 23, 2004

Affiliated Managers Group Inc., a US company that owns stakes in 18 money management firms, acquired an equity interest in AQR Capital Management, its fifth transaction this year. AQR, based in Greenwich, Conn., is a quantitative manager and hedge fund company that manages about $12 billion, Beverly-based AMG said in a statement. AQR's management will hold the remaining equity in the company. Terms of the acquisition weren't disclosed. AQR oversees $6.5 billion in hedge fund assets and also runs long-only international stock funds. AMG, whose affiliates include value managers Third Avenue Asset Management LLC and Friess Associates LLC, which runs the Brandywine funds, has been preparing to expand. In October, AMG said it might raise about $100 million under a forward equity sale agreement with Merrill Lynch & Co. and could use the proceeds to make other investments. (Bloomberg)

Kadant to cut 136 jobs at French unit

Kadant Inc., which manufactures paper making and recycling equipment, plans to eliminate 136 full-time jobs at its unprofitable Kadant Lamort unit in Vitry-le-Francois, France. The cuts, to be made during the next six months, will save $5 million to $7 million annually, Acton-based Kadant said in a statement. Severance pay and related expenses will add $6 million to $10 million in costs in the fourth quarter, the statement said. The cuts represent about 42 percent of a unit that employs 320 people, Kadant said in the statement. Kadant shares rose 19 cents to $19.24 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading. The shares have fallen 11 percent this year. (Bloomberg)

Wyeth, Boston Biomedical in pact

Wyeth Pharmaceuticals agreed to license Boston Biomedical Research Institute's patented immunotherapy technology for the treatment of Alzheimer's disease. In a press release, Boston Biomedical Research, of Watertown, said Wyeth will pay an upfront licensing fee and will pay patent costs for the technology. Additional milestone payments are possible, based on the issuance of certain Boston Biomedical Research patent claims, approval of a commercial product covered by issued patent claims and royalties on the product. Specific terms of the agreement weren't disclosed. Boston Biomedical Research is a not-for-profit institution dedicated to basic biomedical research. (Dow Jones)

THE NATION
More US homes have HDTV capability

The number of US households with a television set capable of getting high-definition channels has doubled in the past year, to 7 percent of the total, according to a new study by Leichtman Research Group Inc. of Durham, N.H. It estimates that by year's end, 10 million US households will have an HD-capable TV set, growing to 45 million by late 2008. In an indication of how HDTV is becoming more of a mass-market consumer phenomenon, the mean household income of HDTV owners has dropped to $80,000 from $95,000 last year, according to Leichtman, a leading market analysis firm that follows the cable, satellite, and broadband Internet markets. Among households with incomes over $75,000, 14 percent have a TV that can get HDTV programming when plugged into an over-the-air high-definition tuner or cable or satellite link. That compares to 5 percent of those with incomes under $75,000, Leichtman said. (Peter J. Howe)

Computer Associates said to name CEO

Troubled business software company Computer Associates International Inc. reportedly has selected longtime IBM executive John Swainson as chief executive. Swainson, an executive at rival International Business Machines Corp., where he has worked for 26 years, will replace interim chief Kenneth Cron, Newsday and The Wall Street Journal reported yesterday. "We currently have no comment for you on the CEO search," said Computer Associates spokeswoman Shannon Lapierre. A spokesman for IBM also refused to comment. Swainson, 50, is a Canadian native whose most recent responsibility at IBM has been for worldwide software sales. The software and storage company, based in Islandia, N.Y., has been beset by accounting woes that caused it to restate financial results from 2000 and 2001. (AP)

Citigroup to sell financing unit to GE

Citigroup Inc., the world's biggest bank, agreed to sell its business that provides lending and leasing to the trucking industry to General Electric Co. for about $4.4 billion as it sheds equipment financing units. Citigroup, based in New York, will record a gain of about $90 million as a result of the transaction, the company said in a statement. GE Commercial Finance, the business-to-business financing unit of GE, will keep the CitiCapital Transportation financial Services Group in Dallas and add 600 workers, it said in a statement. Citigroup has been selling its equipment leasing businesses, selling its UK-based medical, construction, and industrial unit July 28 to CIT Group Inc. GE chief executive Jeffrey Immelt is buying companies he thinks can help push profit growth to at least 10 percent annually starting next year, including leasing and business financing. Shares of Fairfield, Conn.-based GE declined 18 cents to $36.09 in New York Stock Exchange trading. Citigroup rose 2 cents to $45.17. (Bloomberg)

US dollar flirts with record low

The US dollar hovered near its record low against the euro, which traded at just more than $1.30 with markets cautious ahead of the Thanksgiving holiday. Despite the failure of a weekend meeting in Berlin of the world's top finance officials to send a signal of concerted action to halt the dollar's slide, the euro stayed below the record of $1.3074 reached last Thursday. It was quoted at $1.3038 in late New York trading, up from Friday's late rate of $1.3014. Concern over the US trade and budget deficits has been a major factor in the dollar's decline, and US Treasury Secretary John Snow said Sunday he stressed to his counterparts at the Berlin meeting that "the United States is dealing with its deficit." The dollar's slide has worried European officials who fear their fragile, export-led recovery will be hurt. Economists say President Bush has little incentive to support the dollar because its drop helps US exporters. (AP)

Loews to buy Gulf South Pipeline

Loews Corp. has agreed to purchase Gulf South Pipeline LP from Entergy-Koch LP for $1.14 billion. Gulf South Pipeline owns and operates an 8,000-mile natural gas pipeline, located on the US Gulf Coast. It is based in Houston with field offices in Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama and Florida. The system comprises about 6,800 miles of transmission pipeline, 1,200 miles of gathering pipeline and 68.5 billion cubic feet of working gas storage capacity. Loews, a New York holding company, said it sees Gulf South as "a highly attractive pipeline system," both as a stand-alone business and as a complement to its Texas Gas Transmission unit. Entergy-Koch is a venture of energy company Entergy Corp. and privately owned Koch Energy Inc., a unit of Koch Industries Inc. The transaction is expected close before the end of the year. Shares of Loews closed at $67.75, up $1.12, or 1.7 percent, on the New York Stock Exchange. (AP/Dow Jones)
