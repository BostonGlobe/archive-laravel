A brew with a boost

October 14, 2004

Page 2 of 2 -- Kallman said she knows beer well, and she knows how to sell it.

ADVERTISEMENT

In April, she was featured in Inc. magazine's "25 Entrepreneurs We Love" feature, sharing the limelight with savvy business stars like fashion designer Betsey Johnson, Apple Computer cofounder Steve Jobs, and pioneering rap record producer Russell Simmons.

She is a veteran in the beer industry. In 1984, Kallman and Jim Koch cofounded the Boston Beer Co., maker of Samuel Adams beer. She left the company in 1999 and, after a brief hiatus, started New Century Brewing Co. in Randolph. Its first product, Edison, was launched on Sept. 10, 2001.

New Century has only four employees, including Kallman, at its Randolph headquarters. The beer is brewed in Utica, N.Y., and distributed in Massachusetts and Atlanta.

Kallman said she hatched the idea for MoonShot at a Red Hot Chili Peppers concert last year. After tailgating with the Edison crew in the parking lot of the Tweeter Center in Mansfield, she drank a couple of Mountain Dew sodas, and enjoyed the boost the caffeine-laden soft drink provided.

After the concert, she called up her brewmaster, and asked him to come up with a recipe. The result was MoonShot.

Though MoonShot's patent is pending, not everyone agrees that combining a caffeine boost with a beer buzz is a good idea. "I'm afraid this would give people a false sense of security, like they can drink and they're not going to get drunk because of the caffeine," said Shari Sprong, a community health associate at the Greater Boston Center for Healthy Communities.

New Century unveiled MoonShot earlier this year. A hip, 170-seat restaurant on Main Street in Norwell, Mount Blue was one of the first restaurants to receive cases of it during a "soft launch" on Memorial Day weekend. It sells for $3.25 a bottle at the restaurant, and Mount Blue co-owner Jayne Bowe said it has piqued the interest of the younger crowd.

"We do well with it. A lot of kids come in when we have bands play, and they're intrigued," said Bowe.

"We're just getting started. It takes time. One bar, one restaurant, one package store at a time," said Kallman.

But Kallman will have to speed up her marketing campaign, because the big guys at Anheuser-Busch are tapping into the buzz around caffeinated booze. Starting next month, the St. Louis-based beer giant will begin rolling out "B-to-the-E," a canned brew infused with caffeine, guarana and ginseng.

MoonShot is sold in package stores across the southern suburbs, according to its website, www.moonshotbeer.com.
