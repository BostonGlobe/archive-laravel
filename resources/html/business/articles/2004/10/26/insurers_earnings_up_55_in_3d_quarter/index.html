BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Insurer's earnings up 55% in 3d quarter

October 26, 2004

Allmerica Financial Corp.'s third-quarter earnings rose 55 percent, and the property and casualty operations reported ''solid" pretax segment earnings for the period despite being hurt by catastrophe losses primarily related to hurricanes in the Southeast. The Worcester insurance and financial services holding company said earnings were $17.7 million, or 33 cents a share, for the quarter, up from net income of $11.4 million, or 21 cents a share, a year earlier. Total segment income after taxes was $19.6 million, or 37 cents a share, nearly flat from $19.7 million, or 37 cents a share, in the third quarter of last year. During the quarter, the company incurred gross catastrophe losses of about $99 million, primarily related to hurricanes in the Southeast. After reinsurance, pretax catastrophe losses were about $62 million, compared to $17 million in the year-ago period. (Dow Jones)

Vertex Pharmaceuticals narrows loss

Vertex Pharmaceuticals Inc. narrowed its loss for the third quarter ended Sept. 30, helped by increased HIV drug sales and stronger revenue from collaborative research and development projects. The Cambridge company reported a loss of $38.8 million, or 49 cents per share, smaller than the loss of $86.4 million, or $1.12, in the year-ago period. Excluding charges, the loss was $36.2 million, or 46 cents per share, just ahead of estimates for a loss of 48 cents from analysts surveyed by Thomson First Call. Vertex said results include charges of about $2.6 million from restructuring and retiring convertible notes during the period. Total revenue grew 70 percent to $26.8 million from $15.8 million last year, Vertex said. Collaborative research and development revenue climbed 62 percent to $22.4 million, while sales of HIV drugs more than doubled to $4.4 million. (AP)

Cytyc names chief financial officer

Medical device maker Cytyc Corp. said it hired former Modus International Inc. chief financial officer Timothy Adams to assume the same job. Adams will replace acting chief financial officer Leslie Teso-Lichtman. Adams will take the title of vice president and treasurer, in addition to CFO, and will begin effective Nov. 8. The Boxborough company said Teso-Lichtman will continue in the role of vice president and controller. (AP)

Harvard Business School gets $25m gift

Harvard Business School has received a $25 million donation in support of its doctoral programs from Hansjoerg Wyss, an entrepreneur who built Synthes Inc., a leading international medical device company. The gift, part of the school's $500 million capital campaign, is the largest ever given to a business school doctoral program, the school said. ''Strengthening doctoral education is central to Harvard University's commitment to building a pipeline of faculty who will educate new generations of students around the world," Harvard president Lawrence Summers said. (AP)

THE NATION

AOL to adopt Sender ID technology

America Online reversed course and said it would adopt a proposed technology from Microsoft Corp. designed to help beat back spam and other e-mail blights. AOL said it would use the technology, dubbed Sender ID, because Microsoft had offered a new version to an Internet standards body. The new version addresses AOL's concerns that it and others would have had to ''cast aside" much of the work they had done to develop e-mail sender authentication systems. Sender ID is a hybrid of a Microsoft technology and a similar technology known as Sender Policy Framework, or SPF, which AOL had championed and was testing. The technology, which aims to stop the common spammer and virus-writer tactic of forging, or ''spoofing," sender addresses, has been e-mail operators' best hope for a near-term e-mail authentication standard that could gain the mass adoption needed to make a dent in the problem. (Dow Jones)

Business server software release set

Microsoft Corp. plans a Dec. 1 release for the latest version of its server software that aims to give companies more secure instant messaging and other corporate communications tools. The standard version of Microsoft's Live Communications Server 2005 will start at around $750, said Taylor Collyer, senior director of product management, about the same as the previous version. An enterprise edition, which can be coupled with other servers to allow for many more users, will start at $3,000. Businesses that buy the new software also will be able to pay extra for the option of sending and receiving messages from Yahoo, AOL, and Microsoft's instant messaging systems, regardless of which brand the user is running. That add-on should be available in the first half of 2005. (AP)

Airline turns to board member for CFO

US Airways Group Inc. said it chose board member Ronald E. Stanley as chief financial officer, replacing David M. Davis, who is resigning to take a job with Kraton Polymers LLC. Stanley joined US Airways' board in May 2004 and served as chairman of the board's audit committee. Davis started at US Airways in April 2002 as vice president of financial planning and analysis, and was promoted to executive vice president and CFO last spring. The company also said George M. Philip, executive director of the New York State Teachers' Retirement System, was elected to the board. (AP)

Office Depot to sell Sirius radios

Sirius Satellite Radio Inc. said Office Depot Inc. will begin selling its radios at 900 US stores. Office Depot called the radios a complement to its existing small-business offerings and said it will carry a palm-sized Sirius radio and related accessories. Sirius's satellite radios offer more than 120 channels of programming. The company has about a quarter of the subscribers of larger rival XM Satellite Radio Inc. (AP)

Record industry, Spanish website settle

The operators of a Spanish-based website that sold music downloads have agreed to pay $10.5 million to settle a copyright infringement lawsuit brought by several recording companies. Sakfield Holding Company S.L., which ran Puretunes.com, agreed to pay the Recording Industry Association of America. Four individuals identified in court documents as the site's operators -- Daniel Rung, Michael Rung, Matthew Rung, and Wayne Rosso -- also agreed to pay a combined $500,000. Under the terms of the settlement, which were approved by US District Court Royce C. Lamberth, Sakfield and Puretunes' operators agreed not to engage in any activity that would violate music company copyrights. Puretunes went off-line in June 2003, less than two months after its launch. A month later, the top five recording companies and their labels sued Sakfield. (AP)

Universal, Warner form joint venture

Universal Music Group and Warner Music Group are teaming up to launch a firm to manage how the two major recording companies track artists' music royalties. The joint venture, tentatively named Royalty Services, should take over initial processing of song royalties for the companies within two years, Tsvi Gal, chief information officer for Warner Music Group, said. The recording companies will each own a minority stake in the venture, with software developer Exigen Group owning the rest. All three companies combined are investing roughly $30 million in the startup, according to record company sources familiar with the venture. (AP)
