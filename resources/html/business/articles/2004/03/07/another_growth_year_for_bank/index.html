Another growth year for bank

By Kathy McCabe  |  March 7, 2004

Eastern Bank on Tuesday will report a 12th straight year of record earnings, capping a year of growth and expansion around Greater Boston.

Eastern, with main offices in Lynn, will report its 2003 financial results during its annual meeting scheduled for 6 p.m. at the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston.

One highlight will be a 9.3 percent increase in loan growth, a bank spokesman said.

Increased demand for consumer loans, including for cars and home equity lines of credit, pushed total loans to $3.4 billion last year, compared to $3.1 billion in 2002.

''We had some increase in our commercial loans as well, but it was the consumer side that really drove things for us," said Joe Bartolotta, vice president of public relations.

Eastern, with $4.7 billion in assets, is one of the state's largest independent banks. On the North Shore, Eastern has 26 local branches and ranks among the region's largest private employers with 800 employees.

On Tuesday, Eastern CEO Stanley J. Lukowski and Richard Holbrook, the bank president, will present an overview of Eastern's performance in 2003.

Highlights included four new branch openings, the most by Eastern in a single year.

Two of the branches were in Shaw's supermarketsin East Boston and Sharon,while traditional branches opened in Dedham and South Wey-mouth.

Eastern this year plans three to five new branches around Greater Boston.

The bank this month will complete its acquisition of Middlesex Bank & Trust Co. in Newton, a single-branch bank owned by Westport National Bank in Connecticut. Eastern plans to open a branch in the Auburndale section of Newton this spring, Bartolotta said.

Two credit unionsmerge in Chelsea

Two Chelsea credit unions tied the knot last week.

Metropolitan Credit Union, one of the state's largest credit unions, merged on March 1 with Carmel Credit Union, an $18 million asset institution. The merger will boost Metropolitan's total assets to $538 million, the credit union said.

Robert M. Cashman, chief executive at Metropolitan, said the merger is a good fit.

''It's really a natural extension for us," he said in an interview. ''They're obviously much smaller than us, but we felt it was a good opportunity for us to grow our [market share] in Chelsea."

The merger gives Metropolitan 3,500 new customers and a prime new branch location.

Metropolitan converted Carmel's old office on Spruce Street into its sixth branch. The new location, with a drive-up window, inches Metropolitan closer to downtown Chelsea. The credit union's main office is on Revere Beach Parkway. Metropolitan, which has more than 100,000 members, also has branches in Peabody, Burlington, Framingham, and Lawrence.

Cultural landscapeprogram proposed

The Essex National Heritage Commission in Salem wants to help communities plan for growth and economic development by focusing on the region's cultural landscape.

The commission is working with the state Department of Conservation and Recreation to bring the Heritage Landscape Inventory Program to Essex County. Heritage landscapes include city streets, town centers, scenic roads, and historic sites that help define a community's character. Once identified, a landscape can help a community plan for growth, said Annie Harris, executive director of the commission.

''We live in an extraordinary area that is much richer in historic and natural resources than most other parts of the country," Harris said. They ''are integral to the qualify of life here and to our future economic development."

Workshops are scheduled to introduce community planners, business leaders, and residents to the concept. The schedule is March 16, 7 p.m., at Sawyer Free Library in Gloucester; March 17, 4:30 p.m., at Danvers Savings Bank, Danvers; and March 18, 7 p.m., at Amesbury Town Hall. For information, call 978-744-0444.

Monopoly inspiresdigital lottery game

A Rhode Island maker of electronic lottery games is betting on Monopoly, the storied board game once made in Salem by Parker Brothers.

Gtech Holdings Corp. plans to design a Monopoly lottery to be played on computer terminals in convenience stores, restaurants, bars, and on the Internet. It will also develop a game based on Battleship as part of a three-year licensing deal with Hasbro Properties Group, the company said.

Hasbro Inc. has produced Monopoly since acquiring Parker Brothers in 1991. Monopoly's possible availability as a lottery game, expected next fall, will mark a new chapter in the evolution of Monopoly. Closer to home, Winning Moves Inc. of Danvers developed a Monopoly card game and markets Monopoly 1935 First Deluxe Edition, a reproduction of the original game made in Salem.
