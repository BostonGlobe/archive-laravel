Citizens gets US OK for Charter One buy

August 17, 2004

Federal regulators granted Citizens Financial Group approval for its biggest acquisition to date, Charter One Financial Inc. of Ohio. The Federal Reserve Board said the pending merger likely would have a minimal impact on competition even in markets where the two banks overlap. When the $10.5 billion acquisition is complete, Citizens will be the 11th-biggest bank in the nation ranked by assets. Citizens is owned by the Royal Bank of Scotland, the fifth-biggest bank in the world. The acquisition still needs approval from Massachusetts banking regulators. Charter One's shareholders are expected to vote on the merger Aug. 23, and the bank expects to close the deal by the fourth quarter. (Sasha Talcott)

Covad launches Internet phone service

Covad Communications Group Inc., a top US independent provider of digital subscriber line high-speed Internet access, has launched a service in Greater Boston selling reduced-price "voice over Internet protocol" phone service to small and medium-sized businesses with 15 to 500 employees. Covad, based in San Jose, Calif., said it expects to begin selling a consumer Internet phone service next year. The business service costs $54 to $59 per month per line for unlimited US calling, which includes features such as Web-based access to voice mail, 10-way conference calls, and a way to instantly reassign office phone numbers from a computer screen. Covad has more than 515,000 DSL subscribers, including people who get its service as subscribers of AT&T and America Online, and is looking to expand into business telecom services. (Peter J. Howe)

Court OK's Bob's Stores liquidation plan

A bankruptcy judge confirmed the Chapter 11 plan of the liquidating retail operation that once ran the Bob's Stores chain. The confirmation was given by US Bankruptcy Judge Louis Kornreich in Wilmington, Del. Unsecured creditors may collect more than 70 cents on the dollar from the bankruptcy liquidation, which saw the sale of the Meriden, Conn.-based casual clothing and footwear superstore chain to TJX Cos. Confirmation came after clothing retailer TJX and the former Bob's Stores came to terms in a settlement that led to the withdrawal of a $3.9 million claim against the bankrupt company. A $15.7 million dispute with the IRS, which could cut into the projected returns for creditors, remains to be resolved, an attorney for the official committee of unsecured creditors said. The creditor attorney, Jay Indyke, said talks are underway toward resolution of the IRS claim at a reduced amount. (Dow Jones/AP)

Interactive Data buys Ill. company

Interactive Data Corp. agreed to purchase privately held FutureSource LLC for $18 million in cash. Assuming the transaction closes during the third quarter, Bedford-based IDC expects it to add to earnings per share by the end of 2005. The deal awaits shareholder and regulatory approvals. FutureSource, of Lombard, Ill., had revenue of about $20.2 million in 2003. Interactive Data said the acquisition will add to its eSignal division, specifically as an information provider to the futures brokerage industry. IDC provides highly specialized financial data that firms use to calculate mutual fund net asset values. (Dow Jones)

Putnam Investments alters holdings

Putnam Investments, the money management unit of Marsh & McLennan Cos., increased its holdings of Rite Aid Corp. and Xerox Corp. and sold shares of financial services companies Bank of New York Co. and Charles Schwab & Co. The Boston-based firm more than doubled its stake in Rite Aid, the third-biggest US drugstore chain, to 14.8 million shares last quarter, according to a regulatory filing. Putnam also raised its position in Xerox by 15 percent to 61.3 million shares, its largest purchase during the period. Putnam manages the $17.8 billion Putnam Fund for Growth and Income and the $14.1 billion Voyager fund. The SEC filing doesn't specify which stocks were bought or sold by which funds. A spokeswoman for Putnam had no comment. Putnam cut its stake in General Electric Co. by 32 percent to 53.7 million shares. The firm also shed shares of financial-services companies including Bank of New York, Charles Schwab, and MBNA Corp. This year, those stocks have fallen 15 percent, 24 percent, and 5.4 percent respectively, amid stagnant equity markets and rising interest rates. (Bloomberg)

Credit-card processor files suit in R.I.

PSW Inc. sued MasterCard, VISA, and two units of First Data Corp. in federal district court in Rhode Island over alleged antitrust violations and 11 other counts. PSW, a Providence-based credit-card processing company for adult-content websites, is seeking more than $240 million in damages from the companies. The complaint alleges the defendants "used monopoly power to employ policies and practices that unreasonably excluded competition and restrained trade in the credit card and credit card processing markets" PSW said. Officials for VISA could not be immediately reached. A MasterCard spokeswoman said the company had not been served the lawsuit yet so it couldn't comment. First Data Merchant Services and First Financial Bank, owned by Greenwood Village, Colo.-based First Data Corp., stopped providing services to PSW about 10 months ago. The company wouldn't comment on the specifics of the lawsuit. (Dow Jones)

Advisers seeking $613m compensation

Consultants, lawyers, and other advisers who helped rehabilitate MCI Inc. are seeking to be paid $613 million, the most ever in a bankruptcy case. KPMG LLP is asking the US Bankruptcy Court in New York to approve almost $173 million in fees and expenses for auditing the company's 2002 and 2003 financial results, according to court documents. Deloitte & Touche LLP is seeking $142 million, and Deloitte Consulting LLP is asking the court to approve $32 million in fees and expenses. Enron Corp., which plans to exit Chapter 11 protection at year end, may ultimately result in as much as $700 million in fees, while Global Crossing Ltd.'s advisers collected $174 million, the previous record. US Bankruptcy Judge Arthur Gonzalez will consider the fee and expense requests from about 45 firms retained by MCI, the second-biggest US long-distance telephone company, and its creditors at a hearing in New York in October. (Bloomberg)

Airline seeks to delay pension funding

US Airways Group Inc., which is desperately seeking new labor deals with its employees to avoid a second trip into bankruptcy, said it is seeking to conserve cash by delaying required payments to its machinists' and flight attendants' pension plans. The airline said it would ask the IRS for permission to defer about $68 million in funds for those plans, making those payments over the next five years, rather than the next 18 months. The airline said the move, if approved, would not affect the monthly payments due to pensioners. The airline said only the machinists and flight attendants are being targeted because they are the only two unions that still have a defined-benefit pension plan. (AP)

Safety gap widens between SUVs, cars

People riding in or driving sport utility vehicles were almost 11 percent more likely to die in an accident last year than people in cars, according to new federal data, The New York Times reported last night on its website. Figures from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration for 2003 show the widest recorded gap in safety between SUVs and passenger cars, according to the Times. The main reason for the safety gap, according to federal regulators, is that SUVs are more likely to roll over than cars. As a whole, US crash fatalities declined across the board in 2003 to the lowest levels in six years, the government figures show, with 42,643 people killed in traffic accidents, the news report said.
