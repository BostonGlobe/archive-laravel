MILLIS

Businessman proposes harness track

Due to bring plan to selectmen soon

By and Emma Stickgold, Globe Correspondent  |  April 4, 2004

Rhode Island businessman Louis Giuliano, who has long tried to operate a harness racetrack in Massachusetts, has approached the Board of Selectmen to discuss building one in Millis.

''It certainly would be an economic boost for the town," said Jeffrey Hardin, chairman of the Board of Selectmen. ''I have an open mind about it but I need to know more. . . . I certainly am not against it at this point."

Millis, where the property tax burden falls almost entirely on residents, has been struggling with how to boost its commercial tax base. Communities that are home to racetracks benefit not only from taxes but from host fees, which can amount to tens of thousands of dollars annually.

Hardin said Giuliano approached him in early March to discuss a horse track and is tentatively scheduled to come before the selectmen either this week or next.

Giuliano did not return calls for comment.

Giuliano has a long history of other track ventures in the state. Most recently, he has been trying to build a harness horseracing track in Foxborough.

Residents and officials there have been resistant to the idea. Foxborough Town Meeting last month rejected Giuliano's request for rezoning an area on Route 1, near Gillette Stadium, for a track.

Foxborough officials criticized Giuliano for seeking a racing permit from the state before discussing his plans with them. The town asked the state Racing Commission to rescind a temporary permit it granted to Giuliano.

According to Joseph Betro, chairman of the Racing Commission, Giuliano is scheduled to go before the commission on Wednesday to discuss the license, which Betro said is only valid for Foxborough and can't be used unless Giuliano gets all the necessary local approvals. The license represents a preliminary approval, which would require Giuliano to go before the commission again for an extension, Betro added, if everything else fell into place.

Because the temporary license is not transferable to other communities, Betro said, Giuliano would have to apply separately if he pursues a Millis track. Betro said he hadn't heard anything about Giuliano's plans for Millis.

Giuliano appears to be trying to get a better start with Millis officials.

Hardin praised Giuliano for bringing the proposal to selectmen before buying the land and getting further along in the project.

''I think he's going about it the right way," he said.

Hardin said Giuliano told him he would look for 50 acres in town on which to build a track, a search that could prove difficult.

''I don't know where he will find that land in Millis, although he might be able to do it by cobbling together a couple of pieces of land," Hardin said.

Hardin said traffic could prove to be a concern, but he waved off more general worries about gambling, saying lottery tickets are already sold in town.

Giuliano originally was involved in Plainridge Racecourse but had a dispute with Gary Piontkowski, who runs the track in Plainville, which is next door to Foxborough. There have been multiple lawsuits between Giuliano and about a dozen people and entities associated with Plainridge, according to David Rich, attorney for Ourway Realty, which owns Plainridge.

For example, testimony was heard last week and was to continue this week in a Suffolk Superior Court case, he said. And a federal lawsuit in which Giuliano accused several Plainridge associates of cheating him out of his stake of the track was dismissed in late December, but Giuliano is appealing, Rich said.

Asked if he has concerns about Giuliano's attempts to build tracks -- first in Plainville, then Foxborough, and now possibly in Millis -- Piontkowski said, ''I couldn't care less what Lou Giuliano does."
