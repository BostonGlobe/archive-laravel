BUSINESS IN BRIEF

Work at mall eased

April 24, 2004

The Somerville Board of Aldermen approved a zoning ordinance early yesterday that clears the way for construction to begin immediately at Assembly Square. The new zoning will allow Assembly Square Limited Partners to find new tenants for the nearly empty mall and build a 300,000-square-foot mixed-use Main Street-style development. It also will allow IKEA, the Swedish furniture retailer, to seek expedited approval to build office and residential space next to its planned home furnishings store abutting the mall. Members of the Mystic View Task Force, a citywide community group that has lobbied for a dense development with less retail, said they were disappointed by the vote. (Thomas Grillo)

Eaton bakery buyout final

The Canadian company Tim Hortons bought out most of the Bess Eaton bakery and coffee chain yesterday for $41.6 million, topping an offer by a competitor backed by Dunkin' Donuts. The deal includes 42 of Bess Eaton's 48 retail stores in New England. Paul House, president and chief operating officer of Tim Hortons, said the company plans to keep all 42 locations open and retain all employees. House also said Tim Hortons plans to add stores. The sale was finalized by Bankruptcy Court Judge Arthur Votolato Jr. after a day of bidding. A lawyer representing Bess Eaton Donut Flour Co. closed the bidding after BE Network Acquisitions LLC, a regional group of Dunkin' Donuts franchisees, kept its final offer as $40.8 million. Tim Hortons' offer includes a payment of $1.3 million to George Cioe, a former CEO of Bess Eaton, to settle a lawsuit he has against the doughnut chain. (AP)

THE NATION

Durables orders up 3.4%

Orders to factories for costly manufactured goods, such as cars and machinery, rose by a strong 3.4 percent in March, fresh evidence that America's economic recovery is bounding ahead. The sizable over-the-month increase in durable-goods orders came after an even better 3.8 percent advance in February, the Commerce Department reported yesterday. Durable goods are big-ticket items expected to last at least three years. The strength in March was broad-based and far exceeded the 0.7 percent increase that economists were forecasting. (AP)

Spitzer probes insurers

The office of New York Attorney General Eliot Spitzer, still in talks with mutual fund companies, has started investigations into business practices by the insurance brokerage industry. Three leading insurance brokers, Aon Corp., Willis Group Holdings Ltd., and Putnam Investments' parent company, Marsh & McLennan Cos., said yesterday that they have received subpoenas from Spitzer's office requesting information about compensation agreements they have with insurance underwriters. The investigation focuses on revenue sharing between insurance firms and brokerage firms that sell property and casualty insurance to corporate clients, according to industry sources. (Dow Jones/AP)

. . .Etc.

Inamed Corp., which lost a bid to return silicone breast implants to the US market, and its partner, Genzyme Corp. of Cambridge, won Food and Drug Administration approval of the Hylaform gel to treat wrinkles at the eyes and mouth. Genzyme chose Santa Barbara, Calif.-based Inamed to help market Hylaform. . . . Ad agency Arnold Worldwide Boston has been chosen by shoe and apparel maker Timberland Co. as its agency of record. The Boston ad firm wouldn't say how much the new account is worth, but it will be in charge of creating advertising and marketing campaigns for Timberland, of Stratham, N.H. The new ads will begin appearing in the fall. . . . Unitil Corp. yesterday asked the Massachusetts Department of Telecommunications and Energy to approve an increase in electric rates that would raise bills about 2 percent for most residential and small-business default service customers. The new rates, if approved, would be in place from June 1 through November. (Globe staff and wires services)
