Stanley Works profit skyrockets

April 26, 2004

NEW BRITAIN, Conn. -- The Stanley Works, which makes tools and hardware, Monday posted a nearly eightfold jump in first-quarter profit, helped by a big gain from the sale of a door unit and a 23 percent jump in sales.

ADVERTISEMENT

The nation's leading tool company earned $153.5 million, or $1.84 a share, for the latest period, compared with $19.2 million, or 22 cents a share, in the same quarter a year ago.

Excluding a $95 million gain from the March sale of its residential-door business, Stanley said it would have reported earnings of $58.5 million, or 70 cents a share.

That topped the average estimate of analysts surveyed by Thomson First Call for earnings, excluding items, of 64 cents a share.

In mid-March, the New Britain-based company had forecast first-quarter earnings of 63 cents to 67 cents a share.

Sales rose in the latest quarter to $778.6 million from $632.2 million.

Stanley also projected second-quarter earnings from continuing operations of 63 cents to 67 cents a share.

First Call is looking for second-quarter earnings, excluding items, of 66 cents a share.

Shares of Stanley Works fell 27 cents, or 0.6 percent, to close Monday at $44.53 on the New York Stock Exchange.
