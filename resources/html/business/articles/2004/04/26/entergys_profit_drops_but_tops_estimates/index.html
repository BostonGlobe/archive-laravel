Entergy's profit drops but tops estimates

April 26, 2004

NEW YORK -- Entergy Corp. said Monday its profit tumbled 47 percent in the first quarter, as low volatility in the energy market hampered trading.

Mild weather was also cited as an influence on the latest results.

The New Orleans-based energy distribution and utility company reported net income of $213 million, or 88 cents a share. In the same period a year ago, profits came to $400.9 million, or $1.73 a share, after a gain of $138.6 million, or 61 cents a share, due to a change in the accounting for nuclear plants taken out of operation.

Analysts surveyed by Thomson First Call had forecast, on average, earnings of 87 cents a share for the latest quarter.

Earlier this month, Entergy warned that first-quarter earnings would be pressured by mild weather and lower earnings from its energy-trading venture with privately held Koch Industries Inc. of Wichita, Kan.

Entergy, which had been taking substantially all of the profit from the venture, began splitting it 50-50 with Koch on Jan. 1, in accordance with the terms of the original arrangement.

Entergy's revenue rose more than 10 percent, to $2.25 billion from $2.04 billion. Revenue at the company's competitive businesses increased 31 percent, while domestic electric and natural gas revenue rose by a modest 6.2 percent and 4.5 percent, respectively.

The energy commodity services unit, which includes contributions from Entergy-Koch and Entergy's non-nuclear wholesale assets, posted earnings of $9.8 million, down sharply from $93.8 million last year, mainly because of the loss of income from the Entergy-Koch venture.

Utility operations reported earnings of $128.5 million, up 23 percent.

Entergy reaffirmed its full-year earnings projection of $4.10 to $4.30 a share. In 2003, the company had net income of $950.5 million, or $4.01 a share, on revenue of $9.19 billion. First Call's projection for full-year earnings is $4.22 a share.

Shares of Entergy closed Monday at $56.24, down 10 cents, or 0.2 percent, on the New York Stock Exchange.
