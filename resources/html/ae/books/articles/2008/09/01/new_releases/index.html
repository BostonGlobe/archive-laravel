By Stephanie Schorow
Globe Correspondent / September 1, 2008

DVDS - TV
The Big Bang Theory, season 1
Geek chic goes mainstream with this CBS sitcom about two brilliant yet oddball physicists (Johnny Galecki as Leonard and Jim Parsons as Sheldon) whose techno-centric lifestyles are disrupted when blond bombshell Penny (Kaley Cuoco) moves next door. Rather than simply make fun of geeks nonstop, however, the series celebrates the wacky joys of nerdom. Leonard and Sheldon seem to have a richer life than the more "normal" Penny. Still, Leonard sees whitebread Penny as his Princess Zelda object of desire and their budding relationship suffers all the ups and downs of typical sitcom affairs. Season 2 begins Sept. 22.

CDS - Classical
Joshua Bell
The Four Seasons (Sony Classics)
One of the most popular and one of the most cloyingly overplayed pieces in the classical repertoire is Vivaldi's set of four violin concertos "The Four Seasons," composed in 1723. Superstar violinist Joshua Bell believes he can wrest new life from this old standard, accompanied by musicians of the Academy of St. Martin in the Fields. Indeed, the mop-topped Bell does baroque with the energy of a young rock star. The CD includes another baroque classic, "The Devil's Trill" by Giuseppe Tartini.

Video Games - Action
Lost in Blue: Shipwrecked
Wii (Konami)
The "Lost in Blue" series moves to the Wii platform, shifting this game of survival to the interactive, 3-D mode. Players find themselves stranded on a mysterious island with strange attributes and inhabitants and must either find a way out or make a new life there forever. If it weren't for the Smoke Monster and those pesky others - no wait, that's mixing in our other favorite deserted island adventure, TV's "Lost."

Books - Fiction
The Toss of a Lemon
Padma Viswanathan (Houghton Mifflin)
It's a world hard for us modern American women to imagine: a young Brahmin girl in India is married at 10 and widowed at 18 with two children. She must shave her head and touch no one from dawn to dusk. For the next 60 years, she leaves the family compound only three times. Yet the impact of her confined life is profound on her children and grandchildren. This is the story told in "The Toss of a Lemon" by new author Padma Viswanathan, who was inspired by her own grandmother's stories. Viswanathan will speak about the novel on Friday at 7 p.m. at the Harvard Book Store.
