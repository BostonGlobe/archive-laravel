Film critic Ty Burr took your questions

August 22, 2008
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

Heading to the theater this weekend? Boston Globe film critic Ty Burr was online Aug. 22 at 1 p.m. to field your questions and comments about new and upcoming releases, and more. Miss the chat? Catch up here.

The transcript follows.

Normal View Title

Ty_Burr: Hello, peoples. Ty Burr here from the Globe, ready to talk movies. Anyone out there?
Ty_Burr: Send in your comments, questions, broadsides, whatever, and I'll do my best to answer them or fend them off.
Ty_Burr: (Though wouldn't we all rather be outside?)
David_Duchovny__Guest_: I heard the final Harry P installment (adaptation of book 7) will be released in two parts --is this true, and when?
Ty_Burr: YEs, David, it's true. The first part in summer of 2010, the second in spring of 2011. I'm sure there's already a line outside the theater...
Mikey__Guest_: Is the X-Files movie reallt that bad? Ebert liked it.
Ty_Burr: Yes, Mikey, it's really that bad. Slow, ponderous, unexciting -- just drab filmmaking all around.
Ty_Burr: Re Roger, I will forever praise what he has done to popularize film criticism over the years...
Ty_Burr: but it's my own personal opinion that I don't trust his reviews as much as I used to before his recent health problems.
cheesesteak__Guest_: Shame on you and Wes for "outsourcing" Clone Wars.
cheesesteak__Guest_: wifi-ing in the park ;P
Ty_Burr: Argh, I'm jealous, cheese. As for "CW," I just couldn't bring myself to be excitied about it, it was a busy week, and Russo, our DVD guy, is a Lucas fan from the get-go. It seemed like a logical choice.
kenshin__Guest_: This hasn't been a great summer for action-comedies. Pineapple Express was great fun when it was just Rogen and Franco acting high but the action scenes sucked. Kung Fu Panda had great action scenes but the rest of the movie was dreadfully boring. Does Tropic Thunder find a good balance between laughs and thrills?
Ty_Burr: Good question, kenshin. With both "Pineapple" and "Tropic," I went in with high expectations -- probably too high -- and was slightly disappointed. There are real laughs in both but also the sense in "Tropic" that the script isn't up to the inspiration level of the performances.
Ty_Burr: A few too many scenes just sat there. The ones that took off, really took off. But I think that's a problem with someone who comes out of sketch comedy, as Stiller has -- hard to maintain a consistent tone of lunacy throughout. But your mileage may vary.
cheesesteak__Guest_: Apology ... accepted Capt. Burr.
Ty_Burr: Thanks you, Boba Cheesesteak
Critic__Guest_: I find movies today very predictable - with Hollywood-type happy endings, dumbed-down or all special effects with no story. Any comments?
Ty_Burr: Any comments? No, just basic agreement, at least with mainstream studio fare. You have to look elsewhere for stuff that'll surprise you. Find a good indie video store and ask the clerks.
kenshin__Guest_: have movies ever not been like that?
Ty_Burr: Not since the industry took shape as an industry, in the late teens/early 20s.
Ty_Burr: It's a business, and for businesses to be profitable they have to be streamlined, make a consistent product.
Ty_Burr: And also, yes, good screenwriting has died the death in the last 20 years -- no longer are writers coming to Hollywood from the stage or literature but from film school and effects houses.
Ty_Burr: I've actually been watching a lot of early silents lately, for a project I'm working on. It's surprising to see how often they break the mold, mainly because the mold hadn't been made yet.
kenshin__Guest_: Tarantino? Charlie Kaufman?
kenshin__Guest_: those screenwriters are pretty good
Ty_Burr: I agree, and there are others, but I'd argue they're the exception rather than the rule.
Ty_Burr: They're also two of the few who pay attention to narrative structure (and toy around with it in interesting ways).
Critic__Guest_: Have you seen the recent movie IOUSA? Scary stuff.
Ty_Burr: Nope, missed it at Sundance and not sure when it's going to get a release here.
Ty_Burr: Have read very good things about it, like I need something more to get worried about.
moviegoer__Guest_: Hi Ty, how are you? Just curious if you've ever thought a film would have worked better as a TV show, or vise versa. Also, I want to see "Vicky Cristina Barcelona" tonight. How would you classify the humor in the film? I had a friend who loved the humor, and her mother didn't like it because of the humor. Thanks.
chattycath__Guest_: I saw "VCB" earlier this week -- aside: what an awful name for a movie -- and really liked it. But I just don't get the Scarlet thing?? Loved her in "Lost in Translation," but I don't understand what the fuss is all about. Rebecca Hall was enthralling -- Scarlet, eh, so-so. She always plays the sexpot.
Ty_Burr: Wow, how do you categorize humor? It's difficult, especially in this case, where the laughs arise from the characters and their response to the situations (rather than outside gags). I guess you'd call it character comedy or human comedy.
Ty_Burr: And interesting that Bardem and Cruz are able to walk the line between comedy and drama in the film much better than the two title characters -- who I think are much more thinly written.
Ty_Burr: I like the movie, too -- especially if you go in with low or no expectations. It's a nice bauble, and nice to see Allen so relaxed. As for Scarlett, I actually thought the movie used her well, more so than in the other movies she's made with Woody.
Ty_Burr: I liked the fact that Cristina wasn't very smart and it freed her in certain ways and hung her up in others.
bill_clinton__Guest_: how are they making a new terminator w/o arnold?
Ty_Burr: Well, it's the John Connor story -- the backstory (or forward story) to the first film. Christian Bale's playing Connor. (Whenever I hear that name, I hear Ahnuld saying it: "Jawn Cawnuh.")
Ty_Burr: You'll have to wait until next May to find out, though.
BostonRe__Guest_: Ty, as a comic book fan, do you think The Watchers will do well? That story is HARD to put on film.
Ty_Burr: You mean "Watchmen", Boston Re?
Ty_Burr: If so, I share your concern. It's a complex story with a lot of characters, and the tone is tricky -- a simultaneous celebration and subversion of the comicbook superhero
Ty_Burr: Though I think that the recent "Batman" movies and other films have primed the pump. I also worry that not enough people are familiar with the source comic.
BostonRe__Guest_: Yes sorry, The Watchmen
cheesesteak__Guest_: And it's made by the 300 guy....
Ty_Burr: That's right, Cheese, another reason to worry. Whatever you thought about "300" it wasn't exactly a movie of ideas, and "Watchman" IS a comic of ideas.
newbie__Guest_: I loved, loved, loved "Match Point" (and Scarlett's role), but I hated, hated, hated Scoop (and Scarlett's role). I could not keep my eyes open. Seriously. I actually have no idea what happened in the movie aside from Scarlett slowly in the grass (or maybe I dreamt that). I literally could not stay awake. That said, why do you think Woody always goes back to Scarlett?
Ty_Burr: Interesting question, newbie. I think it's as simple as some artists needing a muse to function. Allen has gone from actress to actress in a series of streaks: Louise Lasser, Diane Keaton, Mia Farrow, etc. It used to have a romantic component (and may still, though I don't want to think about that). But I do think Scarlett fills his need for a pretty and sympathetic actress to inspire him,
bill_clinton__Guest_: were a lot of people familiar with iron man? i'd never heard of it outside the black sabbath song
Ty_Burr: Oh, yeah, I think so. But I'm old enough to remember the Iron Man cartoon on TV. I know several people not as old as me who say IM was always their fave Marvel hero.
flightjkt__Guest_: Hi Ty - have you seen the new Coen Bros. movie yet? The trailer looks great...
Ty_Burr: "Burn After Reading." It does look funny, doesn't it? Also, really really silly. I'm hoping it doesn't go overboard and just float away like "The Ladykillers" did.
Ty_Burr: But, you know what? The bros have earned the right to goof off a bit.
Ty_Burr: (Doesn't mean we have to pay for it, though...)
mikef__Guest_: Hey Ty- thoughts on Ricky Gervais' upcoming films? Think he'll crack into the big time here? Seems so tough for British funnymen to actually make it in Hollywood these days (Steve Coogan, Simon Pegg) but I bet Gervais has the chops...
Ty_Burr: I'm curious, too, mike. Interestingly, all three have movies coming out. Coogan's in "Hamlet 2," opening today, which some people love and I found hard to take. Pegg's trying to go more mainstream Hollywood (sort of) in the upcoming "How to Lose Friends and Alienate People."
Ty_Burr: And then Gervais in "Ghost Town" -- what looks like a total studio gimmick comedy (man briefly dies, comes back to life and sees all the ghosts of recent departed).
Ty_Burr: Funny to watch how these very talented men twist themselves into their idea of what American audiences want.
Neil_Bergman__Guest_: Hi Ty, what's the buzz on Body of Lies?
Ty_Burr: The Leo DiCaprio, Russell Crowe, Ridley Scott movie about the CIA op tracking down Al Qaeda. Haven't seen it, don't know anyone who has.
Ty_Burr: Some good talent there, obviously.
Ty_Burr: Saw an interesting one yesterday in the same basic genre -- "Traitor," with Don Cheadle as a Sudanese-American terrorist and Guy Pearce as the FBI agent on his tail.
Ty_Burr: Solid little movie with a couple of very good twists.
Ty_Burr: In general, I'm wary about Hollywood movies tackling the current political landscape with genre stories. "The Kingdom" being a good example. But we'll see.
cheesesteak__Guest_: Saw a trailer for Body of Lies, I couldn't tell which was worse, Crowe's take on a Virginia? accent or DiCaprio's prosthetic chinbeard.
Ty_Burr: LOL
Ty_Burr: Wait'll you see Viggo Mortensen's Van Dyke chin extension in "Appaloosa," cheese.
Ty_Burr: It looks like rebar sticking out of his face.
flightjkt__Guest_: The other movie I'm really looking forward to seeing is the "Waltz With Bashir" animated doc - did you see it at Cannes & do you think it'll be distributed on a nationwide level despite the subject matter?
Ty_Burr: Yes, I did see it at Cannes, flight, and liked it very very much: It's an animated film (a mixture of rotosa=cope and Flash animation, actually) about the filmmaker's guilt and memories of being in the Israeli Army during the Sabra massacre of 20 years back.
Ty_Burr: Being nearby and doing nothing. He goes and looks up all his old army buddies and tries to come to terms with their culpability.
Ty_Burr: The animation works really well -- plays like a very good graphic novel, reminded me of Joe Sacco's "Palestine".
Ty_Burr: I do know it's getting a release stateside, just not sure when.
Ty_Burr: Ent. Weekly mentions a Dec. 26th release date, but that may be NY/LA only.
Neil_Bergman__Guest_: Are you looking forward to reviewing High School Musical 3?
Ty_Burr: AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!
Ty_Burr: No, not really.
Ty_Burr: I have daughters, so I'll be seeing it. They're properly scornful of Zac Efron, but still -- it's part of the middle school social contract.
Neil_Bergman__Guest_: Is Penelope Cruz taking her game to the next level with Volver & this Woody A flick?
Ty_Burr: Well, Neil, I think she's always been a much better actress than she has gotten credit for. Her looks have worked against her getting critical respect (from audiences more than critics, ironically) and then English isn't her native language, so it has taken her some time to master acting in it.
Ty_Burr: I do think she's the real deal, am glad she steals "VCB" so American audiences get that.
kayla__Guest_: hi ty... coming late to the chat so i apologize if you've addressed this... what's up with warner bros pushing the harry potter movie (#6) from november to next july?!
Ty_Burr: Funny you ask that, kayla -- I've got a Sunday piece coming up on the whole release-date game of Twister.
Ty_Burr: In this case, WB seems to have felt they needed another Summer 09 blockbuster -- they have Terminator Salvation for Memorial Day, but last year's writer's strike has left the pipeline looking pretty empty. So they decided to use HPVI as a midsummer tentpole.
Ty_Burr: I guess they've made enough money this year with "Dark Knight" :)
BostonRe__Guest_: Ty, even my 10 year old niece is sick of HS Musicals
Ty_Burr: Yeah, but how does she feel about the Jonas Brothers?
Neil_Bergman__Guest_: When is the DeNiro/Pacino hitting the theatres?
Ty_Burr: Righteous Kill? Soon - September 12th. I'm guessing they're not hoping for Oscar noms.
Ty_Burr: I've actually lost faith in both those guys. I do hope they surprise me.
JediFonger__Guest___Guest_: hi Ty! =D. came in a bit later. question about the project u r working on. i'm quite the "silent film expert" despite my apprehension for new affairs. it is my opinion that most of the visual film techniques that we know of today have been achieved by the time sound came around in the late 20s. do u agree with that assessment?
Ty_Burr: Yes, I'd agree with that. You see matte shots as early as 1909, earlier even. DW Griffith worked out the shot language we still use to tell stories: establishing shots, close-ups, cross-cutting, etc.
Ty_Burr: Where there has been a quantum shift is in the area of digital effects. Everything analog was rooted in techniques developed in the silent era, but once you bring in the computer, it's a whole new ballgame.
Ty_Burr: Which is why they're making a sequel to "Tron," I guess :)
kayla__Guest_: does ellen page have anything exciting coming up? do you think she can play someone other than a sarcastic, older-than-her-years type character?
Ty_Burr: I do. I think she's a really smart young actress and we're hopefully seeing the beginning of a brilliant career. That said, Hollywood loves to pigeonhole a new talent. If she's really smart, she (and/or her agent) will establish that she can do lots of different types of roles early on.
Ty_Burr: Upcoming, she's got "Whip It!" -- Juno does roller derby, not a good sign -- and a mystery thriller called "Peacock" starring Cillian Murphy. In 2009, she's slated to play Jane Eyre, though -- *that* should be interesting.
JediFonger__Guest_: re: silent film. even 2-color technicolor process was used in Douglas Faibanks 1926 THe Black Pirate. re: FX. modern CG FX are based on filmmaking principles established in the 1920s. FW Murnau and Fritz Lang and even George Melies have already done "CG" ad nauseum. today's 3D cg stuff pales in comparison.
Ty_Burr: Ooh, that's a big long argument -- too long for today. I'd be interested to hear you back that up.
Ty_Burr: Though I agree "Metropolis" hasn't aged a whit in terms of special effects.
Neil_Bergman__Guest_: what's been the most disapointing film for you thus far this year?
Ty_Burr: The Happening. And my hopes weren't even that high!
JediFonger__Guest_: i'll send u an email.
Ty_Burr: do that, jedi
bostonbulldogs__Guest_: Will "The Dark Knight" get serious Oscar attention?
Ty_Burr: I'm not sure, bb. A supporting actor nomination for Ledger, for sure. Maybe a directing nomination or writing. Best Picture nomination is likely but depends on how successful the regular "OScar season" movies are.
Ty_Burr: And it'll sweep the technical nominations, obviously.
Ty_Burr: It's 2:02, and I have to skedaddle. Good chat today, nice to talk about silents and Israeli political animated movies!
Ty_Burr: Thanks, guys -- have a good end of summer and see you in a few weeks.
