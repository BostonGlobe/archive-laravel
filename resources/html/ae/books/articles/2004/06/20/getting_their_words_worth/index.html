Getting their words' worth

Writers reveal their current choices for rewarding reading

June 20, 2004

Carl Dennis,

author of ''Selected Poems: 1974-2004"''What Narcissism Means to Me, by Tony Hoagland. The poems in this book manage to be both skeptical and engaged at the same time."

DBC Pierre,author of ''Vernon God Little: A 21st Century Comedy in the Presence of Death"

''La Silla del Aguila (The Seat of the Eagle), by Carlos Fuentes. A swashbuckling novel about a president on the roller coaster of Mexican government."

Susan Orlean,

author of ''The Orchid Thief"

'''Vernon God Little, by DBC Pierre. I had to tear it out of my husband's hands because I was jealous -- I've never heard him laugh out loud so often, even when he's reading my writing."

Michael Chabon,

author of ''The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay"

''I am rereading [Raymond Chandler's] The Big Sleep (a lot less hard-boiled than I remember, almost whimsical in places), concurrently with The I. L. Peretz Reader [by I. L. Peretz] (this great Yiddish writer was unknown to me before now but has already become of one my favorites), and Against Culture: Development, Politics and Religion in Indian Alaska [by Kirk Dombrowski]. It's all research for my new novel, if you can imagine that."

Erik Larson,

author of ''The Devil in the White City"

''I just finished Straight Man, by Richard Russo, and absolutely loved it. I'm halfway through The Blind Man of Seville, by Robert Wilson. Very good, very dark."

Laura Hillenbrand,

author of ''Seabiscuit: An American Legend"

''Three Junes, by Julia Glass. This is a quietly lovely novel chronicling the lives of three people in the month of June in three different years. I listened to this book several months ago, but it continues to resonate in my memory."

Tony Hoagland,

author of ''What Narcissism Means to Me"

''I'm reading a great book of poems called The Brass Brouhaha Girl, by Adrian Blevins. I'm also proud to say that I am in the last hundred pages of War and Peace [by Leo Tolstoy], which has been wonderful."

Gao Xingjian,

author of ''Buying a Fishing Rod for My Grandfather"

''Nicolas Baverez's La France Qui Tombe, a trenchant and foreboding analysis of France's social problems. And a selection of late Ming Dynasty prose writings called Wan Ming Xiaopin Jingcui (Gems of Late Ming Prose), which brilliantly portrays the lives of the wealthy elite of those times and the ruthless intrigues people resorted to for political gain."

Atul Gawande,

author of ''Complications: A Surgeon's Notes on an Imperfect Science"

''I'm on a Philip Roth kick. Just finished his Nathan Zuckerman novel The Ghostwriter. It was an intriguing book, but against American Pastoral and The Human Stain -- two masterpieces -- a mere diversion. Now on to Sabbath's Theater."
