?SAMMY KEYES AND THE BACKSTAGE CLASH?

The trouble with big fans

Chapter four of a children's story in eight parts

By Wendelin Van Draanen  |  October 19, 2004

The story so far: Marissa and her friend Sammy sneak into the Town Center Mall to see Marissa's favorite band, The Boyz, shoot a video. In a closet, they find the head of mall security bound and gagged by a couple of females he refers to as "Amazons." In exchange for a promise from the two not to tell anyone he was kidnapped by girls, he agrees to pretend the two friends are reporters for their school paper and lets them go backstage. There, the Boyz are squabbling over who will get to sing solo in the video, and a woman named Vanessa appears to be in charge. One of the Boyz, Ace, asks the girls if they saw the "really big girls" in the corridor, and Marissa forgets her promise. "You mean the Amazons?" she asks, and quickly realizes her mistake.

`You -- you saw them?" Vanessa asks, zipping in closer to Marissa and me. "Well we," Marissa catches herself and looks at me all wide-eyed, like, Oh no! What do I say now?

Everyone's staring at us. The Boyz, their parents, even the record execs. Toby comes forward, saying, "Those knockouts you were talking about? They're for real?"

Jackson looks at Ace. "I thought you were just jivin' us, man."

"Who are they?" Marissa asks.

Vanessa's eyebrows knit together, and for the first time since we entered the room she looks worried. She takes a deep breath and rasps, "They must be professional stalkers."

"Professional stalkers?" I ask. "You're saying they get paid?"

Her head snaps to face me. "No! I am most definitely not saying that. I'm saying they're apparently very good at what they do, that's all."

"And what they do is stalk Ace?" I ask. "Or will any old celebrity do?"

"Who are you people?" Vanessa says, checking her watch. "Of course I mean Ace! Where have you been? Girls are crazy for him!"

Jackson glowers and cuts a look at Toby, who shrugs, shakes his head, and looks down.

Then Ace shoots Marissa a quick pucker-up, which makes me want to deck the punk. He may think he's the hottest thing since jalapenos, but there's no way I'm going to let him mess with my friend.

"So?" Ace's dad asks, getting things back on track. "Where did you say you saw them?" Then he turns to the Ponytail and says, "Remember those love-sick fans I told you were after Ace?"

The Ponytail nods, then asks, "Did you call them Amazons?"

I glance at Marissa, then say, "Uh, we actually only heard about them."

"From?" the Ponytail asks.

I shrug. "You know, people outside."

"Ah," he says.

"It's true!" Ace says to the Ponytail. "They're Amazons, and they follow me everywhere I go! They're crazy!"

"Crazy for Ace," Vanessa says with a laugh. "I'm not sure what would happen if they ever got ahold of him. Apparently these are big, big fans!"

A man pops his head inside the conference room. "They're ready for you!" he calls, and all of a sudden everyone is collecting things and hustling for the door.

Marissa and I follow behind the others, but as we approach the doorway, I stop dead in my tracks.

"What?" Marissa asks.

I sniff the air. "Do you smell that?" I whisper. "Sulfur? Rotten eggs?"

She sniffs, too, and her whole face pinches up. "Oooh, gross! Who farted?"

"Who farted?" I blink, then charge out the door, looking up and down the corridor.

"What are you doing?" Marissa asks.

"Is that guard boss around here anywhere?"

She looks, too. "No."

I hurry up the corridor, following The Boyz and their entourage.

"Sammy!" Marissa whispers, chasing after me. "Why are you spun up all of a sudden?"

"Because," I whisper back, "that sulfur-egg smell? I also smelled it in the supply closet." Marissa doesn't say anything so I add, "Where we found the guard boss?"

Finally she whispers, "So you're saying . . ."

"So I'm saying that one of the people we were just in that conference room with was also in the supply closet tying up the guard boss!"

Her face is all scrunched up. "Because you recognize the smell of their farts?"

I shrug. "Eau de Pee-yuu?"

"Oh, please! And besides," she says, nodding her nose at the group in front of us, "the guard boss said he was tied up by Amazons and none of them are what I'd call Amazons."

"True. But I know what I smelled. It's a very distinctive odor."

"So what are you going to do? Sniff `em out?"

I laugh. "I think I'll leave that up to the guard boss."

We follow the others through a corridor door and into a curtained-off area inside the rotunda. We can hear a big crowd out in the mall, and when Marissa and I peek through the curtains, we both say, "Holy smokes!" From store to store, from the barricades in front of the stage to as far as we could see, the mall is packed with teenage girls.

Marissa whispers, "I can't believe this is the mall," and she is right. The stage looks like a real stage with big speakers, lights, and mike stands wrapped in tons of scarves. The guards keeping kids back look like real concert security, and the kids in the crowd have signs reading THE BOYZ RULZ and WE LOVE THE BOYZ and HE'S MY ACE OF HEARTS.

All of a sudden Vanessa pulls us back, saying, "I don't really know why you girls are here, but do me a favor: Stay out of the way and clear of this aisle."

"Where can we watch from?" Marissa asks.

"Over here," she says, then leads us around back to the other side of the stage where we can't see diddly-squat.

The minute she's gone, Marissa says, "I'm not staying here!" and moves so she can see The Boyz fidgeting in the wings, getting ready to go onstage.

Now, Marissa may be into watching The Boyz, but personally, I find everything else that's going on much more interesting. Even though I told Marissa that I'd leave the sniffing out to the guard boss, I can't help wondering which of the people in the conference room might be in cahoots with Amazons. Who had wanted the guard boss out of the way? And why? Had he seen something? Heard something?

Smelled something?

Well, OK. Forget that. But somebody gagged him and tied him up and tossed him in a closet. If it was someone who already had access to The Boyz, what was the point?

So while Marissa is watching Ace, Toby, and Jackson, I focus on the people backstage. Evan has his headphones on, and is mouthing words as he dances. I am actually amazed: Toby's little brother has moves!

The Ponytail has his eye on Vanessa as she checks her watch and then ducks out into the corridor. Ace's dad is trying to use his cellphone, but apparently can't get through because he looks very irritated, then finally snaps it closed and puts it away. Toby and Jackson's moms are acting like the Gossip Girls, their heads together, their eyes shifting around.

Then someone onstage announces, "Helllllllloooooo, Santa Martina!" and suddenly Vanessa is back, fluttering all over The Boyz, giving them last minute instructions, shooing Ace's dad away.

The announcer goes on. "As you all know, this is a video shoot, so The Boyz will be performing one of their songs several times. We need you to stay energized! Create a vibe! Sing along! And just because this is a video shoot, it doesn't mean the boys will be lip-synching. As a matter of fact, we're gonna mix it up a little for you! You will have the bonus treat of hearing each of the boys get two turns at singing lead, so help `em out! Show them you love `em. Keep it pumpin' out there!"

The crowd cheers and whoops while the announcer gets some instruction from a man at the mixboard. Then the announcer says into the microphone, "And now ladies and gentlemen, they've been called `The Backstreet Boys from uptown,' `more in sync than 'N Sync.' `Talented.' `Smooth.' And just plain `Hot'! Please welcome Ace, Toby, and Jackson -- The Boyz!!!!"

The mall goes wild as music starts pumping through the sound system. Girls are screaming. Shouting. Wailing.

Marissa grabs my arm and jumps up and down, saying, "Isn't this exciting? Isn't this so exciting?"

We can't see that much bigger excitement is lurking in the wings.
