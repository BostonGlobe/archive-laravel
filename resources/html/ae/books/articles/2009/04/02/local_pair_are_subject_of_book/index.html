Local pair are subject of book

Marilyn Cloran of Swampscott entered ''Shadow at Sunset'' in the Greater Lynn Photographic Association contest.
By Wendy Killeen
Globe Correspondent / April 2, 2009
  • Email|
  • Print|
  • Single Page|
  • |
Text size – +

PROMISING FUTURES: Since it opened in 2004, KIPP Academy Lynn, a charter middle school, has become known for its work with students from lower-income families, preparing them for college and life. But what do Dave Levin and Mike Feinberg have to do with it?

Described as "two very entertaining, pushy, drama-prone, caring young men" by author Jay Mathews, they are the founders of KIPP, the Knowledge is Power Program and the subject of Mathews's new book, "Work Hard. Be Nice. How Two Teachers Created the Most Promising Schools In America."

Mathews, who covers education for the Washington Post, will visit KIPP Academy Lynn to discuss his book from 1 to 4 p.m. Tuesday. The public is invited to meet Mathews and visit the school's classrooms.

The Lynn academy is one of 66 KIPP schools in 19 states and Washington, D.C. It serves many students who enter the school below grade level. Students in the Lynn academy are 56 percent Hispanic, 24 percent African-American, and 20 percent white.

While each KIPP school decides its own curriculum, Mathews says the emphasis is on lots of movement, games, chants, songs, and real-world exposure through field trips. In addition, he said, there is a focus on hard work, good behavior, and going to college.

Mathews is the author of six previous books and created Newsweek's annual rankings of the country's best high schools. He also is a recipient of the Benjamin Fine Award for Outstanding Education Reporting.

Call 781-598-1609 or visit www.kipplynn.org.

PIANO PRODIGY: Kadar Qian, 11, of Westford, is the youngest pianist to win a competition sponsored by the Quincy Symphony Orchestra.

He performs the Chopin Concerto No. 2 in F Minor with the orchestra at 2:30 p.m. Sunday at North Quincy High School. He also is the recipient of a $1,000 scholarship.

Qian has studied piano since age 4 with Ronald Kmiec of Carlisle. He had his first solo recital in February and has appeared with others performers at Carnegie Hall in New York and Jordan Hall in Boston.

Since he was 9, he has competed in eight concerto competitions. In addition to winning the Quincy competition, he placed second in the 2009 Nashoba Youth Orchestra Competition.

Qian takes a piano seminar course and chamber music classes at the New England Conservatory and has performed in contemporary and chamber music concerts at the school. A fifth-grader at the Norman E. Day School in Westford, he plays the violin and is first chair in the school's orchestra. He's also a member of the school's math team.

A WORLD OF PHOTOS: The Greater Lynn Photographic Association presents The International, a digital photo and slide competition with entries from around the world, in Danvers Saturday and in Lynn Monday and Tuesday. There are between 80 and 100 such salons held around the world each year. The Lynn event is the largest of four in the Northeast. It generally receives entries from 40 to 50 countries and 45 states. Images are reviewed by a panel of three judges and the winners are showcased in a projection slide presentation, choreographed to music. Saturday's presentation is at 3 p.m. at Danvers High School. Admission is $7. The presentations Monday and Tuesday begin at 7:30 p.m. at the association, 500 Boston St., Lynn, Admission is $5. Visit www.greaterlynnphoto.org.

HOSPICE VOLUNTEERS: Merrimack Valley Hospice in Lawrence is seeking volunteers and offering training. Volunteers help in a variety of ways, including providing companionship to terminally ill patients and assisting families. They also are needed for administrative tasks and fund-raising. The process begins with an informational meeting Monday at 360 Merrimack St., Lawrence. Sessions run through June in Lawrence and North Andover. Call 978-552-4522 or e-mail smeehan@homehealthfoundation.org.

LIVE IN GLOUCESTER: Jimmy Dunn, who began his comedy career in Gloucester, returns Saturday to headline a benefit show to raise money for school scholarships. The event benefits the Lieutenant Colonel Jack A. Lucido Memorial Scholarship Foundation. Established in 1988 by the friends and family of Lucido, the foundation has awarded more than $70,000 in scholarships to graduates of Gloucester High School. It expects to present four scholarships to members of the class of 2009. Dunn, who recently drove a cab in a series of Olympia Sports commercials, is best known for his sports humor, but also explores a range of other subjects. The comedy night is at 8 p.m. at the Old Timers Tavern, 14 Rogers St., Gloucester. Tickets are $20. Call 978-922-5956.

WHO'S WHAT WHERE: Moonsu Han, an economics professor at North Shore Community College, has been elected to the board of the Massachusetts Council on Economic Education. He is the first representative of a community college chosen to be a member of the board. . . . Abigail C. Davies of Newburyport is one of four students from Phillips Exeter Academy in Exeter, N.H., to reach the semifinals of the USA Biology Olympiad, the country's premiere biology competition for high school students. Davies, a senior, and the other students learn this month whether they are among the 20 highest-scoring students from around the country who are invited to the finals, which are held in Virginia in June.
