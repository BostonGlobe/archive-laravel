January 30, 2006

CDs

POP
Barry Manilow
The Greatest Songs of the Fifties
Arista

Get ready to swoon. The crooner who gave us ''Mandy" and ''Copacabana" is back, reinterpreting classic '50s standards in his signature style. This collaboration with legendary producer Clive Davis includes familiar favorites like ''Unchained Melody," and ''Beyond the Sea," as well as Manilow's new takes on songs forever associated with the artists who recorded them, including Elvis Presley's ''Are You Lonesome Tonight?" and Tony Bennett's ''Rags to Riches." Plus, the bonus DVD includes an interview with Manilow and intimate behind-the-scenes footage of him at work in the recording studio.

DVDs

ANIMATION
Corpse Bride
Building on the popularity of his beloved cult favorite, ''The Nightmare Before Christmas," director Tim Burton returns with another darkly romantic stop-animation tale featuring a whimsical score by longtime collaborator Danny Elfman. The story follows a shy groom, voiced by Johnny Depp, who accidentally marries a corpse bride (Helena Bonham Carter) rather than the lovely young woman (Emily Watson) he is supposed to wed. Much to his surprise, he finds that he enjoys his time in the underworld with his new bride. When he begins to fall in love with her, he finds himself torn between the conflicting desires in his heart.

ACTION
Mega Man Maverick Hunter X
Sony PSP
(Capcom)
This remake of the original Super Nintendo Entertainment System game boasts loads of improvements, from new environments to fresh renderings of its 3-D character models. Plus it includes an animated film that fills in background on the characters' world and offers insight into plot details that occurred before the first game. Help the robot X, who was designed to benefit mankind, and his buddy Zero, as they attempt to stop the nefarious Sigma from blotting out humanity.
