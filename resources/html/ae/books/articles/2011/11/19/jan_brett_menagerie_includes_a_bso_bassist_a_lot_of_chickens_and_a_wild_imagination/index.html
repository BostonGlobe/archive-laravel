Jan Brett's menagerie

Author shares her life with a BSO bassist, a lot of chickens, and a wild imagination

By Linda Matchan
Globe Staff / November 19, 2011

NORWELL - Children’s book author and illustrator Jan Brett has never seen a creature or furry thing she didn’t want to draw.

That’s why she wouldn’t go near the poem “The Night Before Christmas,’’ even though she loved it. “I couldn’t illustrate it,’’ said Brett. “What do you draw? Not a creature was stirring, not even a mouse.’’

Still, the famous poem tugged at Brett, known for “The Mitten’’ and more than 35 other elaborately illustrated children’s books rich in anthropomorphized animals, trolls, and other creatures, most notably her signature hedgehog named “Hedgie,’’ who shows up in every book.

Then, one morning she woke up with a brainstorm.

“I realized nothing was happening inside the house, but maybe something could be happening outside the house,’’ Brett said. “The elves could run away and sneak onto the sleigh. That’s what I would do if I had to make toys all day.’’

Brett’s interpretation of “The Night Before Christmas,’’ was first published in 1998, featuring a pair of stowaway elves who have a field day with the toys while Santa’s back is turned. But though Brett is a traditionalist when she comes to children’s books, the multimedia universe beckoned. It was time, she said, to expand her “creative universe.’’

Brett, 61, happens to be married to Boston Symphony Orchestra bass player Joe Hearne who is also her savvy business manager and the mastermind behind what BSO managing director Mark Volpe refers to as Brett’s “book empire.’’ (This includes more than 37 million books in print; a tour bus wrapped in artwork from her books; and a prodigious website with hundreds of activities.)

In 2010, Hearne got the idea of projecting Brett’s artwork over the stage in the Holiday Pops performances of “The Night Before Christmas.’’ This year, he asked the BSO to consider a book and DVD project with Brett’s publisher, Penguin Young Readers Group. As they talked, the idea kept getting bolder. The Boston Pops would play the original music. The book would feature Brett’s illustrations as well as an interview and studio tour. Jim Dale, who recorded the Harry Potter audio books, would be the narrator. Then came the animated interactive app for iPads and iPhones. Thus, “Jan Brett’s The Night Before Christmas Deluxe Book & DVD edition’’ was born.

The Boston Pops will perform “The Night Before Christmas’’ at all their holiday concerts, from Dec. 7-24.

“Joe is a different bass player, and he’s one of those guys whose mind never stops,’’ Volpe said. “He’s a very astute business guy. They’re a Mom-and-Pop shop. I can’t imagine one without the other.’’

Hearne met his match in Brett, who seems like the last person who’d need to expand her creative universe. You see this the moment you get to their door, which is covered with forged iron silhouettes of some of her animal characters, including, of course, a hedgehog.

Then there’s the chicken barn with the Polish rooster weather vane on it (Brett collects, breeds, and shows exotic chickens), and the turtle pond she recently built to help her with research for the book she’s working on about a turtle named Mossy who grows a garden on her back.

“Let’s start here,’’ said Hearne, who answered the door and jumped right into a tour. The main hallway has a vaulted ceiling hand-painted with leaves; the hallway is covered with framed illustrations from each of her books. Since there are so many books, the illustrations turns corners and climb the stairs. Among them are pictures from “Berlioz the Bear’’ featuring a bass-playing bear thinly disguised as her husband.

The house has the feel of one of Brett’s books, which have intricate, decorative borders at the edges of the pages. There are a lot of decorative surprises at the margins of her home, too - her collection of colorful Majolica pottery, chandelier made of antlers, dining room chairs with needlepoint seats she created and made to reflect the designs of their Majolica pottery.

The walls of their bedroom are covered with more of Brett’s illustrations. These are unpublished and each one commemorates the most memorable and happy event of the year, which they select together.

“We are mostly hedgehogs,’’ said Hearne. “But sometimes we become bears or moose.’’

The pictures chronicle a charmed and adventurous life, with a lot of exotic travel so Brett can do research for her books. There’s Brett and Hearne as hedgehogs in New Orleans; in the Canadian Arctic; in Taos and Qatar; on tour with the BSO in South America; flying with the Blue Angels.

Married 31 years, they met while flying: Hearne taught flying for 20 years. They have three children from previous marriages.

“Together we have chickens,’’ said Hearne.

Brett’s chickens are probably the most extreme example of how her books sometimes lead to obsessions. It started with a book with a chicken character, and led to a fascination with exotic chickens. She has two chicken barns and a chicken pen in Norwell, and more at their summer home in the Berkshires where they live during the Tanglewood season. (In what could easily be a Brett illustration, they shuttle the birds back and forth in Hearne’s Jaguar.)

She keeps hens, mandarin ducks, and a flock of exotic black Polish bantams with what appear to be fluffy white snowballs on their heads. Their pens have piped-in classical music and a duck pond.

“You saw her chicken coop? My husband says if it had cable he could live there,’’ said Brett’s younger sister Jeannie, who lives in Maine and also illustrates children’s books, including “L Is for Lobster’’ and most recently “My Cat, Coon Cat.’’

“It was normal for us to have a lot of pets,’’ she said. Even though they lived on Main Street in Hingham, their menagerie included horses, chickens, a donkey, rabbits, and guinea pigs. Jan Brett, however, as taken it to a different level.

“She is very well-respected in the field of chickens,’’ said Jeannie. “I gotta hand it to her - when she starts a hobby she really gets into it. I don’t know the lingo, but she produces super-duper chickens.’’

Jan Brett has been seriously breeding chickens for 10 years. She advertises her “Folkloric Bantams’’ in Poultry Press, to which she subscribes, and competes in shows. She’d just returned from the Crossroads of America poultry competition in Indiana, with 14 of her Bantams and Hearne.

“They did pretty well. I was hoping for better; I got a Number Two pullet,’’ said Brett, sounding disappointed. “It would be nice if they thought I was one of the top breeders.’’

No matter. She keeps busy enough. Brett will be the narrator when the Boston Pops perform “The Night Before Christmas’’ on Dec. 7. She and Hearne are planning two trips for future books - birding in India in February, and Sweden and Russia in June. And she runs marathons.

“I get ideas running,’’ she said.

“We’re often asked, ‘How do you people do all this?’’’ said Hearne. “The answer is we don’t watch television. And we’re not very social.’’
