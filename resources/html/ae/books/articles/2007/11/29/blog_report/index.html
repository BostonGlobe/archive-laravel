Blog report

Globe writers weigh in on arts, entertainment, and life around town.

Email|Print| Text size – +
November 29, 2007

DISHING

Slaw for all

We brought dinner to my sister-in-law's house and I never had time to make coleslaw, which I had intended to do.

So Sunday night, I pulled out my little hand-held razor sharp Feemster's slicer (I love this little tool), and sliced red and green cabbages, then shredded carrots on another funky instrument, a Chinese grater, and put together a vat of slaw.

To my mind, slaw goes with everything except caviar and foie gras. And I don't take portions like the size they give you in seafood houses. I heap it on the plate and go back for more. When the big brimming bowl was done, I realized it serves many more than six, which is what I wrote on the recipe.

"If you're in the crowd, it may serve five others," my husband said.

[Sheryl Julian] [boston.com/lifestyle/food/dishing]

EXHIBITIONIST

I'm Not There

I'm not sure if I'd recommend "I'm Not There" to anybody who isn't into Dylan. It's beautifully filmed, and features a stunning performance by Cate Blanchett. If you don't believe in Bob, though, it might feel a bit, well, too precious.

But it's hard to imagine Todd Haynes putting together a better soundtrack.

Two discs with serious props given to Stephen Malkmus ("Ballad of a Thin Man" and "Can't Leave Her Behind"), John Doe ("I Dreamed I Saw St. Augustine" and "Pressing On") and a few older singers (Roger McGuinn, Richie Havens) who might not do much for the boys in marketing, but who understand how to do Dylan.

My Morning Jacket's Jim James even gets screen time, his eyes closed and face painted white for his majestic performance of "Goin' To Acapulco."

[Geoff Edgers] [boston.com/ae/theater_arts/exhibitionist]

GO TO IT

Extreme Bingo

The fourth Monday of every month is "Extreme Bingo" night at the Milky Way Lounge in JP.

DJs Kristen Snow & Elizabeth Grant spin indie, punk, dance, riotgrrl, new wave, and other random goodness during the games. There's no cover, and you can win prizes.

I've never witnessed Extreme Bingo before, but it sounds pretty cool. I'd like to check it out.

[Emily Sweeney] [boston.com/ae/sidekick/blog]

MOVIE NATION

Pass the stuffing

Everyone got something to eat at the movies over the Thanksgiving holidays, with five new studio releases combining for a healthy upturn in overall box office grosses. Seriously; you could just about hear the Hollywood execs groaning from the turkey-related tryptomine overdose on the living room couch.

Disney's "Enchanted" was the runaway winner, with $35 million over the weekend and a $50 million five-day haul. A star got born, too: Amy Adams now sits atop the Internet Movie Database Starmeter.

But "Enchanted" was at 3,700 movie theaters and thus unavoidable; good word of mouth and positive reviews wrapped it up. More interesting is the appearance of buppie holiday comedy-drama "This Christmas" in the No. 2 spot, with $18.6 million over the weekend and $27 million for the five-day span. At 1,858 theaters, that translates to a $14.5K per-theater-average - higher than "Enchanted."

Actioner "Hitman" ($21 million), goopy family drama "August Rush" ($13 million), and Stephen King-derived horror film "The Mist" ($13 million) all played well to their respective post-prandial niches. In other words, in the weekend's feast they were the equivalent of the cranberry sauce, squash, and that peas-and-pearl onions dish your aunt makes that nobody eats. Except that this time someone ate it.

For further numbers, check out the Box Office Mojo chart and Leonard Klady at Movie City News.

[Ty Burr] [boston.com/ae/movies/blog]

OFF THE SHELF

A writing center grows in Roxbury

826 Boston, a youth writing center grounded in Dave Eggers's sense of whimsy, is hosting a housewarming and community fair Saturday (Dec. 1) from 2 to 5 p.m. in Egleston Square, Roxbury.

It will be an afternoon of performances, refreshments, and an introduction to 826's free writing programs for students ages 6-18. There will be writing workshops for youths from 1 to 2 p.m., followed by a special guest appearance and ribbon-cutting at 826's new home at 3035 Washington St.

826 Boston is the seventh nonprofit writing center Eggers has opened across the country. (The address of the first center was 826 Valencia in San Francisco.) Kevin Feeney, who frequented 826 Valencia as a high school student, now attends Harvard and was instrumental in the development of the Boston center.

For more information, call 617-442-5400. (The center is less than a 10-minute walk from Jackson Square or Stony Brook MBTA stations on the Orange Line. Free parking is available on the street and in the lot across from the 826 entrance on Washington St.)

[Jan Gardner] [boston.com/ae/books/blog]

SOUND EFFECTS

Jackson 5, Michael Included, Plotting Tour

Jermaine Jackson told BBC 6 Music that he and his brothers are hoping to hit concert stages as the Jackson 5 in 2008. Answering the obvious follow-up question, Jermaine said "He was at the meetings. Michael will be involved." The last time the group toured, as the Jacksons, was in support of 1984's "Victory." Michael, who had already released "Thriller," announced his departure on the last night of that tour. Nearly a quarter century later, after years of musical and personal isolation and a very public child abuse trial, who can blame Michael for returning to the warm, fuzzy embrace of his family? Sound Effects just hopes they don't let Papa Joe into rehearsals. The brothers are also laying down some studio tracks, and Jermaine reports that a new album may be in the works.

[Joan Anderman] [boston.com/ae/music/blog]

STYLEPHILE

Black Friday tragedy!

I made it quite clear last year that I'd rather wear a pair of Kleenex boxes filled with red ants on my feet than Crocs, those hideous foam rubber shoes that are the preferred footwear of children and adults who are too lazy to lace their shoes.

Now it seems that the company I despise more than Robin Williams is opening a store in Boston on Friday. I think a demonstration is in order! Who wants to join me in a fashion picket line? They're going to be selling the clown shoes, and Crocs apparel. I can only imagine what that looks like.

So, if you have anyone named Bozo, Clarabelle, or Krusty on your shopping list, your Black Friday just got much easier. That is if you can get past me in the picket line. Good luck!

[Christopher Muther] [boston.com/lifestyle/fashion/stylephile]

VIEWER DISCRETION

Twist and shout

Lessons learned from the final showdown on "Dancing With the Stars:"

1) If you are older than, I don't know, 24, it might be best not to dress like a wind-up doll. (I love Marie. I do. But still.)

2) If a judge really gives you a dressing-down, he might give you a . . . 7. Ouch. That's way off the gymnastics scale of score inflation.

3) You might be able to get Posh Spice to come watch you dance, but don't expect her to crack a smile.

4) You can be the crowd favorite, even without a history of singing and dancing (or booty-shaking, at least) in front of big audiences. Mel B. might have a higher score, but Helio: Wow. If the ladies of America don't vote him into victory, I'll be fairly stunned.

5) If you watch "Dancing With the Stars" with your 3-year-old, she might have trouble going to bed, because she's too ramped up from practicing her dance moves. As in: "Did they do this?" (Shimmies, flaps arms like a bird.) "Did they do this?" (Twists waist back and forth.) "Did they do this?" (Bends over, puts head on floor.)

[Joanna Weiss] [boston.com/ae/tv/blog]
