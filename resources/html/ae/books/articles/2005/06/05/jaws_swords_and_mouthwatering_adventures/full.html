ON AUDIO

Jaws, swords, and mouthwatering adventures

By Rochelle O'Gorman  |  June 5, 2005

You are sitting on the beach, sipping a ghastly sugar-free, flavor-free lemonade, munching the rest of your son's now soggy peanut butter and jelly sandwich. Luckily, you can't taste it -- you don't need to, because Ruth Reichl is distilling an exquisite Japanese meal into your ears, describing delicate flavors in such detail that this could be food porn.

Reichl may have outdone herself with ''Garlic and Sapphires: The Secret Life of a Critic in Disguise" (Random House Audio, abridged nonfiction, four CDs, five hours, $27.50, read by the author; also available as a download from www.audible.com, $19.25 abridged, $24.47 unabridged at 10 hours and 57 minutes, narrated by Bernadette Dunne). This former Los Angeles and New York Times restaurant critic takes us along from her job interview at the Gray Lady to the day she left, and includes the disguises she donned along the way to trick restaurateurs into treating her like every other diner.

The CD version contains a five-recipe card, culled from the many in the print version. Reichl reads the abridged audio with candor and crackle, but the longer, more delectable, unabridged version contains so many more of her columns and anecdotes that you may not be able to help yourself from inhaling the audio and then savoring the printed book.

This appears to be the summer of the memoir. Reading their autobiographies are Gene Wilder (''Kiss Me Like a Stranger: My Search for Love and Art," unabridged by Audio Renaissance, $29.95, and through Audible.com, $20.97) and Brooke Shields (''Down Came the Rain: My Journey Through Postpartum Depression," abridged by Hyperion Audiobooks, $31.98, and through Audible.com, $22.39). Lauren Bacall updates her life story in ''By Myself and Then Some" (abridged by Harper Audio, $22.95), and Matthew McGough details a dream come true in ''Bat Boy: My True Life Adventures Coming of Age With the New York Yankees" (abridged by Random House Audio, $27.50, and through Audible.com, $19.25, plus an unabridged version on Audible.com, read by Jason Harris, $24.47). The latest biography of Ol' Blue Eyes, written by Anthony Summers and Robbyn Swan (''Sinatra: The Life," abridged by Random House Audio, $34.95, and through Audible.com, $24.47, plus an unabridged version on Audible.com, read by Scott Brick, $34.97), is read by actor Tony Roberts.

One surprisingly good listen comes from actress and activist Jane Fonda in ''My Life So Far" (Random House Audio, abridged nonfiction, eight CDs, 10 hours, $34.95; also available as an abridged download from Audible.com, $24.47). Surprising because she is unabashedly candid about her more controversial acts. Fonda gives one of the best author readings this audiophile has ever heard.

Remember the summer of '75, when most of the country could manage no more than a splash in the ocean, thanks to ''Jaws"? In this summer of '05, we have ''The Devil's Teeth: A True Story of Survival and Obsession Among America's Great White Sharks," by Susan Casey (Random House Audio, abridged nonfiction, five CDs, 5 hours and 30 minutes, $26.95, read by the author; also available as a download from Audible.com, $18.87 abridged; $24.47 unabridged at 10 hours and 57 minutes, narrated by Kimberly Farr).

A combination of memoir and natural history, this book describes the shark population just off the California coast, at the Farallon Islands. Casey reveals another world, one that is raw and wild, and one that can actually be seen on the included ''enhanced CD." You can listen to one disc and put another in your computer to view photos of the places you just heard about.

There is a ton of new fiction out there this summer, but slicing through the competition is ''Zorro," by Isabel Allende (Harper Audio, unabridged fiction, 13 CDs, 15 hours, $39.95, read by Blair Brown). Allende sets a fictional character in a world that seems eerily real, infusing this Robin Hood-like character with emotions and reactions that ring very true. This lively adventure is read, as usual, with verve and confidence by audiobook veteran Brown.

The ladies of literature have contributed quite the list this year for summer listening, what with Sue Miller's ''Lost in the Forest" (unabridged by Random House Audio, $34.95, and through Audible.com, $24.47), Elizabeth Berg's ''The Year of Pleasures" (unabridged by Brilliance Audio, $29.95), Dorothea Benton Frank's latest, ''Pawleys Island: A Lowcountry Tale" (unabridged by Brilliance Audio, $36.95), and Alice Hoffman's magical tale of getting what you wish for, ''The Ice Queen" (unabridged by Time Warner Audio, $31.98, and through Audible.com, $18.89).

Lighter listening can be had with Rebecca Wells's ''Ya-Yas in Bloom" (unabridged by Harper Audio, $39.85); thrills from Lisa Scottoline's ''Devil's Corner" (unabridged by Harper Audio, $39.95); and chills from Laurell K. Hamilton's ''A Stroke of Midnight" (unabridged by Brilliance Audio, $38.95).

Lawrence Block's latest Matthew Scudder novel, ''All the Flowers Are Dying," is now available (Harper Audio, $20.95, and through Audible.com, $20.97). Another character happily welcomed back is LA Police Detective Harry Bosch, who investigates the 1988 death of a 16-year-old girl in Michael Connelly's ''The Closers" (unabridged by TimeWarner AudioBooks, $46.98, and through Audible.com, $32.89). And a historical mystery is unveiled in Iain Pears's short novel ''The Portrait" (unabridged by Penguin Audio, $25.95, and through Audible.com, $18.17).

If you need something diverting while driving the kids to that overpriced, overcrowded amusement park, pop J. K. Rowling's latest, ''Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince" (unabridged by Listening Library, $75 for CDs and $50 for cassettes), into the CD player. Nimble-throated Jim Dale is narrating this unabridged production, available on July 16. Dale reads another unabridged Listening Library title with great gusto, ''Around the World in 80 Days," by Jules Verne (unabridged, $29.95, and through Audible.com, $20.97), enhanced by music from around the world.

Rochelle O'Gorman is publisher and editor in chief of audiobookcafe.com.
