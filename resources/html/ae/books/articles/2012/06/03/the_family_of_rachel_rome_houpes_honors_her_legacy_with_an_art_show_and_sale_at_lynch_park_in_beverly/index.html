HER ART LIVES ON: Friends and family of the late Rachel Rome Houpes are gathering Saturday at the Carriage House in Beverly’s Lynch Park for the opening of a memorial show and sale of her artwork.

All proceeds benefit art programs for the disabled offered at Northeast ARC in Danvers.

Her husband, John Houpes, and sons Aaron and Jacob Rome are hosting “Rachel Rome Houpes: A Celebration of Art” for her many longtime friends and the wider community.

For more than 40 years, Houpes created and taught art in Beverly. She was a mainstay at Hadassah; pivotal in the Beverly Guild of Artists; and a cofounder of the PTO.

A lifelong learner, she earned a master’s degree in education in the late 1980s. She also was a regular columnist and contributor to several local newspapers.

Houpes was a world traveler from Alaska to the Galapagos, from Europe to Nepal and Hong Kong, always writing and often painting and sketching along the way.

She married John Houpes in 1988, and his encouragement helped her pursue her art with renewed passion. Frequent winters in San Miguel de Allende, Mexico, soon followed, and the trips inspired her to new heights of creativity.

The subjects of her works range from oil paintings of classic New England scenes to big, bold acrylics capturing San Miguel. In addition to landscapes and vibrant street scenes, Houpes created mixed-media abstracts using glitter, charcoal, watercolors, and anything else within reach.

The show and sale includes more than five dozen original paintings, drawings, and sketches and a limited selection of prints. A commemorative book also is available for purchase.

“Rachel obviously lived like she painted and painted like she lived: with passion, curiosity, adventure, and love,” said her son Jake. “When you get to know her art, when you experience the boldness and the energy of it, you will then know her as well. Among Rachel’s many legacies is her art, and it is the hope of her family that it continues to be enjoyed by others for many years to come.”

A resident of Beverly for 41 years, Houpes died in March 2011 at age 69.

The show is open noon to 9 p.m. Saturday and next Sunday, and 4 to 8 p.m. June 11-15. The opening reception runs 5 to 9 p.m. Saturday.

Visit www.rachelromehoupesart.com.

GREAT WHITE WAY: The Polymnia Choral Society presents “A Salute to the Great White Way” Saturday at Memorial Hall in Melrose.

Devin McCall, a senior at Melrose High School and winner of Polymnia’s “Spotlight on High School Talent” contest, sings with the chorus. McCall has appeared in many productions, most recently in his school’s production of “Legally Blonde: The Musical.”

Local performer Leigh Barrett is guest soloist and the a cappella men’s group Blue of a Kind is featured.

“We’re doing a very diverse and fun group of songs,” said music director Murray Kidd. “We will be joined by a band of amazing jazz professionals, and they are going to take our music even higher in energy.”

The concert begins at 7:30 p.m. Tickets are $25, $15 for seniors and students, and $30 for table seats. Visit www.polymnia.org.

AUTHOR’S CORNER:Yana Barton reads from her book, “SuperDad,” at 10 a.m. Friday at the Andover Bookstore. The picture book for ages 1 to 8 is about a regular father who is SuperDad to his daughters and son. It follows the family’s adventures through the seasons, from building sandcastles, to roasting marshmallows, apple picking, and skiing.

Barton, of Andover, is a mother of three. All profits from the sale of “SuperDad” are being donated to charities supporting children’s causes.

IN LOCAL GALLERIES:“Beyond the Sea,” an exhibition of photographs by Bobby Baker, is at the Marblehead Arts Association through June 24. A reception with the artist is 2 to 4 p.m. next Sunday. “My work reflects places my heart and mind seek out and feel happiest at, and that always seems to lead to the Atlantic seacoast,” said Baker, of Brentwood, N.H. “This exhibition of my work stays true to this, my love of seaside life.” The photographs were shot on Cape Cod, Plum Island, and coastal California. . .  Works on paper by the late William A. Millett are on exhibit at the Parish Center for the Arts in Westford throughout June. The show includes watercolors and pastels of landscapes, interiors, and portraits. Millett was a prolific painter who worked into his 80s. He died in 2007 at age 85. Millett and his family were longtime residents of Westford and many of the scenes in his works will be familiar to members of the community. Receptions are 4:30 to 8 p.m. Friday, and 1 to 4 p.m. next Sunday.
